ladder 
ˈladər

▶noun
1 a structure consisting of a series of bars or steps between two upright lengths of wood, metal, or rope, used for climbing up or down something. 
• a series of ascending stages by which someone or something may advance or progress: employees on their way up the career ladder.
2 British (in tights or stockings) a run. 
▶verb British (with reference to tights or stockings) develop or cause to develop a run. ORIGIN Old English hlǣd(d)er, of West Germanic origin; related to Dutch leer and German Leiter .

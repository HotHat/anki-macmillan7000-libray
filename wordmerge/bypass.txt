bypass 
BrE ˈbʌɪpɑːs, AmE ˈbaɪˌpæs 
A. noun 
① (road) 旁路 pánglù 
② Medicine 心脏搭桥手术 xīnzàng dāqiáo shǒushù 
B.  transitive verb 
① (avoid) 为…加设旁路 wèi… jiā shè pánglù 
② (not consult) 越过 yuèguo he bypassed his supervisor and gave the report to the manager 他越过主管直接把报告给了经理 
③ (circumvent) 避开 bìkāi the security measures could be easily bypassed 安全措施很容易被钻空子 
④ (travel round) 绕过 ràoguo they bypassed the hotel and headed for the nearby bar 他们绕过旅馆去了附近的酒吧 
⑤ Medicine 用旁通管取代 yòng pángtōngguǎn qǔdài surgery to bypass an artery 用旁通管取代动脉的手术 
bypass 
▶noun follow the signs for the bypass: detour, alternate route, alternative route, diversion, shortcut.
▶verb
1 bypass the farm: go around, go past, make a detour around; avoid.
2 an attempt to bypass the problem: avoid, evade, dodge, escape, elude, circumvent, get around, shortcut around, skirt, sidestep, steer clear of; informal duck.
3 they bypassed the regulations: ignore, pass over, neglect, go over the head of; informal short-circuit.

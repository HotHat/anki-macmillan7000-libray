pastry 
ˈpāstrē

▶noun (plural pastries) a dough of flour, shortening, and water, used as a base and covering in baked dishes such as pies. 
• an item of food consisting of sweet pastry with a cream, jam, or fruit filling. ORIGIN late Middle English (as a collective term): from paste, influenced by Old French pastaierie .

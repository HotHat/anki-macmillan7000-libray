religion 
rəˈlijən

▶noun the belief in and worship of a superhuman controlling power, especially a personal God or gods: ideas about the relationship between science and religion. 
• a particular system of faith and worship: the world's great religions. 
• a pursuit or interest to which someone ascribes supreme importance: consumerism is the new religion. 
PHRASES
get religion informal be converted to religious belief and practices.DERIVATIVES religionless adjectiveORIGIN Middle English (originally in the sense ‘life under monastic vows’): from Old French, or from Latin religio(n-) ‘obligation, bond, reverence,’ perhaps based on Latin religare ‘to bind.’

wool 
wo͝ol

▶noun
1 the fine soft curly or wavy hair forming the coat of a sheep, goat, or similar animal, especially when shorn and prepared for use in making cloth or yarn. 
• yarn or textile fiber made from wool: carpets made of 80 percent wool and
20 percent nylon 
a sampler in colored wools 
[as modifier] : her blue wool suit.
2 a thing resembling wool in form or texture, in particular: 
• [with modifier] the soft underfur or down of some other mammals: beaver wool. 
• [with modifier] a metal or mineral made into a mass of fine fibers: lead wool.

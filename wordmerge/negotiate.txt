negotiate 
BrE nɪˈɡəʊʃɪeɪt, AmE nəˈɡoʊʃiˌeɪt 

A.  intransitive verb 谈判 tánpàn to negotiate about or over [something]; 协商某事 to negotiate for [something]; 谈判谋求某物 to negotiate with [somebody]; 与某人谈判 
B.  transitive verb 
① (discuss) 商定 shāngdìng ‹sale, loan, salary›; 谈成 tánchéng ‹agreement›
② (manoeuvre around) 顺利通过 shùnlì tōngguò ‹bend, narrow street›; 成功越过 chénggōng yuèguo ‹obstacle, hill›
③ (deal with) 解决 jiějué ‹problem, difficulty›; 通过 tōngguò ‹interview, test›
④ Finance 转让 zhuǎnràng ‹cheque, bond, securities›
negotiate 
nəˈɡōSHēˌāt

▶verb
1 [with object] obtain or bring about by discussion: he negotiated a new contract with the sellers. 
• [no object] try to reach an agreement or compromise by discussion with others: his government's willingness to negotiate.
2 [with object] find a way over or through (an obstacle or difficult path): there was a puddle to be negotiated.
3 [with object] transfer (a check, bill, or other document) to the legal ownership of another person. 
• convert (a check) into cash. DERIVATIVES negotiant 
-SH(ē)ənt 
noun ( archaic)ORIGIN early 17th century: from Latin negotiat- ‘done in the course of business,’ from the verb negotiari, from negotium ‘business,’ from neg- ‘not’ + otium ‘leisure.’

rectangle 
ˈrekˌtaNGɡəl

▶noun a plane figure with four straight sides and four right angles, especially one with unequal adjacent sides, in contrast to a square. ORIGIN late 16th century: from medieval Latin rectangulum, from late Latin rectiangulum, based on Latin rectus ‘straight’ + angulus ‘an angle.’

vicious 
ˈviSHəs

▶adjective
1 deliberately cruel or violent: a vicious assault. 
• (of an animal) wild and dangerous to people. 
• serious or dangerous: a vicious flu bug.
2 literary immoral: every soul on earth, virtuous or vicious, shall perish.
3 archaic (of language or a line of reasoning) imperfect; defective. ORIGIN Middle English (in the sense ‘characterized by immorality’): from Old French vicious or Latin vitiosus, from vitium ‘vice.’

authentic 
ôˈTHen(t)ik 
(abbreviation auth.) 
▶adjective
1 of undisputed origin; genuine: the letter is now accepted as an authentic document 
authentic 14th-century furniture. 
• made or done in the traditional or original way, or in a way that faithfully resembles an original: the restaurant serves authentic Italian meals 
every detail of the movie was totally authentic. 
• based on facts; accurate or reliable: an authentic depiction of the situation. 
• (in existentialist philosophy) relating to or denoting an emotionally appropriate, significant, purposive, and responsible mode of human life.
2 Music (of a church mode) comprising the notes lying between the principal note or final and the note an octave higher. Compare with plagal. ORIGIN late Middle English: via Old French from late Latin authenticus, from Greek authentikos ‘principal, genuine.’

endure 
inˈd(y)o͝or enˈd(y)o͝or

▶verb
1 [with object] suffer (something painful or difficult) patiently: it seemed impossible that anyone could endure such pain.
2 [no object] remain in existence; last: these cities have endured through time. DERIVATIVES endurer nounORIGIN Middle English: from Old French endurer, from Latin indurare ‘harden,’ from in- ‘in’ + durus ‘hard.’

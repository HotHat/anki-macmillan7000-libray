canteen 
kanˈtēn

▶noun
1 a restaurant provided by an organization such as a military camp, college, factory, or company for its soldiers, students, staff, etc.
2 a small water bottle, as used by soldiers or campers. ORIGIN mid 18th century (originally denoting a type of shop in a barracks or garrison town): from French cantine, from Italian cantina ‘cellar.’

emphasis 
ˈemfəsəs

▶noun (plural emphases 
-ˌsēz
) special importance, value, or prominence given to something: they placed great emphasis on the individual's freedom 
different emphases and viewpoints. 
• stress laid on a word or words to indicate special meaning or particular importance. 
• vigor or intensity of expression: he spoke with emphasis and with complete conviction. ORIGIN late 16th century: via Latin from Greek, originally ‘appearance, show,’ later denoting a figure of speech in which more is implied than is said (the original sense in English), from emphainein ‘exhibit,’ from em- ‘in, within’ + phainein ‘to show.’

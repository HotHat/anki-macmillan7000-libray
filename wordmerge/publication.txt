publication 
ˌpəbləˈkāSH(ə)n

▶noun the preparation and issuing of a book, journal, piece of music, or other work for public sale: the publication of her first novel. 
• a book, journal, etc. issued for public sale: scientific publications. 
• the action of making something generally known: the publication of April trade figures. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘public announcement or declaration’): via Old French from Latin publicatio(n-), from publicare ‘make public’ (see publish) .

niece 
BrE niːs, AmE nis 
noun (daughter of brother) 侄女 zhínǚ ; (daughter of sister) 外甥女 wàishengnǚ 
niece 
nēs

▶noun a daughter of one's brother or sister, or of one's brother-in-law or sister-in-law. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French, based on Latin neptis ‘granddaughter,’ feminine of nepos ‘nephew, grandson’ (see nephew), from an Indo-European root shared by Dutch nicht,German Nichte .

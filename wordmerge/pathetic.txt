pathetic 
pəˈTHedik

▶adjective
1 arousing pity, especially through vulnerability or sadness: she looked so pathetic that I bent down to comfort her. 
• informal miserably inadequate: his test scores in Chemistry were pathetic.
2 archaic relating to the emotions. ORIGIN late 16th century (in the sense ‘affecting the emotions’): via late Latin from Greek pathētikos ‘sensitive,’ based on pathos ‘suffering.’

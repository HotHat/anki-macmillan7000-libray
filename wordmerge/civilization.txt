civilization 
ˌsivələˈzāSH(ə)n

▶noun the stage of human social development and organization that is considered most advanced: they equated the railroad with progress and civilization. 
• the process by which a society or place reaches an advanced stage of social development and organization. 
• the society, culture, and way of life of a particular area: the great books of Western civilization 
the early civilizations of Mesopotamia and Egypt. 
• the comfort and convenience of modern life, regarded as available only in towns and cities: the fur traders moved further and further from civilization.

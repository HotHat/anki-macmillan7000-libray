consolidate 
kənˈsäləˌdāt

▶verb [with object]
1 make (something) physically stronger or more solid: the first phase of the project is to consolidate the outside walls. 
• reinforce or strengthen (one's position or power): the company consolidated its position in the international market.
2 combine (a number of things) into a single more effective or coherent whole: all manufacturing activities have been consolidated in new premises. 
• combine (a number of financial accounts or funds) into a single overall account or set of accounts. 
• combine (two or more legal actions involving similar questions) into one for action by a court. DERIVATIVES consolidator 
kənˈsälədādər 
nounORIGIN early 16th century (in the sense ‘combine into a single whole’): from Latin consolidare, from con- ‘together’ + solidare ‘make firm’ (from solidus ‘solid’).

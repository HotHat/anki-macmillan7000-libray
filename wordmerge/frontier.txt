frontier 
ˌfrənˈtir

▶noun a line or border separating two countries. 
• the extreme limit of settled land beyond which lies wilderness, especially referring to the western US before Pacific settlement: his novel of the American frontier. 
• the extreme limit of understanding or achievement in a particular area: the success of science in extending the frontiers of knowledge. DERIVATIVES frontierless adjectiveORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French frontiere, based on Latin frons, front- ‘front.’

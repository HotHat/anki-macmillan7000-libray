depot 
ˈdēpō ˈdepō

▶noun a place for the storage of large quantities of equipment, food, or some other commodity: an arms depot. 
• North American a railroad or bus station. 
• a place where buses, trains, or other vehicles are housed and maintained and from which they are dispatched for service. 
• the headquarters of a regiment; a place where recruits or other troops are assembled. ORIGIN late 18th century (in the sense ‘act of depositing’): from French dépôt, from Latin depositum ‘something deposited’ (see deposit) .

violently 
BrE ˈvʌɪələntli, AmE ˈvaɪ(ə)ləntli 
adverb 
① (with physical violence) 猛烈地 měngliè de ‹attack, hit, push›; 通过暴力 tōngguò bàolì ‹demonstrate, struggle›; 残忍地 cánrěn de ‹kill›he was violently kicked 他被狠狠地踢了一脚 to die violently 遭暴力致死 
② (with great force) 激烈地 jīliè de ‹argue, criticize, erupt, rage›; 强烈地 qiángliè de ‹disagree, affect, feel›; 剧烈地 jùliè de ‹explode, blow›
③ (suddenly) 突然 tūrán ‹change, swing, swerve›; 猛然 měngrán ‹accelerate, brake›
④ (exceedingly) 极度地 jídù de to be violently opposed to [something] 竭力反对某事 to be violently ill or sick BrE 病得很厉害 
⑤ (harshly, garishly) 刺眼地 cìyǎn de ‹coloured›

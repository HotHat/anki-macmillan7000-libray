fiction 
ˈfikSH(ə)n

▶noun literature in the form of prose, especially short stories and novels, that describes imaginary events and people. 
• invention or fabrication as opposed to fact: he dismissed the allegation as absolute fiction. 
• [in singular] a belief or statement that is false, but that is often held to be true because it is expedient to do so: the notion of that country being a democracy is a polite fiction. DERIVATIVES fictionist 
ˈfikSH(ə)nəst 
nounORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘invented statement’): via Old French from Latin fictio(n-), from fingere ‘form, contrive.’ Compare with feign and figment .

sausage 
ˈsôsij

▶noun a cylindrical length of minced and seasoned pork, beef, or other meat encased in a skin, typically sold raw to be grilled, boiled, or fried before eating. 
• minced and seasoned meat that has been encased in a skin and cooked or preserved, sold mainly to be eaten cold in slices: smoked German sausage. 
• [usually as modifier] used in references to the characteristic cylindrical shape of sausages: mold into a sausage shape. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old Northern French saussiche, from medieval Latin salsicia, from Latin salsus ‘salted’ (see sauce) .

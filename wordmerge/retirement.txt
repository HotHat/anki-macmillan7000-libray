retirement 
rəˈtī(ə)rmənt

▶noun
1 the action or fact of leaving one's job and ceasing to work: a man nearing retirement 
the library has seen a large number of retirements this year. 
• the period of one's life after leaving one's job and ceasing to work: he spent much of his retirement traveling in Europe. 
• the action or fact of ceasing to play a sport competitively.
2 the withdrawal of a jury from the courtroom to decide their verdict. 
• the period of time during which a jury decides their verdict: a three-hour retirement.
3 seclusion: he lived in retirement in Miami. 
• archaic a secluded or private place: Vermont, where he has a sweet country retirement.

submission 
səbˈmiSHən

▶noun
1 the action or fact of accepting or yielding to a superior force or to the will or authority of another person: they were forced into submission. 
• Wrestling an act of surrendering to a hold by one's opponent. 
• archaic humility; meekness: servile flattery and submission.
2 the action of presenting a proposal, application, or other document for consideration or judgment: reports should be prepared for submission at partners' meetings. 
• a proposal, application, or other document presented in this way. 
• Law a proposition or argument presented by a lawyer to a judge or jury. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French, or from Latin submissio(n-), from the verb submittere (see submit) .

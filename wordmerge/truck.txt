truck
1 
trək

▶noun
1 a large, heavy motor vehicle used for transporting goods, materials, or troops. 
• British a railroad vehicle for carrying freight, especially a small open one. 
• a low flat-topped cart used for moving heavy items.
2 an undercarriage with four to six wheels pivoted beneath the end of a railroad car. 
• each of two axle units on a skateboard, to which the wheels are attached.
3 a wooden disk at the top of a ship's mast or flagstaff, with sheaves for signal halyards. 
▶verb [with object] chiefly North American convey by truck: the food was trucked to St. Petersburg 
(as noun trucking) : industries such as trucking. 
• [no object] drive a truck. 
• [no object] informal go or proceed, especially in a casual or leisurely way: he walked confidently behind them and trucked on through! DERIVATIVES truckage

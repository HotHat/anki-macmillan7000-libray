potential 
pəˈten(t)SHəl

▶adjective [attributive] having or showing the capacity to become or develop into something in the future: a two-pronged campaign to woo potential customers. 
▶noun
1 latent qualities or abilities that may be developed and lead to future success or usefulness: a young broadcaster with great potential 
the potentials of the technology were never wholly controllable. 
• (often potential for/to do something) the possibility of something happening or of someone doing something in the future: the crane operator's clear view reduces the potential for accidents 
pesticides with the potential to cause cancer.
2 Physics the quantity determining the energy of mass in a gravitational field or of charge in an electric field. DERIVATIVES potentiality

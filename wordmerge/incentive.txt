incentive 
inˈsen(t)iv

▶noun a thing that motivates or encourages one to do something: there is no incentive for customers to conserve water. 
• a payment or concession to stimulate greater output or investment: tax incentives for investing in depressed areas 
[as modifier] : incentive payments. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin incentivum ‘something that sets the tune or incites,’ from incantare ‘to chant or charm.’

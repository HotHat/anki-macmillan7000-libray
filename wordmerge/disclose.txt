disclose 
disˈklōz

▶verb [with object] make (secret or new information) known: they disclosed her name to the press 
[with clause] : the magazine disclosed that he had served a prison sentence for fraud. 
• allow (something) to be seen, especially by uncovering it: he cleared away the grass and disclosed a narrow opening descending into the darkness. DERIVATIVES disclosable adjective. discloser nounORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French desclos-, stem of desclore, based on Latin claudere ‘to close.’

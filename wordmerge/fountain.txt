fountain 
ˈfount(ə)n

▶noun
1 an ornamental structure in a pool or lake from which one or more jets of water are pumped into the air. 
• short for drinking fountain. 
• a thing that spurts or cascades into the air: little fountains of dust.
2 chiefly literary a natural spring of water. 
• a source of a desirable quality: the government always quotes this report as the fountain of truth. 
▶verb [no object] spurt or cascade like a fountain: an enormous curtain of lava fountained into the sky. DERIVATIVES fountained 
ˈfountnd 
adjective ( literary).ORIGIN Middle English ( sense
2 of the noun): from Old French fontaine, from late Latin fontana, feminine of Latin fontanus, adjective from fons, font- ‘a spring.’

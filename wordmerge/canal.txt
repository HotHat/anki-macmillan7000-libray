canal 
kəˈnal

▶noun an artificial waterway constructed to allow the passage of boats or ships inland or to convey water for irrigation. 
• a tubular duct in a plant or animal, serving to convey or contain food, liquid, or air: the ear canal. 
• Astronomy any of a number of linear markings formerly reported as seen by telescope on the planet Mars. [named canali (‘channels’) by 
G.  
V.  Schiaparelli (1835–1910); the markings are now thought to have arisen from eye or lens defects.] ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French, alteration of chanel ‘channel,’ from Latin canalis ‘pipe, groove, channel,’ from canna ‘cane.’

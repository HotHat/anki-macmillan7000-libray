venue 
ˈvenˌyo͞o

▶noun the place where something happens, especially an organized event such as a concert, conference, or sports event: the river could soon be the venue for a powerboat world championship event. 
• Law the county or district within which a criminal or civil case must be heard. ORIGIN late 16th century (denoting a thrust or bout in fencing; also in the Law sense): from Old French, literally ‘a coming,’ feminine past participle of venir ‘come,’ from Latin venire .

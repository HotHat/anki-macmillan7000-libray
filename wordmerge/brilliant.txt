brilliant 
ˈbrilyənt

▶adjective
1 (of light or color) very bright and radiant.
2 exceptionally clever or talented: a brilliant young mathematician 
a brilliant idea. 
• outstanding; impressive: his brilliant career at Harvard. 
• British informal very good, excellent, or marvelous: we had a brilliant time 
[as exclamation] : “Brilliant!” he declared excitedly as she finished telling him what had happened. 
▶noun a diamond of brilliant cut. ORIGIN late 17th century: from French brillant ‘shining,’ present participle of briller, from Italian brillare, probably from Latin beryllus (see beryl) .

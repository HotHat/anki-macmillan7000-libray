tiger 
ˈtīɡər

▶noun a very large solitary cat with a yellow-brown coat striped with black, native to the forests of Asia but becoming increasingly rare. tiger[Panthera tigris, family Felidae.] 
• used to refer to someone fierce, determined, or ambitious: despite his wound, he still fought like a tiger 
one of the sport's young tigers. 
• (also tiger economy) a dynamic economy of one of the smaller eastern Asian countries, especially that of Singapore, Taiwan, or South Korea. 
PHRASES
have a tiger by the tail have embarked on a course of action that proves unexpectedly difficult but that cannot easily or safely be abandoned.ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French tigre, from Latin tigris, from Greek.

mathematical 
BrE maθ(ə)ˈmatɪk(ə)l, AmE ˌmæθ(ə)ˈmædək(ə)l 
adjective 
① (relating to mathematics) 数学的 shùxué de ‹formula, equation, genius›
② (precise) 精确的 jīngquè de with mathematical precision 极为精确地 
③ (good at mathematics) 擅长数学的 shàncháng shùxué de to have a mathematical mind 有一个数学般精确的头脑 
④ (possible but improbable) 微乎其微的 wēi hū qí wēi de ‹chance›
mathematical 
ˌmaTH(ə)ˈmadək(ə)l 
(also mathematic) 
▶adjective relating to mathematics: mathematical equations.

figurative : he arranged the meal with mathematical precision on a plate. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin mathematicalis, from Greek mathēmatikos, from mathēma, mathēmat- ‘science,’ from the base of manthanein ‘learn.’

succeed 
səkˈsēd

▶verb
1 [no object] achieve the desired aim or result: a mission which could not possibly succeed 
he succeeded in winning a pardon.
2 [with object] take over a throne, inheritance, office, or other position from: he would succeed Hawke as prime minister. 
• [no object] become the new rightful holder of an inheritance, office, title, or property: he succeeded to his father's kingdom. 
• come after and take the place of: her embarrassment was succeeded by fear. 
PHRASES
nothing succeeds like success proverb success leads to opportunities for further and greater successes.DERIVATIVES succeeder noun ( archaic)ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French succeder or Latin succedere ‘come close after,’ from sub- ‘close to’ + cedere ‘go.’

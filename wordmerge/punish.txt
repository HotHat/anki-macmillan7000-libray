punish 
ˈpəniSH

▶verb [with object] inflict a penalty or sanction on (someone) as retribution for an offense, especially a transgression of a legal or moral code: I have done wrong and I'm being punished for it. 
• inflict a penalty or sanction on someone for (such an offense): fraudulent acts would be punished by up to two years in prison. 
• treat (someone) in an unfairly harsh way: a rise in prescription charges would punish the poor. 
• subject (someone or something) to severe and debilitating treatment. DERIVATIVES punisher nounORIGIN Middle English: from Old French puniss-, lengthened stem of punir ‘punish,’ from Latin punire, from poena ‘penalty.’

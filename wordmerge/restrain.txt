restrain

transitive verb

② (physically control) 制止 zhìzhǐ ‹patient›policemen had to restrain the accused 警察不得不强行控制住被告 the dog was restrained with a stout lead 那只狗被一条很结实的皮带拴着 
③ (curb) 抑制 yìzhì ‹feelings, laughter›; 控制 kòngzhì ‹prices, inflation›to restrain oneself 克制自己 
restrain 
▶verb
1 Charles restrained his anger: control, keep under control, check, hold/keep in check, curb, suppress, repress, contain, dampen, subdue, smother, choke back, stifle, bottle up, rein back/in; informal keep the lid on.
2 she could barely restrain herself from swearing: prevent, stop, keep, hold back.
3 that beast ought to be restrained: tie up, bind, tether, chain (up), fetter, shackle, manacle, put in irons; informal hog-tie.

sting 
BrE stɪŋ, AmE stɪŋ 

A.  transitive verb(past tense, past participle stung) 
① (wound) «bee» 蜇 zhē ; «insect» 叮 dīng 
② (cause pain to) «smoke, whip, wind, nettle» 刺疼 cìténg ‹person, skin, eyes›
③ figurative (hurt) «remark, insult, criticism» 刺痛 cìtòng to sting [somebody] into action 刺激某人采取行动 
④ colloquial (swindle) 敲诈 qiāozhà to sting [somebody] for [something]; 宰某人某数额 to sting [somebody] for £10 敲某人10英镑的竹杠 
B.  intransitive verb(past tense, past participle stung) 
① (hurt, be site of pain) «cut, hand, eyes» 感觉刺痛 gǎnjué cìtòng it stings! 真蜇人！ to sting from or with [something]; 因某物而感到刺痛 
② (cause pain) «nettle, insect» 引起刺痛感 yǐnqǐ cìtòng gǎn bitter remarks that really stung 十分伤人的刻薄话 
C.  noun 
① (wound) (of bee, wasp, scorpion) 蜇伤 zhēshāng (of mosquito) 叮伤 dīngshāng (of plant) 刺伤 cìshāng 
② (organ) (of insect, scorpion) 蜇针 zhēzhēn (of plant) 刺 cì 
③ (pain) 刺痛 cìtòng 
④ figurative (hurtful effect) 痛苦 tòngkǔ to take the sting out of [something] 减轻某事物引起的痛苦 a sting in the tail 煞风景的结局 
⑤ especially AmE colloquial (in con game, law enforcement) 圈套 quāntào a sting operation 诱捕的圈套 
sting 
▶noun
1 a bee sting: prick, wound, injury, puncture.
2 this cream will take the sting away: smart, pricking; pain, soreness, hurt, irritation.
3 the sting of his betrayal: heartache, heartbreak, agony, torture, torment, hurt, pain, anguish.
4 there was a sting in her words: sharpness, severity, bite, edge, pointedness, asperity; sarcasm, acrimony, malice, spite, venom.
5 informal the victim of a sting: swindle, fraud, deception; trickery, sharp practice; informal rip-off, con, fiddle, bunco.
▶verb
1 she was stung by a scorpion: prick, wound, bite; poison.
2 the smoke made her eyes sting: smart, burn, hurt, be irritated, be sore.
3 the criticism stung her: upset, wound, cut to the quick, sear, grieve, hurt, pain, torment, mortify.
4 he was stung into action: provoke, goad, incite, spur, prick, prod, rouse, drive, galvanize. ANTONYMS deter.
5 informal they stung a bank for thousands: swindle, defraud, cheat, fleece, gull; informal rip off, screw, shaft, bilk, do, rook, diddle, take for a ride, chisel, gouge.

cloak 
klōk

▶noun an outdoor overgarment, typically sleeveless, that hangs loosely from the shoulders. 
• something serving to hide or disguise something: lifting the cloak of secrecy on the arms trade. 
▶verb [with object] dress in a cloak: she cloaked herself in black. 
• hide, cover, or disguise (something): the horror of war was cloaked in the trappings of chivalry. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French cloke, dialect variant of cloche ‘bell, cloak’ (from its bell shape), from medieval Latin clocca ‘bell.’ Compare with clock1 .

suffer 
BrE ˈsʌfə, AmE ˈsəfər 

A.  transitive verb 
① (undergo) 遭受 zāoshòu he suffered a severe heart attack 他心脏病严重发作 the region has suffered severe job losses 该地区失业问题严重 to suffer hunger 挨饿 
② formal (tolerate) 忍受 rěnshòu I won't suffer it a moment more 我一刻也受不了了 to not suffer fools gladly 不能容忍愚蠢的人 
B.  intransitive verb 
① (feel pain) 受苦 shòukǔ martyrs who suffered for their faith 为信仰而受难的殉道者 she suffers terribly with her feet colloquial 她的脚痛得不得了 to be the first to suffer 第一个吃苦头 to suffer in silence 默默忍受痛苦 
② (with illness) 患病 huànbìng to suffer from depression/high blood pressure 患抑郁症/高血压 she was still suffering from shock 她仍然惊魂未定 
③ (be in bad situation) 受到妨碍 shòudào fáng'ài business suffered badly in the recession 生意在经济衰退期受到重创 the project suffers from a lack of funds 这个项目受困于资金短缺 
④ (become worse) 变差 biànchà she keeps late hours, and her work is beginning to suffer 她睡得很晚，工作质量开始受影响 his health will suffer 他的健康将会受到损害 
suffer 
▶verb
1 I hate to see him suffer: hurt, ache, be in pain, feel pain; be in distress, be upset, be miserable.
2 she suffers from asthma: be afflicted by/with, be affected by, be troubled with, have.
3 the nation suffered a humiliating defeat: undergo, experience, be subjected to, receive, endure, face.
4 the school's reputation has suffered: be impaired, be damaged, deteriorate, decline.
5 archaic he was obliged to suffer her intimate proximity: tolerate, put up with, bear, stand, abide, endure; formal brook.
6 archaic my conscience would not suffer me to accept: allow, permit, let, give leave to, sanction.

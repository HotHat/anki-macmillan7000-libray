weaken 
BrE ˈwiːk(ə)n, AmE ˈwikən 

A.  intransitive verb 
① (lose physical strength) «person, muscle» 变虚弱 biàn xūruò 
② (lose power) «resolution, structure, alliance, army, love» 动摇 dòngyáo the party's grip on power was weakening 该党对权力的控制日趋减弱 
③ Economics, Finance «economy» 衰退 shuāituì ; «demand» 减少 jiǎnshǎo ; «price, exchange rate» 降低 jiàngdī ; «currency» 贬值 biǎnzhí 
B.  transitive verb 
① (physically) 使…虚弱 shǐ… xūruò ‹person, heart›; 减少 jiǎnshǎo ‹stamina, resistance›
② (in power) 削弱 xuēruò ‹allegiance, government, will›the war weakened support for the regime 这场战争削弱了对该政权的支持 
③ Economics 使…衰退 shǐ… shuāituì ‹economy›; 使…降低 shǐ… jiàngdī ‹price, exchange rate›; 使…贬值 shǐ… biǎnzhí ‹currency›
④ (structurally) 动摇 dòngyáo ‹structure, defence›; 使…松动 shǐ… sōngdòng ‹riverbank›
⑤ (dilute) 稀释 xīshì ‹solution, concentration›
weaken 
▶verb
1 the virus weakened him terribly: enfeeble, debilitate, incapacitate, sap, enervate, tire, exhaust, wear out; wither, cripple, disable, emasculate.
2 she tried to weaken the blow for him: reduce, decrease, diminish, soften, lessen, moderate, temper, dilute, blunt, mitigate.
3 our morale weakened: decrease, dwindle, diminish, wane, ebb, subside, peter out, fizzle out, tail off, decline, falter.
4 the move weakened her authority: impair, undermine, erode, eat away at, compromise; invalidate, negate, discredit.

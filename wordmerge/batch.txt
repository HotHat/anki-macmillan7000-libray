batch 
BrE batʃ, AmE bætʃ 

A.  noun 
① (quantity) 一批 yī pī we produce 400 batches of tins daily 我们每天生产400批罐头 I have a huge batch of letters to answer 我有一大批信要回复 in batches 分批 
② Computing 批处理 pīchǔlǐ 
B.  transitive verb 分批处理 fēnpī chǔlǐ ‹newspapers, orders›
batch 
baCH

▶noun a quantity or consignment of goods produced at one time: a batch of cookies 
the company undertakes thirty-six separate quality control checks on every batch. 
• informal a number of things or people regarded as a group or set: a batch of hostile letters came. 
• Computing a group of records processed as a single unit, usually without input from a user. 
▶verb [with object] arrange (things) in sets or groups. ORIGIN late 15th century (in the senses ‘process of baking,’ ‘quantity produced at one baking’): based on an Old English word related to bacan (see bake1). Current senses date from the early 18th century.

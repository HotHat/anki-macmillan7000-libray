auction 
ˈôkSH(ə)n

▶noun a public sale in which goods or property are sold to the highest bidder: the books are expected to fetch a six-figure sum at tomorrow's auction 
[as modifier] : an auction sale. 
• the action or process of selling something at an auction: the Ferrari sold at auction for $10 million. 
• Bridge the part of the play in which players bid to decide the contract in which the hand shall be played. 
▶verb [with object] sell or offer for sale at an auction: his collection of vintage cars is to be auctioned off tomorrow. 
PHRASES
on the auction block see on the block at block. ORIGIN late 16th century: from Latin auctio(n)- ‘increase, auction,’ from the verb augere ‘to increase.’

bully
1 
ˈbo͝olē

▶noun (plural bullies) a person who uses strength or power to harm or intimidate those who are weaker. 
▶verb (bullies, bullying, bullied) [with object] use superior strength or influence to intimidate (someone), typically to force him or her to do what one wants: a local man was bullied into helping them. ORIGIN mid 16th century: probably from Middle Dutch boele ‘lover.’ The original usage was as a term of endearment applied to either sex; later becoming a familiar form of address to a male friend. The current sense dates from the late 17th century.

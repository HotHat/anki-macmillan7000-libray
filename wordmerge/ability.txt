ability 
əˈbilədē

▶noun (plural abilities)
1 [in singular, with infinitive] possession of the means or skill to do something: the manager had lost his ability to motivate the players 
they'll examine your ability to pay.
2 talent, skill, or proficiency in a particular area: a man of exceptional ability 
students of all abilities. 
PHRASES
to the best of one's ability as well as one can: Conrad must do his job to the best of his abilities.ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French ablete, from Latin habilitas, from habilis ‘able.’

fiscal 
ˈfisk(ə)l

▶adjective relating to government revenue, especially taxes: monetary and fiscal policy. 
• chiefly North American relating to financial matters: the domestic fiscal crisis. 
• North American used to denote a fiscal year: the budget deficit for fiscal 1996. 
▶noun archaic a legal or treasury official in some countries. ORIGIN mid 16th century: from French, or from Latin fiscalis, from fiscus ‘purse, treasury’ (see fisc) .

desert
1 
dəˈzərt

▶verb [with object] abandon (a person, cause, or organization) in a way considered disloyal or treacherous: he deserted his wife and daughter and went back to England. 
• (of a number of people) leave (a place), causing it to appear empty: good weather came after the summer hordes had deserted the beaches. 
• (of a quality or ability) fail (someone), especially at a crucial moment when most needed: her luck deserted her. 
• [no object] Military (of a soldier) illegally run away from military service. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French deserter, from late Latin desertare, from Latin desertus ‘left waste’ (see desert2) .

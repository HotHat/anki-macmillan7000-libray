expedition 
ˌekspəˈdiSH(ə)n

▶noun
1 a journey or voyage undertaken by a group of people with a particular purpose, especially that of exploration, scientific research, or war: an expedition to the jungles of the Orinoco. 
• the people involved in an expedition: many of the expedition have passed rigorous courses. 
• informal a short trip made for a particular purpose: a shopping expedition.
2 formal promptness or speed in doing something: the landlord shall remedy the defects with all possible expedition. ORIGIN late Middle English: via Old French from Latin expeditio(n-), from expedire ‘extricate’ (see expedite). Early senses included ‘prompt supply of something’ and ‘setting out with aggressive intent’ The notions of ‘speed’ and ‘purpose’ are retained in current senses. Sense
1 dates from the late 16th century.

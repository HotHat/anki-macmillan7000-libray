July 
jəˈlī

▶noun (plural Julys) the seventh month of the year, in the northern hemisphere usually considered the second month of summer: I had a letter from him in July 
[as modifier] : one hot July afternoon in 1981. ORIGIN Middle English: from Latin Julius (mensis)‘(month) of July,’ named after Julius Caesar.

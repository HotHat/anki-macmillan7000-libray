interrupt 
ˌin(t)əˈrəpt

▶verb [with object]
1 stop the continuous progress of (an activity or process): the buzzer interrupted his thoughts. 
• stop (someone speaking) by saying or doing something: “Of course …” Shepherd began, but his son interrupted him 
[with direct speech] : “Hold on,” he interrupted.
2 break the continuity of (a line or surface): the coastal plain is interrupted by chains of large lagoons. 
• obstruct (something, especially a view). DERIVATIVES interruptible adjective. interruptive 
ˌin(t)əˈrəptiv 
adjectiveORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin interrupt- ‘broken, interrupted,’ from the verb interrumpere, from inter- ‘between’ + rumpere ‘to break.’

▶noun North American a type of beer with a bitter flavor and higher alcoholic content: amber-colored beers, ales, and stouts. 
• chiefly British beer. ORIGIN Old English alu, ealu, of Germanic origin; related to Old Norse ǫl. Formerly the word referred especially to unhopped or paler-colored varieties of beer.

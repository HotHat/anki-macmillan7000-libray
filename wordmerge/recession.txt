recession 
rəˈseSH(ə)n

▶noun
1 a period of temporary economic decline during which trade and industrial activity are reduced, generally identified by a fall in GDP in two successive quarters.
2 chiefly Astronomy the action of receding; motion away from an observer. DERIVATIVES recessionary 
rəˈseSHəˌnerē 
adjectiveORIGIN mid 17th century: from Latin recessio(n-), from recess- ‘gone back,’ from the verb recedere (see recede) .

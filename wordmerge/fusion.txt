fusion 
ˈfyo͞oZHən

▶noun the process or result of joining two or more things together to form a single entity: a fusion of an idea from anthropology and an idea from psychology 
malformation or fusion of the three bones in the middle ear. 
• Physics short for nuclear fusion. 
• the process of causing a material or object to melt with intense heat, especially so as to join with another: the fusion of resin and glass fiber in the molding process. 
• music that is a mixture of different styles, especially jazz and rock. 
▶adjective referring to food or cooking that incorporates elements of diverse cuisines: their fusion fare includes a sushi-like roll of gingery rice and eel wrapped in marinated Greek grape leaves. DERIVATIVES fusional

adjectiveORIGIN mid 16th century: from Latin fusio(n-), from fundere ‘pour, melt.’

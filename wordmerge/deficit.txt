deficit 
ˈdefəsət

▶noun the amount by which something, especially a sum of money, is too small. 
• an excess of expenditure or liabilities over income or assets in a given period: an annual operating deficit 
the budget will remain in deficit. 
• (in sports) the amount or score by which a team or individual is losing: came back from a 3–0 deficit. 
• technical a deficiency or failing, especially in a neurological or psychological function: deficits in speech comprehension. ORIGIN late 18th century: via French from Latin deficit ‘it is lacking,’ from the verb deficere (see defect1) .

bureaucrat 
ˈbyo͝orəˌkrat

▶noun an official in a government department, in particular one perceived as being concerned with procedural correctness at the expense of people's needs. ORIGIN mid 19th century: from French bureaucrate, from bureaucratie (see bureaucracy) .

asthma 
ˈazmə

▶noun a respiratory condition marked by spasms in the bronchi of the lungs, causing difficulty in breathing. It usually results from an allergic reaction or other forms of hypersensitivity. ORIGIN late Middle English: from medieval Latin asma, from Greek asthma, from azein ‘breathe hard.’

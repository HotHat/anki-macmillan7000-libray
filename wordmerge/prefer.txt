prefer 
prəˈfər

▶verb (prefers, preferring, preferred) [with object]
1 like (one thing or person) better than another or others; tend to choose: I prefer Venice to Rome 
[with infinitive] : I would prefer to discuss the matter in private 
[with clause] : Val would presumably prefer that you didn't get arrested 
(as adjective preferred) : his preferred candidate.
2 formal submit (a charge or a piece of information) for consideration: the police will prefer charges.
3 archaic promote or advance (someone) to a prestigious position: he was preferred to the post. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French preferer, from Latin praeferre, from prae ‘before’ + ferre ‘to bear, carry.’

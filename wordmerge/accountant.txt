accountant 
əˈkount(ə)nt 
(abbreviation acct.) 
▶noun a person whose job is to keep or inspect financial accounts. ORIGIN Middle English: from legal French, present participle of Old French aconter (see account). The original use was as an adjective meaning ‘liable to give an account,’ hence denoting a person who must do so.

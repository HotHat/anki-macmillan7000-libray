hail
1 
hāl

▶noun pellets of frozen rain that fall in showers from cumulonimbus clouds. 
• [in singular] a large number of objects hurled forcefully through the air: a hail of bullets. 
▶verb [no object]
1 (it hails, it is hailing, etc.) hail falls: it hailed so hard we had to stop.
2 [with adverbial of direction] (of a large number of objects) fall or be hurled forcefully: missiles and bombs hail down from the sky. ORIGIN Old English hagol, hægl (noun), hagalian (verb), of Germanic origin; related to Dutch hagel and German Hagel .

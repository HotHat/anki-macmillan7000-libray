juvenile 
ˈjo͞ovəˌnīl ˈjo͞ovənl

▶adjective of, for, or relating to young people: juvenile crime. 
• childish; immature: she's bored with my juvenile conversation. 
• of or denoting a theatrical or film role representing a young person: the romantic juvenile lead. 
• relating to young birds or other animals. 
▶noun a young person. 
• Law a person below the age at which ordinary criminal prosecution is possible (18 in most countries). 
• a young bird or other animal. 
• an actor who plays juvenile roles. DERIVATIVES juvenility 
ˌjo͞ovəˈnilədē 
nounORIGIN early 17th century: from Latin juvenilis, from juvenis ‘young, a young person.’

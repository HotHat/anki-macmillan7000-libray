underestimate 
A.  /BrE ʌndərˈɛstɪmeɪt, AmE ˌəndərˈɛstəˌmeɪt
transitive verb 低估 dīgū 
B.  /BrE ʌndərˈɛstɪmət, AmE ˌəndərˈɛstəmət
noun 低估 dīgū 
underestimate 
▶verb 
ˌəndərˈestəˌmāt 
[with object] estimate (something) to be smaller or less important than it actually is: the administration has grossly underestimated the extent of the problem. 
• regard (someone) as less capable than they really are: he had underestimated the new president. 
▶noun 
ˌəndərˈestəmət 
[usually in singular] an estimate that is too low.

sibling 
BrE ˈsɪblɪŋ, AmE ˈsɪblɪŋ 
noun 
① (elder brother) 兄 xiōng ; (younger brother) 弟 dì 
② (elder sister) 姐 jiě ; (younger sister) 妹 mèi 
sibling 
ˈsibliNG

▶noun each of two or more children or offspring having one or both parents in common; a brother or sister. ORIGIN Old English in the sense ‘relative’ (see sib, -ling). The current sense dates from the early 20th century.

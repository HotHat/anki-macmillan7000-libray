behalf 
bəˈhaf

▶noun (in phrase on (US also in) behalf of or on someone's/something's behalf)
1 in the interests of a person, group, or principle: votes cast by labor unions on behalf of their members.
2 as a representative of: he had to attend the funeral on Mama's behalf.
3 on the part of; done by: this wasn't simply a philanthropic gesture on his behalf. ORIGIN Middle English: from a mixture of the earlier phrases on his halve and bihalve him, both meaning ‘on his side’ (see by, half) .

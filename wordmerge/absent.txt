absent 
▶adjective 
ˈabsənt 

1 not present in a place, at an occasion, or as part of something: most students were absent from school at least once 
absent colleagues 
wings are absent in several species of crane flies.
2 (of an expression or manner) showing that someone is not paying attention to what is being said or done: she looked up with an absent smile. 
▶verb 
abˈsent 
[with object] (absent oneself) stay or go away: various people absented themselves because of his presence 
halfway through the meal, he absented himself from the table. 
▶preposition 
ˈabsənt 
formal,North American without: employees could not be fired absent other evidence. ORIGIN Middle English: via Old French from Latin absens, absent- ‘being absent,’ present participle of abesse, from ab- ‘from, away’ + esse ‘to be.’

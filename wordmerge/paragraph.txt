paragraph 
ˈperəˌɡraf

▶noun a distinct section of a piece of writing, usually dealing with a single theme and indicated by a new line, indentation, or numbering. 
▶verb [with object] arrange (a piece of writing) in paragraphs. DERIVATIVES paragraphic 
ˌparəˈɡrafik 
adjectiveORIGIN late 15th century: from French paragraphe, via medieval Latin from Greek paragraphos ‘short stroke marking a break in sense,’ from para- ‘beside’ + graphein ‘write.’

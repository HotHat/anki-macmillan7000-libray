lime
1 
līm

▶noun a white caustic alkaline substance consisting of calcium oxide, which is obtained by heating limestone and which combines with water with the production of much heat; quicklime. 
• (also slaked lime) a white alkaline substance consisting of calcium hydroxide, made by adding water to quicklime. 
• (in general use) any of a number of calcium compounds, especially calcium hydroxide, used as an additive to soil or water. 
• archaic birdlime. 
▶verb [with object]
1 treat (soil or water) with lime to reduce acidity and improve fertility or oxygen levels. 
• (often as adjective limed) give (wood) a bleached appearance by treating it with lime: limed oak dining furniture.
2 archaic catch (a bird) with birdlime. DERIVATIVES limy (also limey)

scarlet 
ˈskärlət

▶adjective
1 of a brilliant red color: a mass of scarlet berries.
2 chiefly dated (of an offense or sin) wicked; heinous. 
• immoral, especially promiscuous or unchaste. 
▶noun a brilliant red color: papers lettered in scarlet and black. 
• scarlet clothes or material. ORIGIN Middle English (originally denoting any brightly colored cloth): shortening of Old French escarlate, from medieval Latin scarlata, via Arabic and medieval Greek from late Latin sigillatus ‘decorated with small images,’ from sigillum ‘small image.’

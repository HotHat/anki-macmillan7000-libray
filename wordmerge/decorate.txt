decorate 
ˈdekəˌrāt

▶verb [with object]
1 make (something) look more attractive by adding extra items or images to it. 
• provide (a room or building) with a color scheme, paint, wallpaper, etc.: the five bedrooms are individually decorated.
2 confer an award or medal on (a member of the armed forces): he was decorated for outstanding bravery. ORIGIN mid 16th century (in the sense ‘to grace or honor’): from Latin decoratus ‘embellished’ (past participle of decorare), from decus, decor- ‘beauty, honor, or embellishment.’

factory 
ˈfakt(ə)rē

▶noun (plural factories)
1 a building or group of buildings where goods are manufactured or assembled chiefly by machine. 
• [with modifier] a person, group, or institution that continually produces a great quantity of something specified: a huge factory of lying, slander, and bad English.
2 historical an establishment for traders carrying on business in a foreign country. ORIGIN late 16th century (in sense 2): from medieval Latin factoria from Latin factor (see factor). Sense
1 from late Latin factoria, literally ‘oil press.’

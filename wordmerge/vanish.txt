vanish 
ˈvaniSH

▶verb [no object]
1 disappear suddenly and completely: Mary vanished without a trace. 
• gradually cease to exist: the days of the extended family are vanishing.
2 Mathematics become zero. ORIGIN Middle English: shortening of Old French e(s)vaniss-, lengthened stem of e(s)vanir, from Latin evanescere ‘die away.’

ecology 
ēˈkäləjē

▶noun
1 the branch of biology that deals with the relations of organisms to one another and to their physical surroundings.
2 (also Ecology) the political movement that seeks to protect the environment, especially from pollution. ORIGIN late 19th century (originally as oecology): from Greek oikos ‘house’ + -logy .

distinctive 
dəˈstiNG(k)tiv

▶adjective characteristic of one person or thing, and so serving to distinguish it from others: juniper berries give gin its distinctive flavor. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘serving to differentiate’): from late Latin distinctivus, from Latin distinct- ‘distinguished’ (see distinct) .

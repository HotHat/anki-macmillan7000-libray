envisage 
BrE ɪnˈvɪzɪdʒ, ɛnˈvɪzɪdʒ, AmE ənˈvɪzɪdʒ 
transitive verb 
① (anticipate) 展望 zhǎnwàng to envisage [something]; 设想 ‹failure, prosperity, situation, possibility›it is envisaged that … 预期… 
② (visualize) 想象 xiǎngxiàng ‹scene, outcome›
envisage 
ənˈvizij

▶verb [with object] contemplate or conceive of as a possibility or a desirable future event: the Rome Treaty envisaged free movement across frontiers. 
• form a mental picture of (something not yet existing or known): he knew what he liked but had difficulty envisaging it. ORIGIN early 19th century: from French envisager, from en- ‘in’ + visage ‘face.’

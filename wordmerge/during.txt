during 
ˈd(y)o͝oriNG

▶preposition throughout the course or duration of (a period of time): the restaurant is open during the day 
the period during which he grew to adulthood. 
• at a particular point in the course of: the stabbing took place during an argument at a party. ORIGIN late Middle English: present participle of the obsolete verb dure ‘last, endure, extend,’ via Old French from Latin durare ‘to last’ (see duration) .

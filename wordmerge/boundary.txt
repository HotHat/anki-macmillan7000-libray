boundary 
ˈbound(ə)rē

▶noun (plural boundaries) a line that marks the limits of an area; a dividing line: the eastern boundary of the wilderness 
the boundary between the US and Canada 
[as modifier] : a boundary wall. 
• (often boundaries) a limit of a subject or sphere of activity: a community without class or political boundaries. ORIGIN early 17th century: variant of dialect bounder, from bound2 + -er1, perhaps on the pattern of limitary.

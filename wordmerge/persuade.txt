persuade 
pərˈswād

▶verb [with object and infinitive] cause (someone) to do something through reasoning or argument: it wasn't easy, but I persuaded him to do the right thing. 
• [with object] cause (someone) to believe something, especially after a sustained effort; convince: they must often be persuaded of the potential severity of their drinking problems 
[with object and clause] : he did everything he could to persuade the police that he was the robber. 
• (of a situation or event) provide a sound reason for (someone) to do something: the cost of the manor's restoration persuaded them to take in guests. DERIVATIVES persuasible 
pərˈswāzəb(ə)l 
adjectiveORIGIN late 15th century: from Latin persuadere, from per- ‘through, to completion’ + suadere ‘advise.’ usage: For a discussion of the difference between persuade and convince, see usage at convince .

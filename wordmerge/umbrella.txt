umbrella 
ˌəmˈbrelə

▶noun
1 a device consisting of a circular canopy of cloth on a folding metal frame supported by a central rod, used as protection against rain or sometimes sun.
2 a protecting force or influence: the American nuclear umbrella over the West. 
• a screen of fighter aircraft or antiaircraft artillery.
3 [usually as modifier] a thing that includes or contains many different elements or parts: an umbrella organization.
4 Zoology the gelatinous disk of a jellyfish, which it contracts and expands to move through the water. DERIVATIVES umbrellaed adjective. umbrellalike 
-ˌlīk 
adjectiveORIGIN early 17th century: from Italian ombrella, diminutive of ombra ‘shade,’ from Latin umbra (see umbra) .

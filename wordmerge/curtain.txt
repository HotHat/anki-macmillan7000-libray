curtain 
BrE ˈkəːt(ə)n, AmE ˈkərtn 
A. noun 
① (for window) 窗帘 chuānglián (for door) 门帘 ménlián a pair of curtains 一副窗帘 to open/draw the curtains 拉开/拉上窗帘 
② figurative (screen, cover) 幕状物 mùzhuàngwù a curtain of rain 雨幕 a curtain of silence 以沉默编织的帷幕 
③ Theatre 帷幕 wéimù after the final curtain 最后一幕之后 to raise/lower the curtain 启幕/落幕 the curtain has fallen on … …结束了 ‹career, era›the curtain rose on the new millennium 新千年开始了 
④ to be curtains (for [somebody]) colloquial （某人）该完蛋了 (mǒu rén)gāi wándàn le 
B.  transitive verb 给…装上帘子 gěi… zhuāngshang liánzi ‹window, room›PHRASAL VERB curtain off transitive verb [curtain something off], [curtain off something] 用帘子隔开 yòng liánzi gékāi to curtain [something] off from [something]; 把…和某物用帘子隔开 ‹hospital bed, cubicle, alcove›to be curtained off from the rest of the room 被帘子和房间的其他部分隔开 
curtain 
▶noun he drew the curtains: drape, drapery; window treatment, window hanging, screen, blind(s), shade; valance, cafe curtain.
▶verb the bed was curtained off from the rest of the room: conceal, hide, screen, shield; separate, isolate.

average 
BrE ˈav(ə)rɪdʒ, AmE ˈæv(ə)rɪdʒ 
A. noun 
① Mathematics 平均数 píngjūnshù at an average of … 以…的平均值 to take an average 取平均值 
② (standard amount, level) 一般水平 yībān shuǐpíng the national average 全国平均水平 on average 以平均数论 the law of averages 平均律 
B.  adjective 
① Mathematics 平均的 píngjūn de 
② (usual, ordinary) 普通的 pǔtōng de we have above average inflation in this country 我国的通货膨胀率偏高 it was a pretty average sort of film 这部电影很一般 
C.  transitive verb 平均达到 píngjūn dádào I average seven hours' work a day 我平均一天工作7小时 PHRASAL VERB average out: A. intransitive verb to average out to or at …; 平均为… píngjūn wéi… their working day averages out at 7-8 hours 他们每个工作日平均为7到8小时 I pay the mortgage and he pays the bills: it seems to average out okay 我付按揭还款，他付日常开支：看来两者持平 
B.  transitive verb [average something out, average out something] 算出…的平均值 suànchū… de píngjūnzhí 
average 
▶noun the price is above the national average: mean, median, mode; norm, standard, rule, par.
▶adjective
1 the average temperature in May: mean, median, modal.
2 a woman of average height: ordinary, standard, normal, typical, regular; midsize.
3 a very average director: mediocre, second-rate, undistinguished, ordinary, middle-of-the-road, unexceptional, unexciting, unremarkable, unmemorable, indifferent, pedestrian, lackluster, forgettable, amateurish; informal OK, so-so, 'comme ci, comme ça', fair-to-middling, no great shakes, underwhelming, plain-vanilla. ANTONYMS outstanding, exceptional.
PHRASES
on average on average, we get about two million visitors each year: normally, usually, ordinarily, generally, in general, for the most part, as a rule, typically; overall, by and large, on the whole.

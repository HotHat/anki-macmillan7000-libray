stubborn 
ˈstəbərn

▶adjective having or showing dogged determination not to change one's attitude or position on something, especially in spite of good arguments or reasons to do so: he accused her of being a silly, stubborn old woman. 
• difficult to move, remove, or cure: the removal of stubborn screws. ORIGIN Middle English (originally in the sense ‘untamable, implacable’): of unknown origin.

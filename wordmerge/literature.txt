literature 
ˈlidərəCHər ˈlidərəˌCHo͝or

▶noun written works, especially those considered of superior or lasting artistic merit: a great work of literature. 
• books and writings published on a particular subject: the literature on environmental epidemiology. 
• leaflets and other printed matter used to advertise products or give advice. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘knowledge of books’): via French from Latin litteratura, from littera (see letter) .

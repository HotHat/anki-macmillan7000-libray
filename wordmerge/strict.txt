strict 
strikt

▶adjective demanding that rules concerning behavior are obeyed and observed: my father was very strict 
a strict upbringing. 
• (of a rule or discipline) demanding total obedience or observance; rigidly enforced: civil servants are bound by strict rules on secrecy. 
• (of a person) following rules or beliefs exactly: a strict vegetarian. 
• exact in correspondence or adherence to something; not allowing or admitting deviation or relaxation: a strict interpretation of the law. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘restricted in space or extent’): from Latin strictus, past participle of stringere ‘tighten, draw tight.’

Mrs. 
ˈmisiz ˈmisəs

▶noun a title used before a surname or full name to address or refer to a married woman, or a woman who has been married, without a higher or honorific or professional title: Mrs. Sally Jones. ORIGIN early 17th century: abbreviation of mistress; compare with missus .

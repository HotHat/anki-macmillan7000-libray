imitation 
ˌiməˈtāSH(ə)n

▶noun
1 the action of using someone or something as a model: a child learns to speak by imitation. 
• an act of imitating a person's speech or mannerisms, especially for comic effect: he attempted an atrocious imitation of my English accent. 
• Music the repetition of a phrase or melody in another part or voice, usually at a different pitch.
2 a thing intended to simulate or copy something else: [as modifier] : an imitation diamond. 
PHRASES
imitation is the sincerest form of flattery proverb copying someone or something is an implicit way of paying them a compliment.ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin imitatio(n-), from the verb imitari (see imitate) .

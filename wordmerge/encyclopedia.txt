inˌsīkləˈpēdēə enˌsīkləˈpēdēə 
(also chiefly British encyclopaedia) 
▶noun a book or set of books giving information on many subjects or on many aspects of one subject and typically arranged alphabetically. ORIGIN mid 16th century: modern Latin, from pseudo-Greek enkuklopaideia for enkuklios paideia ‘all-around education.’

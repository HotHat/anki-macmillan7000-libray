innocence 
ˈinəsəns

▶noun the state, quality, or fact of being innocent of a crime or offense: they must prove their innocence. 
• lack of guile or corruption; purity: the healthy bloom in her cheeks gave her an aura of innocence. 
• used euphemistically to refer to a person's virginity: they'd avenge assaults on her innocence by others. 
PHRASES
in all innocence without knowledge of something's significance or possible consequences: she knew the gift had been chosen in all innocence.DERIVATIVES innocency noun ( archaic)ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French, from Latin innocentia, from innocent- ‘not harming’ (based on nocere ‘injure’).

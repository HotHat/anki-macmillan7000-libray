publicity 
pəbˈlisədē

▶noun the notice or attention given to someone or something by the media: the case attracted wide publicity in the press. 
• the giving out of information about a product, person, or company for advertising or promotional purposes: head of publicity and marketing 
[as modifier] : publicity photographs. 
• material or information used for publicity: we distributed publicity from a stall in the marketplace. ORIGIN late 18th century: from French publicité, from public ‘public’ (see public) .

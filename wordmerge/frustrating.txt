frustrate 
ˈfrəstrāt

▶verb [with object] prevent (a plan or attempted action) from progressing, succeeding, or being fulfilled: his attempt to frustrate the merger. 
• prevent (someone) from doing or achieving something: an increasingly popular way to frustrate car thieves. 
• cause (someone) to feel upset or annoyed, typically as a result of being unable to change or achieve something: (as adjective frustrating) : it can be very frustrating to find that the size you want isn't there. 
▶adjective archaic frustrated. DERIVATIVES frustrater nounORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin frustrat- ‘disappointed,’ from the verb frustrare, from frustra ‘in vain.’

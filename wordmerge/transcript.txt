transcript 
ˈtran(t)skript

▶noun a written or printed version of material originally presented in another medium. 
• Biochemistry a length of RNA or DNA that has been transcribed respectively from a DNA or RNA template. 
• an official record of a student's work, showing courses taken and grades achieved. DERIVATIVES transcriptive 
ˌtranˈskriptiv 
adjectiveORIGIN Middle English: from Old French transcrit, from Latin transcriptum, neuter past participle of transcribere (see transcribe). The spelling change in the 15th century was due to association with the Latin.

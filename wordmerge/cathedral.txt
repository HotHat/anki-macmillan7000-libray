cathedral 
kəˈTHēdrəl

▶noun the principal church of a diocese, with which the bishop is officially associated: [in names] : St. Paul's Cathedral. ORIGIN Middle English (as an adjective, the noun being short for cathedral church ‘the church that contains the bishop's throne’): from late Latin cathedralis, from Latin cathedra ‘seat,’ from Greek kathedra .

October 
äkˈtōbər

▶noun the tenth month of the year, in the northern hemisphere usually considered the second month of autumn: the project started in October 
[as modifier] : on an October night. ORIGIN late Old English, from Latin, from octo ‘eight’ (being originally the eighth month of the Roman year).

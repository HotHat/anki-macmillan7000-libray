luxury 
ˈləkSH(ə)rē ˈləɡZH(ə)rē

▶noun (plural luxuries) the state of great comfort and extravagant living: he lived a life of luxury. 
• an inessential, desirable item that is expensive or difficult to obtain: luxuries like raspberry vinegar and state-of-the-art CD players 
he considers bananas a luxury. 
▶adjective luxurious or of the nature of a luxury: a luxury yacht 
luxury goods. ORIGIN Middle English (denoting lechery): from Old French luxurie, luxure, from Latin luxuria, from luxus ‘excess.’ The earliest current sense dates from the mid 17th century.

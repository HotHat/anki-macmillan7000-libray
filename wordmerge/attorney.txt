attorney 
əˈtərnē

▶noun (plural attorneys)
1 a person appointed to act for another in business or legal matters.
2 chiefly US a lawyer. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French atorne, past participle of atorner ‘assign,’ from a ‘toward’ + torner ‘turn.’

ˌrefəˈrendəm

▶noun (plural referendums or referenda

) a general vote by the electorate on a single political question that has been referred to them for a direct decision. ORIGIN mid 19th century: from Latin, gerund (‘referring’), or neuter gerundive (‘something to be brought back or referred’) of referre (see refer) .

expire 
BrE ɪkˈspʌɪə, ɛkˈspʌɪə, AmE ˌɪkˈspaɪ(ə)r 
intransitive verb 
① (end) «passport, permit, licence, lease, contract» 到期 dàoqī ; «time limit, period of time» 结束 jiéshù ; «patent, prison sentence» 期满 qīmǎn ; «term of office» 届满 jièmǎn 
② formal (exhale) «person, animal» 呼气 hū qì ; «plant» 释放气体 shìfàng qìtǐ 
③ formal (die) «person, animal» 死亡 sǐwáng 
expire 
▶verb
1 my contract has expired: run out, become invalid, become void, lapse; end, finish, stop, come to an end, terminate.
2 the spot where he expired: die, pass away/on, breathe one's last; informal kick the bucket, bite the dust, croak, buy it, buy the farm; dated depart this life.
3 technical the breath is then expired: breathe out, exhale, blow out, expel.

expire 
ˌikˈspī(ə)r

▶verb
1 [no object] (of a document, authorization, or agreement) cease to be valid, typically after a fixed period of time: the old contract had expired. 
• (of a period of time) come to an end: the three-year period has expired. 
• (of a person) die.
2 [with object] technical exhale (air) from the lung. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French expirer, from Latin exspirare ‘breathe out,’ from ex- ‘out’ + spirare ‘breathe.’

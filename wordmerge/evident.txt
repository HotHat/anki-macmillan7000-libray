evident 
ˈevədənt

▶adjective plain or obvious; clearly seen or understood: she ate the cookies with evident enjoyment. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French, or from Latin evidens, evident- ‘obvious to the eye or mind,’ from e- (variant of ex-)‘out’ + videre ‘to see.’

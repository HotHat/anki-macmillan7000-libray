revert 
▶verb 
rəˈvərt 

1 [no object] (revert to) return to (a previous state, practice, topic, etc.): he reverted to his native language 
he ignored her words by reverting to the former subject. 
• convert to (the Islamic faith): I reverted to Islam five years ago. 
• Biology return to (a former or ancestral type): it is impossible that a fishlike mammal will actually revert to being a true fish. 
• Law (of property) return or pass to (the original owner) by reversion.
2 [no object] chiefly Indian reply or respond to someone: we texted both Farah and Shirish, but neither of them reverted 
if interested, kindly revert with your updated resume.
3 [with object] archaic turn (one's eyes or steps) back: on reverting our eyes, every step presented some new and admirable scene. 
▶noun 
ˈrēˌvərt 
a person who has converted to the Islamic faith. DERIVATIVES reverter 
rəˈvərdər 
noun (Law)ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French revertir or Latin revertere ‘turn back.’ Early senses included ‘recover consciousness,’ ‘return to a position,’ and ‘return to a person (after estrangement).’

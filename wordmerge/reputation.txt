reputation 
ˌrepyəˈtāSH(ə)n

▶noun the beliefs or opinions that are generally held about someone or something: his reputation was tarnished by allegations that he had taken bribes. 
• a widespread belief that someone or something has a particular habit or characteristic: his knowledge of his subject earned him a reputation as an expert. ORIGIN Middle English: from Latin reputatio(n-), from reputare ‘think over’ (see repute) .

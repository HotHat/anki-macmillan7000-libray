prosecution 
ˌpräsəˈkyo͞oSH(ə)n

▶noun
1 the institution and conducting of legal proceedings against someone in respect of a criminal charge: Olesky faces prosecution on charges he spied for Russian intelligence 
they lacked the funds to embark on private prosecutions. 
• (the prosecution) [treated as singular or plural] the party instituting or conducting legal proceedings against someone in a lawsuit: the main witness for the prosecution.
2 the continuation of a course of action with a view to its completion: the network's prosecution of its commercial ends. ORIGIN mid 16th century ( sense 2): from Old French, or from late Latin prosecutio(n-), from prosequi ‘pursue, accompany’ (see prosecute) .

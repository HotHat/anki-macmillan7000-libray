duration 
d(y)o͝orˈāSH(ə)n

▶noun the time during which something continues: the subway stop has been closed for the duration of the convention 
a flight of over eight hours' duration. 
PHRASES
for the duration until the end of something, especially a war: he was in the navy for the duration plus six. 
• informal for a very long time: some stains may be there for the duration.DERIVATIVES durational adjectiveORIGIN late Middle English: via Old French from medieval Latin duratio(n-), from durare ‘to last,’ from durus ‘hard.’

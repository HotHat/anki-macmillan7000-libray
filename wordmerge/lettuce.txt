lettuce 
ˈledəs

▶noun
1 a cultivated plant of the daisy family, with edible leaves that are a usual ingredient of salads. Many varieties of lettuce have been developed with a range of form, texture, and color. [Lactuca sativa, family Compositae.] 
• used in names of other plants with edible green leaves, e.g., lamb's lettuce, sea lettuce.
2 informal US paper money; greenbacks. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French letues, laitues, plural of laitue, from Latin lactuca, from lac, lact- ‘milk’ (because of its milky juice).

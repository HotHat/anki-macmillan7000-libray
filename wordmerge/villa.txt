villa 
ˈvilə

▶noun (especially in continental Europe) a large and luxurious country residence. 
• a large country house of Roman times, having an estate and consisting of farm and residential buildings arranged around a courtyard. 
• British a detached or semidetached house in a residential district, typically one that is Victorian or Edwardian in style. ORIGIN early 17th century: from Italian, from Latin.

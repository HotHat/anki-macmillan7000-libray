entertain 
BrE ɛntəˈteɪn, AmE ˌɛn(t)ərˈteɪn 
A. transitive verb 
① (amuse, occupy) 使快乐 shǐ kuàilè to entertain [somebody] with [something]; 用某事物给某人带来快乐 to keep [somebody] entertained; 一直逗某人高兴 
② (play host to) 款待 kuǎndài to entertain [somebody] to dinner 请某人吃饭 
③ (consider, nurture) 怀有 huáiyǒu ‹ambition, hope, thought›; 考虑 kǎolǜ ‹suggestion, proposal›
B.  intransitive verb 款待 kuǎndài 
entertain 
▶verb
1 she wrote plays to entertain them: amuse, divert, delight, please, charm, cheer, interest; informal bring the house down; engage, occupy, absorb, engross. ANTONYMS bore.
2 he entertains foreign visitors: receive, host, play host/hostess to, invite (around/round/over), throw a party for; wine and dine, feast, cater for, feed, treat, welcome, fête.
3 we don't entertain much: receive guests, have people around/round/over, have company, throw/have a party.
4 I would never entertain such an idea: consider, give consideration to, contemplate, think about, give thought to; countenance, tolerate, support; formal brook. ANTONYMS reject.

motive 
ˈmōdiv

▶noun
1 a reason for doing something, especially one that is hidden or not obvious: a motive for his murder.
2 (in art, literature, or music) a motif: the entire work grows organically from the opening horn motive. 
▶adjective [attributive]
1 producing physical or mechanical motion: the charge of gas is the motive force for every piston stroke.
2 causing or being the reason for something: the motive principle of a writer's work. DERIVATIVES motiveless 
ˈmōdivləs 
adjective. motivelessly 
ˈmōdivləslē 
adverb. motivelessness nounORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French motif (adjective used as a noun), from late Latin motivus, from movere ‘to move.’

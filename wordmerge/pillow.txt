pillow 
ˈpilō

▶noun a rectangular cloth bag stuffed with feathers, foam rubber, or other soft materials, used to support the head when lying down. 
• a piece of wood or metal used as a support; a block or bearing. 
▶verb [with object] rest (one's head) as if on a pillow. 
• literary serve as a pillow for: her shoulder pillowed his weary head. DERIVATIVES pillowy adjectiveORIGIN Old English pyle, pylu; related to Dutch peluw and German Pfühl, based on Latin pulvinus ‘cushion.’

county 
ˈkoun(t)ē

▶noun (plural counties) (in the US) a political and administrative division of a state, providing certain local governmental services. 
• a territorial division of some countries, forming the chief unit of local administration. DERIVATIVES countywide adjective & adverbORIGIN Middle English: from Old French conte, from Latin comitatus, from comes, comit- (see count2). The word seems earliest to have denoted a meeting held periodically to transact the business of a shire.

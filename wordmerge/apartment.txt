apartment 
əˈpärtmənt

▶noun North American a suite of rooms forming one residence, typically in a building containing a number of these. 
• North American a large building containing residential suites; an apartment building. 
• British an apartment that is well appointed or used for vacations: we rented an apartment by the beach for a week. 
• (apartments) a suite of rooms in a very large or grand house set aside for the private use of a monarch or noble: the Imperial apartments. ORIGIN mid 17th century (denoting a suite of rooms for the use of a particular person or group): from French appartement, from Italian appartamento, from appartare ‘to separate,’ from a parte ‘apart.’

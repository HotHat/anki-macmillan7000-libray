misery 
ˈmiz(ə)rē

▶noun (plural miseries) a state or feeling of great distress or discomfort of mind or body: she went upstairs and cried in misery 
he wrote endlessly about his frustrations and miseries. 
• (usually miseries) a cause or source of great distress or discomfort: the miseries of war. 
PHRASES
make someone's life a misery (or make life a misery for someone) cause someone severe distress by continued unpleasantness or harassment.put someone/something out of their misery end the suffering of a person or animal in pain by killing them. 
• informal release someone from suspense or anxiety by telling them something they are anxious to know.ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French miserie, from Latin miseria, from miser ‘wretched.’

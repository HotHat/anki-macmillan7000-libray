education 
ˌejəˈkāSH(ə)n

▶noun
1 the process of receiving or giving systematic instruction, especially at a school or university: a new system of public education. 
• the theory and practice of teaching: colleges of education. 
• a body of knowledge acquired while being educated: his education is encyclopedic and eclectic. 
• information about or training in a particular field or subject: health education.
2 (an education) an enlightening experience: a day with those kids was an education in patience and forbearance. DERIVATIVES educationist 
ˌejəˈkāSH(ə)nəst 
nounORIGIN mid 16th century: from Latin educatio(n-), from the verb educare (see educate) .

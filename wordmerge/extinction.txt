▶noun
1 the state or process of a species, family, or larger group being or becoming extinct: the extinction of the great auk 
mass extinctions. 
• the wiping out of a debt.
2 Physics reduction in the intensity of light or other radiation as it passes through a medium or object, due to absorption, reflection, and scattering: ultraviolet extinction. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin exstinctio(n-), from exstinguere ‘quench’ (see extinguish) .

widen 
BrE ˈwʌɪd(ə)n, AmE ˈwaɪdn 
A. transitive verb 
① (make wider) 使…变宽 shǐ… biànkuān ‹gap, road, room›to widen a skirt 把裙子加宽 
② figurative (extend) 扩展 kuòzhǎn ‹scope, experience, knowledge›to widen one's lead «athlete, racehorse, political party» 拉大领先距离 
B.  intransitive verb 
① (become wider) widen (out) «river, road, trousers» 变宽 biànkuān his eyes widened 他瞪大了眼睛 tā dèngdàle yǎnjing 
② figurative (increase) «lead, experience» 增加 zēngjiā the gap is widening between the rich and the poor 贫富差距正在拉大 
widen 
▶verb
1 a proposal to widen the highway: broaden, make/become wider, open up/out, expand, extend, enlarge.
2 the organization must widen its support: increase, augment, boost, swell, enlarge.

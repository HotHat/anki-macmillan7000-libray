decisive 
dəˈsīsiv

▶adjective settling an issue; producing a definite result: the Supreme Court voided the statute by a decisive 7–2 vote 
decisive evidence. 
• (of a person) having or showing the ability to make decisions quickly and effectively. ORIGIN early 17th century: from French décisif, -ive, from medieval Latin decisivus, from decis- ‘determined,’ from the verb decidere (see decide) .

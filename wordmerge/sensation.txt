sensation 
BrE sɛnˈseɪʃ(ə)n, AmE sɛnˈseɪʃ(ə)n 
noun 
① Countable(physical feeling) 感觉 gǎnjué (perception) 知觉 zhījué a sensation of falling 坠落感 
② Uncountable(ability to feel) 感觉能力 gǎnjué nénglì loss of sensation in the left arm 左臂失去知觉 
③ Countable(awareness) 觉察 juéchá (impression) 直觉 zhíjué the sensation that …; …的直觉 he had the eerie sensation of being watched 他有一种遭人监视的怪异直觉 
④ Countable(stir) 轰动 hōngdòng to cause or create a sensation 引起轰动 
⑤ Countablecolloquial (person) 引起轰动的人 yǐnqǐ hōngdòng de rén (object) 引起轰动的东西 yǐnqǐ hōngdòng de dōngxi (event) 引起轰动的事件 yǐnqǐ hōngdòng de shìjiàn when she first performed, she was a sensation 她的首次演出引起了轰动 the new model was the sensation of the motor show 这款新车在车展上引起了轰动 
sensation 
▶noun
1 a sensation of light: feeling, sense, awareness, consciousness, perception, impression.
2 he caused a sensation by donating a million dollars: commotion, stir, uproar, furor, scandal, impact; interest, excitement; informal splash, to-do, hullabaloo, hoopla.
3 the movie became an instant sensation: triumph, success, sellout; talking point; informal smash (hit), hit, winner, crowd-pleaser, knockout, blockbuster.

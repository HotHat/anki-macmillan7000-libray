biology 
bīˈäləjē 
(abbreviation biol.) 
▶noun the study of living organisms, divided into many specialized fields that cover their morphology, physiology, anatomy, behavior, origin, and distribution. 
• the plants and animals of a particular area: the biology of Chesapeake Bay. 
• the physiology, behavior, and other qualities of a particular organism or class of organisms: human biology. ORIGIN early 19th century: coined in German, via French from Greek bios ‘life’ + -logy .

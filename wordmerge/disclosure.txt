disclosure 
disˈklōZHər

▶noun the action of making new or secret information known: a judge ordered the disclosure of the government documents. 
• a fact, especially a secret, that is made known: the government's disclosures about missile programs. ORIGIN late 16th century: from disclose, on the pattern of closure.

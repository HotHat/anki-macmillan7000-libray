diagnose 
BrE ˈdʌɪəɡnəʊz, dʌɪəɡˈnəʊz, AmE ˌdaɪəɡˈnoʊs 
transitive verb 
① Medicine 诊断 zhěnduàn ‹illness, patient›to diagnose [something]/[somebody] as [something]; 诊断某疾病/某人为某状况 the doctor diagnosed him as being diabetic 医生诊断他患有糖尿病 to diagnose that … 下诊断结论为… 
② (determine) 判断 pànduàn the mechanic diagnosed what was wrong with the car 机修工查出了汽车的毛病 to diagnose our present economic ills as the result of poor planning; 判断我们目前的经济弊病是计划不周的结果 
diagnose 
ˌdīəɡˈnōs

▶verb [with object] identify the nature of (an illness or other problem) by examination of the symptoms: doctors diagnosed a rare and fatal liver disease. 
• (usually be diagnosed) identify the nature of the medical condition of (someone): she was finally diagnosed as having epilepsy 
20,000 men are diagnosed with skin cancer every year. DERIVATIVES diagnosable adjectiveORIGIN mid 19th century: back-formation from diagnosis .

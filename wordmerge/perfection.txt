perfection 
pərˈfekSH(ə)n

▶noun the condition, state, or quality of being free or as free as possible from all flaws or defects: the satiny perfection of her skin 
his pursuit of golfing perfection. 
• a person or thing perceived as the embodiment of perfection: I am told that she is perfection itself. 
• the action or process of improving something until it is faultless or as faultless as possible: among the key tasks was the perfection of new mechanisms of economic management. 
PHRASES
to perfection in a manner or way that could not be better; perfectly: a blue suit that showed off her blonde hair to perfection.ORIGIN Middle English (in the sense ‘completeness’): via Old French from Latin perfectio(n-), from perficere ‘to complete’ (see perfect) .

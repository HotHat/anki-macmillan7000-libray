adjective 
① (competent and quick) 效率高的 xiàolǜ gāo de to be efficient at doing [something]; 做某事效率高 an efficient secretary 能干的秘书 
② (economical) 收效大的 shōuxiào dà de to make efficient use of energy 有效利用能源 
efficient 
əˈfiSHənt

▶adjective (especially of a system or machine) achieving maximum productivity with minimum wasted effort or expense: fluorescent lamps are efficient at converting electricity into light. 
• (of a person) working in a well-organized and competent way: an efficient administrator. 
• [in combination] preventing the wasteful use of a particular resource: an energy-efficient heating system. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘making, causing,’ usually in efficient cause): from Latin efficient- ‘accomplishing,’ from the verb efficere (see effect). The current sense dates from the late 18th century.

welfare 
ˈwelˌfer

▶noun the health, happiness, and fortunes of a person or group: they don't give a damn about the welfare of their families. 
• statutory procedure or social effort designed to promote the basic physical and material well-being of people in need: the protection of rights to education, housing, and welfare. 
• chiefly North American financial support given to people in need. 
PHRASES
on welfare receiving government financial assistance for basic material needs.ORIGIN Middle English: from the adverb well1 + the verb fare .

ingenious 
inˈjēnyəs

▶adjective (of a person) clever, original, and inventive: he was ingenious enough to overcome the limited budget. 
• (of a machine or idea) cleverly and originally devised and well suited to its purpose. DERIVATIVES ingeniousness 
inˈjēnyəsnəs 
nounORIGIN late Middle English: from French ingénieux or Latin ingeniosus, from ingenium ‘mind, intellect’; compare with engine . usage: Ingenious and ingenuous are often confused. Ingenious means ‘clever, skillful, resourceful’ ( an ingenious device), while ingenuous means ‘artless, frank’ ( charmed by the ingenuous honesty of the child).

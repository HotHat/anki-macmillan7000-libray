mathematics 
maTH(ə)ˈmadiks

▶pl. noun [usually treated as singular] the abstract science of number, quantity, and space. Mathematics may be studied in its own right (pure mathematics), or as it is applied to other disciplines such as physics and engineering (applied mathematics). 
• [often treated as plural] the mathematical aspects of something: the mathematics of general relativity. ORIGIN late 16th century: plural of obsolete mathematic ‘mathematics,’ from Old French mathematique, from Latin (ars) mathematica ‘mathematical (art),’ from Greek mathēmatikē (tekhnē), from the base of manthanein ‘learn.’

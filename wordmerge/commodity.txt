commodity 
kəˈmädədē

▶noun (plural commodities) a raw material or primary agricultural product that can be bought and sold, such as copper or coffee. 
• a useful or valuable thing, such as water or time. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French commodite or Latin commoditas, from commodus (see commodious) .

grant 
BrE ɡrɑːnt, AmE ɡrænt 
A. transitive verb 
① (allow) 准予 zhǔnyǔ ‹favour, interview›; 准许 zhǔnxǔ ‹bail›to grant [somebody] [something]; 将某物准予某人 he granted him his request 他答应了他的要求 to grant [somebody] permission (to do [something]) 允许某人（做某事） permission granted! 同意！ I refused to grant the men access to my home 我拒绝让那些人进我的家门 to grant [somebody] an extension (of time) 准许某人延期 to grant [somebody] one's time 在某人身上花费时间 
② (give formally or legally) 授予 shòuyǔ ‹land, right, power›; 发放 fāfàng ‹visa, pension, compensation›to grant [somebody] asylum 为某人提供政治避难 he has been granted leave of absence 他获准休假 
③ Finance 批准 pīzhǔn ‹loan, overdraft›
④ (concede) 承认 chéngrèn ‹truth, validity, statement›to grant ([somebody]) that …; （向某人）承认… to grant the truth of [something] 承认某事的真实性 he is, I grant you, a convincing witness 我承认，他是个有说服力的证人 
⑤ to take [somebody]/[something] for granted (fail to appreciate) 对…不予重视 duì… bù yǔ zhòngshì ‹person, thing, circumstances›
⑥ to take it for granted that … (assume as true) 理所当然地认为… to take [something] for granted 认为…是理所当然的 rènwéi… shì lǐ suǒ dāng rán de ‹truth, validity, fact›to take too much for granted 过于想当然 guòyú xiǎngdāngrán 
B.  noun 
① (award of money) 拨款 bōkuǎn a grant to do [something]; 做某事的补助金 a grant towards [something]/doing [something]; 某事/做某事的经费 
② Law (of land, property) 让渡 ràngdù a grant of a patent 专利权的转让 a grant of probate 遗嘱检验权的授予

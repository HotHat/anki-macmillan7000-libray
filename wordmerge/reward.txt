reward 
BrE rɪˈwɔːd, AmE rəˈwɔrd 
A. noun 
① Countable and uncountable(recompense) 报偿 bàocháng as a reward for [something]/for doing [something] 作为某事/做某事的奖励 virtue is its own reward 善行本身就是回报 she was given a medal in reward for saving the little girl's life 为奖励她挽救了小姑娘的生命，她获颁一枚奖章 
② Countable(money) 酬金 chóujīn to offer a reward for [something] 为某物提供赏金 
③ figurative (compensation, return) 回报 huíbào to get or receive one's just reward 获得应得的回报 one reward of my job is foreign travel 我工作的一个回报是可以去国外旅行 
B.  transitive verb 
① (recompense) 酬谢 chóuxiè to reward [somebody] for [something]; 因某事物酬谢某人 the finder will be rewarded 寻得者有赏 if you persist, your efforts will be rewarded figurative 如果你坚持不懈，你的努力是不会白费的 
② figurative (merit) 值得 zhídé it's a subject that rewards further investigation 这是一个值得进一步研究的课题 
reward 
▶noun a reward for its safe return: recompense, prize, award, honor, decoration, bonus, premium, bounty, present, gift, payment; informal payoff, perk; formal perquisite.
▶verb they were well rewarded: recompense, pay, remunerate, make something worth someone's while; give an award to. ANTONYMS punish.QUOTE The reward of a thing well done is to have done it.Ralph Waldo Emerson "Nominalist and Realist" (1844)

depart 
BrE dɪˈpɑːt, AmE dəˈpɑrt 

A.  intransitive verb 
① formal (leave) 出发 chūfā to depart from a place 动身离开某地 to depart for a destination 动身去某目的地 
② (deviate) 背离 bèilí you have departed from your original theory 你违背了你最初的理论 to depart from one's usual routine 一改通常的做法 
B.  transitive verb to depart this life literary 去世 qùshì 
depart 
dəˈpärt

▶verb [no object] leave, typically in order to start a journey: they departed for Germany 
a contingent was departing from Cairo. 
• (depart from) deviate from (an accepted, prescribed, or traditional course of action): he departed from the precedent set by many. 
PHRASES
depart this life archaic die.ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French departir, based on Latin dispertire ‘to divide.’ The original sense was ‘separate,’ also ‘take leave of each other,’ hence ‘go away.’

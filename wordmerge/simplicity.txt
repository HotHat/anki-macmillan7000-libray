simplicity 
simˈplisədē

▶noun the quality or condition of being easy to understand or do: for the sake of simplicity, this chapter will concentrate on one theory. 
• the quality or condition of being plain or natural: the grandeur and simplicity of Roman architecture. 
• a thing that is plain, natural, or easy to understand: the simplicities of pastoral living. 
PHRASES
be simplicity itself be extremely easy.ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French simplicite or Latin simplicitas, from simplex (see simplex) .

asylum 
əˈsīləm

▶noun
1 (also political asylum) the protection granted by a nation to someone who has left their native country as a political refugee: she applied for asylum and was granted refugee status. 
• shelter or protection from danger: we provide asylum for those too ill to care for themselves.
2 dated an institution offering shelter and support to people who are mentally ill: he'd been committed to an asylum. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘place of refuge,’ especially for criminals): via Latin from Greek asulon ‘refuge,’ from asulos ‘inviolable,’ from a- ‘without’ + sulon ‘right of seizure.’ The current senses date from the 18th century.

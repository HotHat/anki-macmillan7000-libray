tent

▶noun a portable shelter made of cloth, supported by one or more poles and stretched tight by cords or loops attached to pegs driven into the ground. 
• Medicine short for oxygen tent. 
▶verb
1 [with object] cover with or as if with a tent: the garden had been completely tented over for supper. 
• arrange in a shape that looks like a tent: Tim tented his fingers. 
• (as adjective tented) composed of or provided with tents: they were living in large tented camps.
2 [no object] (especially of traveling circus people) live in a tent. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French tente, based on Latin tent- ‘stretched,’ from the verb tendere. The verb dates from the mid 16th century.

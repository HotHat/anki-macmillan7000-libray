criterion 
ˌkrīˈtirēən

▶noun (plural criteria 
-ˈti(ə)rēə
) a principle or standard by which something may be judged or decided: the launch came too close to violating safety criteria. DERIVATIVES criterial 
-ˈti(ə)rēəl 
adjectiveORIGIN early 17th century: from Greek kritērion ‘means of judging,’ from kritēs (see critic) . usage: Strictly speaking, the singular form (following the original Greek) is criterion and the plural form is criteria. It is a common mistake, however, to use criteria as if it were a singular, as in a further criteria needs to be considered.

shrewd 
SHro͞od

▶adjective
1 having or showing sharp powers of judgment; astute: she was shrewd enough to guess the motive behind his gesture 
a shrewd career move.
2 archaic (especially of weather) piercingly cold: a shrewd east wind. 
• (of a blow) severe: a bayonet's shrewd thrust. 
• mischievous; malicious. ORIGIN Middle English (in the sense ‘evil in nature or character’): from shrew in the sense ‘evil person or thing,’ or as the past participle of obsolete shrew ‘to curse.’ The word developed the sense ‘cunning,’ and gradually gained a favorable connotation during the 17th century.

injure 
BrE ˈɪndʒə, AmE ˈɪndʒər 
transitive verb 
① (hurt) 使受伤 shǐ shòushāng to injure oneself by doing [something]; 做某事使自己受伤 to injure one's arm (playing football) （踢足球时）手臂受伤 
② figurative (damage) 损害 sǔnhài ‹reputation, self-esteem›to injure [somebody's] feelings 伤害某人的感情 
injure 
▶verb
1 he injured his foot: hurt, wound, damage, harm; cripple, lame, disable; maim, mutilate, deform, mangle, break.
2 his comments injured her reputation: damage, mar, impair, spoil, ruin, blight, blemish, tarnish, blacken.
3 dated my actions have injured no one: wrong, abuse, do an injustice to, offend against, maltreat, mistreat, ill-use.

BrE ˈsɪləb(ə)l, AmE ˈsɪləb(ə)l 
noun 音节 yīnjié (to explain [something]) in words of one syllable 用简单明了的字眼（解释某事） 
syllable 
ˈsiləb(ə)l

▶noun a unit of pronunciation having one vowel sound, with or without surrounding consonants, forming the whole or a part of a word; e.g., there are two syllables in water and three in inferno. 
• a character or characters representing a syllable. 
• [usually with negative] the least amount of speech or writing; the least mention of something: I'd never have breathed a syllable if he'd kept quiet. 
▶verb [with object] pronounce (a word or phrase) clearly, syllable by syllable. DERIVATIVES syllabled adjective [usually in combination] : poems of few-syllabled linesORIGIN late Middle English: from an Anglo-Norman French alteration of Old French sillabe, via Latin from Greek sullabē, from sun- ‘together’ + lambanein ‘take.’

spiritual 
ˈspiriCH(o͞o)əl

▶adjective
1 relating to or affecting the human spirit or soul as opposed to material or physical things: I'm responsible for his spiritual welfare 
the spiritual values of life. 
• (of a person) not concerned with material values or pursuits.
2 relating to religion or religious belief: the tribe's spiritual leader. 
▶noun (also Negro spiritual) a religious song of a kind associated with black Christians of the southern US, and thought to derive from the combination of European hymns and African musical elements by black slaves. 
PHRASES
one's spiritual home a place in which one feels a profound sense of belonging: I had always thought of Italy as my spiritual home.ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French spirituel, from Latin spiritualis, from spiritus (see spirit) .

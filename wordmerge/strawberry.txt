strawberry 
ˈstrôˌberē ˈstrôˌb(ə)rē

▶noun (plural strawberries)
1 a sweet soft red fruit with a seed-studded surface.
2 the low-growing plant that produces the strawberry, having white flowers, lobed leaves, and runners, and found throughout north temperate regions. [Genus Fragaria, family Rosaceae; the commercial strawberry is usually 
F.  × ananassa.]
3 a deep pinkish-red color. ORIGIN Old English strēa(w)berige, strēowberige (see straw, berry) .

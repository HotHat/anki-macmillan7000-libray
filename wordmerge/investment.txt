investment 
BrE ɪnˈvɛs(t)m(ə)nt, AmE ɪnˈvɛs(t)mənt 
noun 
① Uncountable(act of investing) 投资 tóuzī he called for more government investment in industry 他呼吁政府对工业增加投资 
② Countable(sum, amount) 投资额 tóuzī'é a good/bad investment 良性/不良投资 
③ Countable(commodity) 投资物 tóuzīwù those antiques were bought as an investment 这些古董是作为投资买下的 
④ Uncountable and countable(commitment) 投入 tóurù the investment of time and energy in [something] 对某事时间和精力的投入 a huge emotional investment 大量的情感投入 
investment 
inˈves(t)mənt

▶noun
1 the action or process of investing money for profit or material result: a debate over private investment in road-building 
a total investment of $50,000. 
• a thing that is worth buying because it may be profitable or useful in the future: a used car is rarely a good investment. 
• an act of devoting time, effort, or energy to a particular undertaking with the expectation of a worthwhile result: the time spent in attending a one-day seminar is an investment in our professional futures.
2 archaic the surrounding of a place by a hostile force in order to besiege or blockade it.

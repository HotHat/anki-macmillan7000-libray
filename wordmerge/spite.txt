spite 
spīt

▶noun a desire to hurt, annoy, or offend someone: he'd think I was saying it out of spite. 
• archaic an instance of a desire to hurt; a grudge: it seemed as if the wind had a spite at her. 
▶verb [with object] deliberately hurt, annoy, or offend (someone): he put the house up for sale to spite his family. 
PHRASES
in spite of without being affected by the particular factor mentioned: he was suddenly cold in spite of the sun. in spite of oneself although one did not want or expect to do so: Oliver smiled in spite of himself.ORIGIN Middle English: shortening of Old French despit ‘contempt,’ despiter ‘show contempt for.’

disappoint 
ˌdisəˈpoint

▶verb [with object] fail to fulfill the hopes or expectations of (someone): I have no wish to disappoint everyone by postponing the visit. 
• prevent (hopes or expectations) from being realized: to disappoint the hopes that are now awakened could be very dangerous. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘deprive of an office or position’): from Old French desappointer .

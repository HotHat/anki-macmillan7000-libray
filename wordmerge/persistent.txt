persistent 
pərˈsist(ə)nt

▶adjective
1 continuing firmly or obstinately in a course of action in spite of difficulty or opposition: one of the government's most persistent critics.
2 continuing to exist or endure over a prolonged period: persistent rain will affect many areas 
persistent reports of human rights abuses by the military. 
• (of a chemical or radioactivity) remaining within the environment for a long time after its introduction: PCBs are persistent environmental contaminants.
3 Botany & Zoology (of a part of an animal or plant, such as a horn, leaf, etc.) remaining attached instead of falling off in the normal manner.

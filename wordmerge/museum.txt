museum 
myo͞oˈzēəm

▶noun a building in which objects of historical, scientific, artistic, or cultural interest are stored and exhibited. ORIGIN early 17th century (denoting a university building, specifically one erected at Alexandria by Ptolemy Soter): via Latin from Greek mouseion ‘seat of the Muses,’ based on mousa ‘muse.’

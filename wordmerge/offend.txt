offend 
əˈfend

▶verb
1 [with object] cause to feel upset, annoyed, or resentful: viewers said they had been offended by bad language. 
• be displeasing to: he didn't smoke and the smell of ash offended him 
(as adjective offending) : they must redesign the offending section of road.
2 [no object] commit an illegal act: a small hard core of young criminals who offend again and again. 
• break a commonly accepted rule or principle: those activities which offend against public order and decency. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French offendre, from Latin offendere ‘strike against.’

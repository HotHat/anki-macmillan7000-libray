critically 
ˈkridəklē

▶adverb
1 in a way that expresses disapproval: a designer spoke critically of capitalism.
2 in a way that expresses or involves an analysis of the merits and faults of a work of literature, music, or art: a critically acclaimed novel. 
• in a way that involves the objective analysis and evaluation of an issue in order to form a judgment: the reader must evaluate critically every article he or she reviews.
3 [as submodifier] to a potentially disastrous degree: the critically endangered Hawaiian monk seal. 
• [as submodifier] to an extreme degree with a risk of death: two critically wounded people died in the hospital 
critically ill patients who were awaiting surgery. 
• with decisive or crucial importance in the success, failure, or existence of something: the livelihood of rural people critically depends on the use of natural resources.

portable 
ˈpôrdəb(ə)l

▶adjective able to be easily carried or moved, especially because of being a lighter and smaller version than usual: a portable television. 
• Computing (of software) able to be transferred from one machine or system to another. 
• (of a loan or pension) capable of being transferred or adapted in altered circumstances. 
▶noun a version of something, such as a small lightweight television or computer, that can be easily carried. 
• North American a small transportable building used as a classroom. DERIVATIVES portably 
-blē 
adverbORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French portable, from late Latin portabilis, from Latin portare ‘carry.’

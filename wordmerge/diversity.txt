diversity 
dəˈvərsədē dīˈvərsədē

▶noun (plural diversities) the state of being diverse; variety: there was considerable diversity in the style of the reports. 
• [usually in singular] a range of different things: newspapers were obliged to allow a diversity of views to be printed. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French diversite, from Latin diversitas, from diversus ‘diverse,’ past participle of divertere ‘turn aside’ (see divert) .

injection 
inˈjekSH(ə)n

▶noun
1 an instance of injecting or being injected: painkilling injections 
an injection of capital was needed. 
• a thing that is injected: a morphine injection. 
• the action of injecting: sometimes a polio vaccine is given by injection. 
• short for fuel injection.
2 the entry or placing of a spacecraft or other object into an orbit or trajectory. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin injectio(n-), from the verb inicere (see inject) .

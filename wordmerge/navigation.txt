navigation 
ˌnavəˈɡāSH(ə)n

▶noun
1 the process or activity of accurately ascertaining one's position and planning and following a route.
2 the passage of ships: bridges to span rivers without hindering navigation. ORIGIN early 16th century (denoting the action of traveling on water): from French, or from Latin navigatio(n-), from the verb navigare (see navigate) .

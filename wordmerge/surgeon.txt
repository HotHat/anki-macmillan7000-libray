surgeon 
ˈsərjən

▶noun a medical practitioner qualified to practice surgery. ORIGIN Middle English: from Anglo-Norman French surgien, contraction of Old French serurgien, based on Latin chirurgia, from Greek kheirourgia ‘handiwork, surgery,’ from kheir ‘hand’ + ergon ‘work.’

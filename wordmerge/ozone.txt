ozone 
ˈōˌzōn

▶noun a colorless unstable toxic gas with a pungent odor and powerful oxidizing properties, formed from oxygen by electrical discharges or ultraviolet light. It differs from normal oxygen (O 2) in having three atoms in its molecule (O 3). 
• short for ozone layer. 
• informal fresh invigorating air, especially that blowing onto the shore from the sea. DERIVATIVES ozonic 
ōˈzänik 
adjectiveORIGIN mid 19th century: from German Ozon, from Greek ozein ‘to smell.’

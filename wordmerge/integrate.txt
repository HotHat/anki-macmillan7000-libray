integrate 
ˈin(t)əˌɡrāt

▶verb [with object]
1 combine (one thing) with another so that they become a whole: transportation planning should be integrated with energy policy. 
• combine (two things) so that they become a whole: the problem of integrating the two approaches. 
• [no object] (of a thing) combine with another to form a whole: the stone will blend with the environment and integrate into the landscape.
2 bring (people or groups with particular characteristics or needs) into equal participation in or membership of a social group or institution: integrating children with special needs into ordinary schools. 
• [no object] come into equal participation in or membership of society or an institution or body: she was anxious to integrate well into her husband's family. 
• desegregate (a school, neighborhood, etc.), especially racially: there was a national campaign under way to integrate the lunch counters 
[no object] : cities' efforts to integrate across urban-suburban lines.
3 Mathematics find the integral of. DERIVATIVES integrability

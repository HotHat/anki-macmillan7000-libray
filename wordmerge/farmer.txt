farmer 
ˈfärmər

▶noun
1 a person who owns or manages a farm.
2 historical a person to whom the collection of taxes was contracted for a fee. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French fermier, from medieval Latin firmarius, from firma (see farm). Sense
1 originally denoted a bailiff or steward who farmed land on the owner's behalf, or a tenant farmer.

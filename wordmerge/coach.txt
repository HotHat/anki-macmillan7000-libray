▶noun
1 a horse-drawn carriage, especially a closed one.
2 a railroad car. 
• North American (also coach class) economy class seating in an aircraft or train: a transatlantic flight in coach isn't the most pleasant experience 
the cheapest coach-class fare.
3 a bus, especially one that is comfortably equipped and used for longer journeys. 
▶verb [no object] travel by coach: they coached to Claude's dwelling. 
▶adverb North American in economy class accommodations in an aircraft or train: flying coach. ORIGIN mid 16th century ( sense
1 of the noun): from French coche, from Hungarian kocsi (szekér) ‘(wagon) from Kocs,’ a town in Hungary.

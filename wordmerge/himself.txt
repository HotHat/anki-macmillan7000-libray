himself 
himˈself

▶pronoun [third person singular]
1 [reflexive] used as the object of a verb or preposition to refer to a male person or animal previously mentioned as the subject of the clause: the steward introduced himself as Pete 
he ought to be ashamed of himself.
2 [emphatic] he or him personally (used to emphasize a particular male person or animal mentioned): Thomas himself laid down what we should do 
he said so himself. 
• chiefly Irish a third party of some importance, especially the master of the house: I'll mention it to himself.

hobby
1 
ˈhäbē

▶noun (plural hobbies)
1 an activity done regularly in one's leisure time for pleasure: her hobbies are reading and gardening.
2 archaic a small horse or pony. ORIGIN late Middle English hobyn, hoby, from nicknames for the given name Robin. Originally sense
2 (compare with dobbin), it later came to denote a toy horse or hobbyhorse, hence ‘a pastime, something done for pleasure.’

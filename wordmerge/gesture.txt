gesture 
ˈjesCHər

▶noun a movement of part of the body, especially a hand or the head, to express an idea or meaning: Alex made a gesture of apology 
so much is conveyed by gesture. 
• an action performed to convey one's feelings or intentions: Maggie was touched by the kind gesture. 
• an action performed for show in the knowledge that it will have no effect: I hope the amendment will not be just a gesture. 
▶verb [no object] make a gesture: she gestured meaningfully with the pistol. 
• [with object] express (something) with a gesture or gestures: he gestured his dissent at this. 
• [with object or infinitive] direct or invite (someone) to move somewhere specified: he gestured her to a chair. DERIVATIVES gestural 
ˈjesCH(ə)rəl 
adjectiveORIGIN late Middle English: from medieval Latin gestura, from Latin gerere ‘bear, wield, perform.’ The original sense was ‘bearing, deportment,’ hence ‘the use of posture and bodily movements for effect in oratory.’

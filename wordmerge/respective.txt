respective 
rəˈspektiv

▶adjective [attributive] belonging or relating separately to each of two or more people or things: they chatted about their respective childhoods. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘relative, comparative’): from medieval Latin respectivus, from respect- ‘regarded, considered,’ from the verb respicere (see respect), reinforced by French respectif, -ive .

geometry 
jēˈämətrē

▶noun the branch of mathematics concerned with the properties and relations of points, lines, surfaces, solids, and higher dimensional analogs. 
• (plural geometries) a particular system of geometry: non-Euclidean geometries. 
• [in singular] the shape and relative arrangement of the parts of something: the geometry of spiders' webs. ORIGIN Middle English: via Old French from Latin geometria, from Greek, from gē ‘earth’ + metria (see -metry) .

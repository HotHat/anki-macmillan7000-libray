avenue 
ˈavəˌn(y)o͞o

▶noun
1 a broad road in a town or city, typically having trees at regular intervals along its sides: tree-lined avenues surround the hotel 
[in names] : Euclid Avenue. 
• [in names] North American a thoroughfare running at right angles to the streets in a city laid out on a grid pattern: 7th Avenue. 
• a tree-lined road or path, especially one that leads to a country house or similar building: an avenue of limes.
2 a way of approaching a problem or making progress toward something: three possible avenues of research suggested themselves. ORIGIN early 17th century ( sense 2): from French, feminine past participle of avenir ‘arrive, approach,’ from Latin advenire, from ad- ‘toward’ + venire ‘come.’

invisible 
BrE ɪnˈvɪzɪb(ə)l, AmE ɪnˈvɪzəb(ə)l 
adjective 
① (not visible) 看不见的 kànbujiàn de invisible to the naked eye; 肉眼看不见的 the fairy made herself invisible 仙子隐身了 
② (concealed) 隐藏的 yǐncáng de ‹object, landmark›the tower was invisible because of the fog 那座塔隐没在雾中 
③ figurative (ignored) 被忽视的 bèi hūshì de 
④ Economics 无形的 wúxíng de ‹earnings, trade›
invisible 
inˈvizəb(ə)l

▶adjective unable to be seen; not visible to the eye: this invisible gas is present to some extent in every home. 
• concealed from sight; hidden: he lounged in a doorway, invisible in the dark. 
• treated as if unable to be seen; ignored or not taken into consideration: before 1971, women artists were pretty well invisible. 
• Economics relating to or denoting earnings that a country makes from the sale of services or other items not constituting tangible commodities: tourism is the most important of our invisible exports. 
▶noun (invisibles) invisible exports and imports. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French, or from Latin invisibilis, from in- ‘not’ + visibilis (see visible) .

examine 
BrE ɪɡˈzamɪn, ɛɡˈzamɪn, AmE ɪɡˈzæmən 
transitive verb 
① (inspect physically) 检查 jiǎnchá ‹patient, part of body, equipment, passport›
② (inspect intellectually) 调查 diàochá ‹facts, problem, evidence›; 审查 shěnchá ‹documents, accounts›
③ School, University 对…进行考试 duì… jìnxíng kǎoshì ‹candidate, student›to examine [somebody] in or on [something]; 测试某人某课程 
④ Law 审问 shěnwèn ‹accused, suspect›; 调查 diàochá ‹case›
examine 
▶verb
1 they examined the bank records: inspect, scrutinize, investigate, look at, study, scan, sift (through), probe, appraise, analyze, review, evaluate, survey; informal check out.
2 students were examined after a year: test, quiz, question; assess, appraise.
3 Law name the witnesses to be examined: interrogate, question, quiz, cross-examine; catechize, give the third degree to, probe, sound out; informal grill, pump.

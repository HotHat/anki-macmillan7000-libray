treasury 
ˈtreZH(ə)rē

▶noun (plural treasuries)
1 the funds or revenue of a government, corporation, or institution: the country's pledge not to spend more than it has in its treasury. 
• (Treasury) (in some countries) the government department responsible for budgeting for and controlling public expenditure, management of the national debt, and the overall management of the economy.
2 a place or building where treasure is stored. 
• a store or collection of valuable or delightful things: the old town is a treasury of ancient monuments. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French tresorie (see treasure) .

intake 
ˈinˌtāk

▶noun
1 an amount of food, air, or another substance taken into the body: your daily intake of calories 
his alcohol intake. 
• an act of taking something into the body: she heard his sharp intake of breath 
a protective factor is the intake of cereal fiber.
2 a location or structure through which something is taken in, e.g., water into a channel or pipe from a river, fuel or air into an engine or machine, commodities into a place, etc.: cut rectangular holes for the air intake. 
• the action of taking something in: facilities for the intake of grain by road. ORIGIN Middle English (originally Scots and northern English): from in + take .

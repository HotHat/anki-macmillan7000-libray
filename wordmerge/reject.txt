reject 
A.  /BrE rɪˈdʒɛkt, AmE rəˈdʒɛkt
transitive verb 
① (turn down) 驳回 bóhuí ‹plea, bid, amendment›; 拒收 jùshōu ‹gift, damaged goods›; 拒绝 jùjué ‹offer, applicant, suggestion›
② (spurn) 嫌弃 xiánqì ‹child›rejected by his friends, he fell into despair 由于遭到朋友们的嫌弃，他陷入了绝望 
③ Medicine 排斥 páichì ‹transplant›
④ (not accept) «machine, computer» 不接受 bù jiēshòu ‹coin, data›
B.  /BrE ˈriːdʒɛkt, AmE ˈriˌdʒɛkt
noun 
① (person) 被拒者 bèijùzhě he sees himself as a social reject 他把自己看成是社会的弃儿 
② Busn (product) 次品 cìpǐn reject goods/china 次品/有瑕疵的瓷器 
reject 
▶verb
1 the loggers rejected the offer: turn down, refuse, decline, say no to, spurn; informal give the thumbs down to. ANTONYMS accept.
2 Jamie rejected her: rebuff, spurn, shun, snub, repudiate, cast off/aside, discard, abandon, desert, turn one's back on, have nothing (more) to do with, wash one's hands of; informal give someone the brush-off; literary forsake. ANTONYMS welcome.
▶noun
1 a bin of factory rejects: substandard article, discard, second.
2 what a reject! failure, loser, incompetent.

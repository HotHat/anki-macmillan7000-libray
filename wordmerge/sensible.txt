sensible 
ˈsensəb(ə)l

▶adjective
1 (of a statement or course of action) chosen in accordance with wisdom or prudence; likely to be of benefit: I cannot believe that it is sensible to spend so much 
a sensible diet. 
• (of a person) possessing or displaying prudence: he was a sensible and capable boy. 
• (of an object) practical and functional rather than decorative: Mom always made me have sensible shoes.
2 archaic readily perceived; appreciable: it will effect a sensible reduction in these figures. 
• [predicative] (sensible of/to) able to notice or appreciate; not unaware of: we are sensible of the difficulties he faces. DERIVATIVES sensibleness 
ˈsensəbəlnəs

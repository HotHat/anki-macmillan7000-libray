proceed 
BrE prəˈsiːd, AmE prəˈsid, proʊˈsid 
A. intransitive verb 
① (continue) 继续 jìxù before we proceed any further (at meeting) 在我们继续下一议项之前 (in middle of speech) 在我们谈下一点之前 please proceed formal 请继续 to proceed to [something]; 接下来处理 ‹item, problem›to proceed with [something]; 继续 jìxù ‹plan, sale› Politics 继续进行 jìxù jìnxíng ‹election› Law 继续审理 jìxù shěnlǐ ‹case›to proceed to do [something]; 接下来做某事 
② (progress) «action, project» 进展 jìnzhǎn ; «interview, talk» 继续 jìxù the trial is proceeding 审理在进行中 
③ formal (move) 前行 qiánxíng to proceed towards/along … 前往…/沿…前行 to proceed to … 前行到… they proceeded on their way 他们继续行进 proceed with care or caution! 一路小心！

⑤ formal (issue, result) to proceed from [something]; «smell» 来自某物 «power» 源自某事物 yuánzì mǒu shìwù «evil» 由某事物引起 yóu mǒu shìwù yǐnqǐ

proceed 
▶verb
1 she was uncertain how to proceed: begin, make a start, get going, move, set something in motion; take action, act, go on, go ahead, make progress, make headway. ANTONYMS stop.
2 he proceeded down the road: go, make one's way, advance, move, progress, carry on, press on, push on. ANTONYMS stop.
3 we should proceed with the talks: go ahead, carry on, go on, continue, keep on, get on, get ahead; (proceed with) pursue, prosecute.
4 there is not enough evidence to proceed against him: take someone to court, start/take proceedings against, start an action against, make a case against, sue.
5 all power proceeds from God: originate, spring, stem, come, derive, arise, issue, flow, emanate.

cottage 
ˈkädij

▶noun a small simple house, typically one near a lake or beach. 
• a dwelling forming part of a farm establishment, used by a worker: farm cottages. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Anglo-Norman French cotage and Anglo-Latin cotagium, from cot2 or cote .

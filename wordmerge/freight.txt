freight 
frāt

▶noun
1 goods transported by truck, train, ship, or aircraft. 
• the transport of goods by truck, train, ship, or aircraft. 
• a charge for transport by freight.
2 (in full freight train) a train of freight cars: sugar and molasses moving by freight.
3 a load or burden. 
▶verb [with object] transport (goods) in bulk by truck, train, ship, or aircraft: the metals had been freighted from the city 
[no object] : ships freighting to Dublin. 
• (be freighted with) be laden or imbued with (something abstract): each word was freighted with anger. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘rental of a ship for transporting goods’): from Middle Dutch, Middle Low German vrecht, variant of vracht ‘ship's cargo.’ Compare with fraught .

aggression 
əˈɡreSHən

▶noun hostile or violent behavior or attitudes toward another; readiness to attack or confront: his chin was jutting with aggression 
territorial aggression between individuals of the same species. 
• the action or an act of attacking without provocation: he called for an end to foreign aggression against his country 
the president has been emboldened by the success of his latest aggressions. 
• forceful and sometimes overly assertive pursuit of one's aims and interests. ORIGIN early 17th century (in the sense ‘an attack’): from Latin aggressio(n-), from aggredi ‘to attack,’ from ad- ‘toward’ + gradi ‘proceed, walk.’

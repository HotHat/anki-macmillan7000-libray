insufficient 
ˌinsəˈfiSH(ə)nt

▶adjective not enough; inadequate: there was insufficient evidence to convict him. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘incapable, incompetent’): via Old French from late Latin insufficient- ‘not sufficing,’ from in- ‘not’ + Latin sufficere (see suffice) .

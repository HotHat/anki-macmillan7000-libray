credibility 
ˌkredəˈbilədē

▶noun the quality of being trusted and believed in: the government's loss of credibility. 
• the quality of being convincing or believable: the book's anecdotes have scant regard for credibility. 
• another term for street credibility. ORIGIN mid 16th century: from medieval Latin credibilitas, from Latin credibilis (see credible) .

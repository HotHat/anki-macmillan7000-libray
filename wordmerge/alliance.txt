alliance 
əˈlīəns

▶noun a union or association formed for mutual benefit, especially between countries or organizations: a defensive alliance between Australia and New Zealand 
divisions within the alliance. 
• a relationship based on an affinity in interests, nature, or qualities: an alliance between medicine and morality. 
• a state of being joined or associated: his party is in alliance with the Greens. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French aliance, from aliere ‘to ally’ (see ally1) .

flexible 
ˈfleksəb(ə)l

▶adjective capable of bending easily without breaking: flexible rubber seals. 
• able to be easily modified to respond to altered circumstances or conditions: flexible forms of retirement. 
• (of a person) ready and able to change so as to adapt to different circumstances: you can save money if you're flexible about where your room is located. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French, or from Latin flexibilis, from flectere ‘to bend.’

liter 
ˈlēdər 
(British litre) (abbreviation l) 
▶noun a metric unit of capacity, formerly defined as the volume of
1 kilogram of water under standard conditions, now equal to 1,000 cubic centimeters (about 1.75 pints). ORIGIN late 18th century: from French litre, alteration of litron (an obsolete measure of capacity), via medieval Latin from Greek litra, a Sicilian monetary unit.

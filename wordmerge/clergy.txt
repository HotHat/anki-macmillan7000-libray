clergy 
ˈklərjē

▶noun (plural clergies) [usually treated as plural] the body of all people ordained for religious duties, especially in the Christian Church: all marriages were to be solemnized by the clergy. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French, based on ecclesiastical Latin clericus ‘clergyman’ (see cleric) .

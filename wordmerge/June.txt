June 
jo͞on

▶noun the sixth month of the year, in the northern hemisphere usually considered the first month of summer: the roses flower in June 
[as modifier] : a June afternoon. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French juin, from Latin Junius (mensis)‘(month) of June,’ variant of Junonius ‘sacred to Juno.’

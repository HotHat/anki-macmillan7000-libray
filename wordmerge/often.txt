often 
ˈôf(t)ən ˈäf(t)ən

▶adverb (oftener, oftenest) frequently; many times: he often goes for long walks by himself 
how often do you have your hair cut? 
• in many instances: vocabulary often reflects social standing. 
PHRASES
as often as not quite frequently or commonly: I had two homes really, because as often as not I was down at her house. more often than not usually: food is scarce and more often than not they go hungry.ORIGIN Middle English: extended form of oft, probably influenced by selden ‘seldom.’ Early examples appear to be northern English; the word became general in the 16th century. usage: When pronouncing often, some speakers sound the t, saying 
ˈôftən
; for others, it is silent, as in soften, fasten, listen . Either pronunciation is acceptable, although 
ˈôfən
is more common.

amendment 
əˈmen(d)mənt

▶noun a minor change in a document. 
• a change or addition to a legal or statutory document: an amendment to existing bail laws. 
• (Amendment) an article added to the US Constitution: the First Amendment. 
• something that is added to soil in order to improve its texture or fertility. ORIGIN Middle English (in the sense ‘improvement, correction’): from Old French amendement, from amender (see amend) .

coalition 
ˌkōəˈliSH(ə)n

▶noun an alliance for combined action, especially a temporary alliance of political parties forming a government or of states: a coalition of conservatives and disaffected Democrats 
the party was only able to govern in coalition with three or even four other parties 
[as modifier] : a coalition government. DERIVATIVES coalitionist 
ˌkōəˈliSH(ə)nəst 
nounORIGIN early 17th century (in the sense ‘fusion’): from medieval Latin coalitio(n-), from the verb coalescere (see coalesce). Usage in politics dates from the late 18th century.

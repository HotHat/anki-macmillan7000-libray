inspect 
inˈspekt

▶verb [with object] look at (someone or something) closely, typically to assess their condition or to discover any shortcomings: they were inspecting my outside paintwork for cracks and flaws. 
• examine (someone or something) to ensure that they reach an official standard: customs officers came aboard to inspect our documents. ORIGIN early 17th century (earlier (late Middle English) as inspection): from Latin inspect- ‘looked into, examined,’ from the verb inspicere (from in- ‘in’ + specere ‘look at’), or from its frequentative, inspectare .

recede 
BrE rɪˈsiːd, AmE rəˈsid 
intransitive verb 
① (retreat, go back) «tide, footsteps» 后退 hòutuì the flood waters receded 洪水退去了 
② (disappear from view) «lights, person, coast» 渐渐远去 jiànjiàn yuǎn qù to recede into the distance 消失在远处 
③ (slope backwards) 向后倾斜 xiàng hòu qīngxié a receding chin/forehead 向后缩的下巴/发际线后移的前额 
④ figurative (fade) «memory, features» 变得模糊 biàn de móhu ; «hopes» 变得渺茫 biàn de miǎománg ; «threat, danger» 逐渐减弱 zhújiàn jiǎnruò prospects of a reconciliation are receding 和解的前景越来越渺茫 
⑤ «hair, hairline» 变秃 biàntū 
recede 
▶verb
1 the floodwaters receded: retreat, go back, go down, move back, move away, withdraw, ebb, subside, abate. ANTONYMS advance, approach.
2 the lights receded into the distance: disappear from view, fade, be lost to view, pass from sight.
3 fears of violence have receded: diminish, lessen, decrease, dwindle, fade, abate, subside, ebb, wane. ANTONYMS intensify, grow.

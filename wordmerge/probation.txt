probation 
prōˈbāSH(ə)n

▶noun Law the release of an offender from detention, subject to a period of good behavior under supervision: I went to court and was put on probation. 
• the process or period of testing or observing the character or abilities of a person in a certain role, for example, a new employee: for an initial period of probation, your manager will closely monitor your progress. ORIGIN late Middle English (denoting testing, investigation, or examination): from Old French probacion, from Latin probatio(n-), from probare ‘to test, prove’ (see prove). The legal use dates from the late 19th century.

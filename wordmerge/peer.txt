peer
1 
pir

▶verb [no object] look keenly or with difficulty at someone or something: Blake screwed up his eyes, trying to peer through the fog. 
• be just visible: the two towers peer over the roofs. 
• archaic come into view; appear. ORIGIN late 16th century: perhaps a variant of dialect pire; perhaps partly from a shortening of appear .

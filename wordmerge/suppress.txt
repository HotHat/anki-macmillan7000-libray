suppress 
səˈpres

▶verb [with object] forcibly put an end to: the uprising was savagely suppressed. 
• prevent the development, action, or expression of (a feeling, impulse, idea, etc.); restrain: she could not suppress a rising panic. 
• prevent the dissemination of (information): the report had been suppressed. 
• prevent or inhibit (a process or reaction): use of the drug suppressed the immune response. 
• partly or wholly eliminate (electrical interference). 
• Psychoanalysis consciously inhibit (an unpleasant idea or memory) to avoid considering it. DERIVATIVES suppressible 
səˈpresəb(ə)l 
adjective. suppressive 
səˈpresiv 
adjectiveORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin suppress- ‘pressed down,’ from the verb supprimere, from sub- ‘down’ + premere ‘to press.’

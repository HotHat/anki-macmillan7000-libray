recover 
BrE rɪˈkʌvə, AmE rɪˈkəvər 
A. transitive verb 
① (get back) 追回 zhuīhuí ‹document, jewellery, vehicle, debt›; 收复 shōufù ‹territory›they recovered the car from the river 他们从河里把汽车捞上来了 
② (regain) 恢复 huīfù ‹faculties, health, sight›to recover one's confidence/strength/breath 恢复信心/体力/正常呼吸 to recover consciousness 苏醒过来 
③ (recoup) 挽回 wǎnhuí ‹expenses, loss, time›the right to recover damages 获得损害赔偿的权利 
④ (reclaim for use) 利用 lìyòng ‹acres, area›to recover land from the sea 填海造地 
⑤ (regain control of) 重新获得 chóngxīn huòdé ‹balance, peace of mind›to recover one's composure, to recover oneself 使自己平静下来 
⑥ (retrieve) 回收 huíshōu ‹space capsule›; 找回 zhǎohuí ‹body›
B.  intransitive verb 
① (recuperate) «person» 康复 kāngfù to recover from …; 从…中康复 he fell into a coma and did not recover 他昏迷后再也没醒过来 
② (regain equilibrium) «person, family, community» 恢复正常 huīfù zhèngcháng to recover from …; 从…中恢复正常 
③ (regain financial strength) «economy, stock market» 复苏 fùsū ; «currency, shares» 回升 huíshēng 
recover 
▶verb
1 he's recovering from a heart attack: recuperate, get better, convalesce, regain one's strength, get stronger, get back on one's feet; be on the mend, be on the road to recovery, pick up, rally, respond to treatment, improve, heal, pull through, bounce back. ANTONYMS deteriorate.
2 later, shares recovered: rally, improve, pick up, make a recovery, rebound, bounce back.
3 the stolen material has been recovered: retrieve, regain (possession of), get back, recoup, reclaim, repossess, redeem, recuperate, find (again), track down. ANTONYMS lose.
4 gold coins recovered from a wreck: salvage, save, rescue, retrieve.
PHRASES
recover oneself as nervous as she was, she convincingly recovered herself: pull oneself together, regain one's composure, regain one's self-control; informal get a grip (on oneself).

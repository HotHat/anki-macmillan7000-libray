nickname 
ˈnikˌnām

▶noun a familiar or humorous name given to a person or thing instead of or as well as the real name. 
▶verb [with object and complement] give a nickname to; call by a nickname: his fraternity brothers nicknamed him “The Bird” because of his skydiving skills. ORIGIN late Middle English: from an eke-name (eke meaning ‘addition’: see eke2), misinterpreted, by wrong division, as a neke name.

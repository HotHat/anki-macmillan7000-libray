seldom 
ˈseldəm

▶adverb not often; rarely: Islay is seldom visited by tourists 
he was seldom absent 
[in combination] : an old seldom-used church. 
▶adjective [attributive] dated not common; infrequent: a great but seldom pleasure. ORIGIN Old English seldan, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch zelden and German selten, from a base meaning ‘strange, wonderful.’

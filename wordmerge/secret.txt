secret 
ˈsēkrit

▶adjective not known or seen or not meant to be known or seen by others: how did you guess I had a secret plan? 
the resupply effort was probably kept secret from Congress. 
• [attributive] not meant to be known as such by others: a secret drinker. 
• fond of or good at keeping things about oneself unknown: he can be the most secret man. 
• US (of information or documents) given the security classification above confidential and below top secret. 
▶noun something that is kept or meant to be kept unknown or unseen by others: a state secret 
at first I tried to keep it a secret from my wife. 
• something that is not properly understood; a mystery: I'm not trying to explain the secrets of the universe in this book. 
• a valid but not commonly known or recognized method of achieving or maintaining something: the secret of a happy marriage is compromise. 
• formerly, the name of a prayer said by the priest in a low voice after the offertory in a Roman Catholic Mass. 
PHRASES
be in on the secret be among the few people who know something. in secret without others knowing. make no secret of something make something perfectly clear.ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French, from Latin secretus (adjective) ‘separate, set apart,’ from the verb secernere, from se- ‘apart’ + cernere ‘sift.’

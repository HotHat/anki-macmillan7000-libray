cruise 
BrE kruːz, AmE kruz 
A. intransitive verb 
① (sail for pleasure) 乘船游览 chéng chuán yóulǎn 
② (travel slowly) 平稳行驶 píngwěn xíngshǐ to cruise at 10,000 metres/at 500 km/h 在1万米高空/以每小时500公里的速度巡航 
③ especially Sport colloquial (do easily) 轻松获取 qīngsōng huòqǔ to cruise to victory/into first place 轻松获得胜利/第一名 to cruise through an exam 轻而易举通过考试 
④ (travel around unhurriedly) 巡行 xúnxíng (travel around looking for sth.) 徘徊寻觅 páihuái xúnmì a police car cruised past us 一辆巡逻警车从我们身边驶过 taxis cruised about, hoping to pick up fares 出租汽车四处寻觅乘客 
⑤ colloquial «homosexual» 寻觅性伙伴 xúnmìxìng huǒbàn 
B.  transitive verb 
① (sail for pleasure) 乘船游览 chéng chuán yóulǎn 
② (travel slowly) 平稳行驶于 píngwěn xíngshǐ yú ‹sea, oceans, skies›to cruise a sea/river 在海/河上平稳航行 
③ (travel around unhurriedly) «person» 徘徊于 páihuái yú ; «car» 巡行于 xúnxíng yú the teenagers were aimlessly cruising the mall 这些少年漫无目的地在购物中心闲逛 a police car was cruising the streets 一辆警车在街上巡逻 
④ (travel around looking for sth.) «police car, vigilantes, teenagers in cars, taxi» 在…徘徊寻觅 zài… páihuái xúnmì ‹streets, city, area›
⑤ colloquial (homosexual) 在…寻觅性伙伴 zài… xúnmìxìng huǒbàn ‹bars, area›
C.  noun 乘船游览 chéng chuán yóulǎn to be/go on a cruise 在/去乘船游览 
cruise 
▶noun a cruise to the islands: boat trip, sea trip; voyage, journey.
▶verb
1 she cruised across the Atlantic: sail, voyage, journey.
2 a taxi cruised past: drive slowly, drift; informal mosey, toodle.

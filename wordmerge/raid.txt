raid 
BrE reɪd, AmE reɪd 
A. noun 
① Military (by soldiers, ships) 突袭 tūxí (by aircraft) 空袭 kōngxí a cross-border raid by guerrillas 游击队的越境突袭 a raid on [something]; 对…的突袭 ‹town, outpost, base›to carry out or make a raid (on [something]) （对某物）实施袭击 
② (surprise swoop) 突击搜查 tūjī sōuchá a raid on [something]; 对…的突击搜查 ‹house, club, casino›to carry out or make a raid (on [something]) 突然搜查（某物） 
③ (robbery) 抢劫 qiǎngjié a raid on [something]; 对…的抢劫 ‹bank, shop, post office›to carry out or make a raid (on [something]) （对某处）实施抢劫 
④ Finance 恶意收购 èyì shōugòu a raid on [something]; 对…的恶意收购 ‹company, business›
B.  transitive verb 
① Military «soldiers, ships» 突袭 tūxí ; «aircraft, pilots» 空袭 kōngxí 
② (swoop on) «police, customs officers» 突击搜查 tūjī sōuchá ‹premises›
③ (rob) 抢劫 qiǎngjié ‹bank›
④ figurative (remove items from) 洗劫 xǐjié ‹piggy bank, cash box›; 清空 qīngkōng ‹fridge, cupboard›
⑤ Finance (take illicitly) 侵吞 qīntūn ‹fund, reserves›(use illicitly) 挪用 nuóyòng ‹fund, reserves›
raid 
▶noun
1 the raid on Dieppe: attack, assault, descent, blitz, incursion, sortie; onslaught, storming, charge, offensive, invasion, blitzkrieg.
2 a police raid: search; informal bust, takedown, shakedown.
▶verb
1 they raided shipping in the harbor: attack, assault, set upon, descend on, swoop on, blitz, assail, storm, rush.
2 armed men raided the store: rob, hold up, break into; plunder, steal from, pillage, loot, ransack, sack; informal stick up, heist.

philosopher 
fəˈläsəfər

▶noun a person engaged or learned in philosophy, especially as an academic discipline. ORIGIN Middle English: from a variant of Old French philosophe, via Latin from Greek philosophos ‘lover of wisdom,’ from philein ‘to love’ + sophos ‘wise.’

predecessor 
ˈpredəˌsesər ˈprēdəˌsesər

▶noun a person who held a job or office before the current holder: the new president's foreign policy is very similar to that of his predecessor. 
• a thing that has been followed or replaced by another: the chapel was built in 1864 on the site of its predecessor. ORIGIN late Middle English: from late Latin praedecessor, from Latin prae ‘beforehand’ + decessor ‘retiring officer’ (from decedere ‘depart’).

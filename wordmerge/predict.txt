predict 
prəˈdikt

▶verb [with object] say or estimate that (a specified thing) will happen in the future or will be a consequence of something: it is too early to predict a result 
[with clause] : he predicts that the trend will continue 
(as adjective predicted) : the predicted growth is 47 percent. ORIGIN early 17th century: from Latin praedict- ‘made known beforehand, declared,’ from the verb praedicere, from prae- ‘beforehand’ + dicere ‘say.’

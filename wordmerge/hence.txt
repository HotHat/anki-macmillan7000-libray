hence 
hens

▶adverb
1 as a consequence; for this reason: a stiff breeze and hence a high windchill.
2 in the future (used after a period of time): two years hence they might say something quite different.
3 (also from hence) archaic from here: hence, be gone. ORIGIN Middle English hennes ( sense 3): from earlier henne (from Old English heonan, of Germanic origin, related to he) + -s3 (later respelled -ce to denote the unvoiced sound).

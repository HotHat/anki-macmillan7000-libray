format 
ˈfôrˌmat

▶noun the way in which something is arranged or set out: the format of the funeral service. 
• the shape, size, and presentation of a book or periodical. 
• the medium in which a sound recording is made available: the album is available as a CD as well as on LP and cassette formats. 
• Computing a defined structure for the processing, storage, or display of data: a data file in binary format. 
▶verb (formats, formatting, formatted) [with object] (especially in computing) arrange or put into a format. 
• prepare (a storage medium) to receive data. ORIGIN mid 19th century: via French and German from Latin formatus (liber)‘shaped (book),’ past participle of formare ‘to form.’

rice 
rīs

▶noun a swamp grass that is widely cultivated as a source of food, especially in Asia. [Oryza sativa, family Gramineae. African rice belongs to the related species 
O.  glaberrima, whereas the so-called wild rice is not a true rice at all.] 
• the seeds of the rice plant used as food. Rice provides the staple diet of half the world's population and is second only to wheat in terms of total output. Rice seedlings are usually planted in flooded fields or paddies, so that terraces are necessary on hillsides and a reliable source of water is essential. 
▶verb [with object] North American force (cooked potatoes or other vegetables) through a sieve or ricer. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French ris, from Italian riso, from Greek oruza .

annoy 
əˈnoi

▶verb [with object] irritate (someone); make (someone) a little angry: your damned cheerfulness has always annoyed me 
[no object] : rock music loud enough to annoy. 
• archaic harm or attack repeatedly: a gallant Saxon, who annoyed this Coast. ORIGIN Middle English (in the sense ‘be hateful to’): from Old French anoier (verb), anoi (noun), based on Latin in odio in the phrase mihi in odio est ‘it is hateful to me.’

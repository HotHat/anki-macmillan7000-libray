presume 
BrE prɪˈzjuːm, AmE prəˈz(j)um 

A.  transitive verb 
① (suppose) 推测 tuīcè I presume he's honest 我想他是诚实的 to presume [somebody]/[something] to be [something]; 推测某人/某物为某物 
② (in court) «person, law, court» 认定 rèndìng ‹defendant, accused›he was presumed dead/innocent/guilty 他被认定死亡/无罪/有罪 
③ (presuppose) 假定 jiǎdìng ‹existence, theory›to presume that …; 假定… 
B.  intransitive verb 
① (dare) 擅自行事 shànzì xíngshì to presume to do [something]; 擅自做某事 I hope I'm not presuming 我希望我没有擅作主张 you presume too much! 你太放肆了！ 
② (take advantage) 利用 lìyòng to presume on or upon [something]/[somebody]; (take advantage) 利用某事物/某人 (rely) 指望某事物/某人 
presume 
▶verb
1 I presumed that it had once been an attic: assume, suppose, dare say, imagine, take it, expect, believe, think, surmise, guess, judge, conjecture, speculate, postulate, presuppose.
2 let me presume to give you some advice: venture, dare, have the audacity/effrontery, be so bold as.
PHRASES
presume on he was careful not to presume on their friendship: take (unfair) advantage of, exploit, take liberties with; count on, bank on, place reliance on.

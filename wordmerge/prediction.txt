prediction 
BrE prɪˈdɪkʃ(ə)n, AmE prəˈdɪkʃ(ə)n 
noun 
① Countable(forecast) 预言 yùyán (sth. that has been predicted) 预言的事 yùyán de shì to make a prediction about [something]; 预测某事 
② Uncountable(action of predicting sth.) 预测 yùcè 
prediction 
prəˈdikSH(ə)n

▶noun a thing predicted; a forecast: a prediction that the Greeks would destroy the Persian empire. 
• the action of predicting something: the prediction of future behavior. ORIGIN mid 16th century: from Latin praedictio(n-), from praedicere ‘make known beforehand’ (see predict) .

testament 
ˈtestəmənt

▶noun
1 a person's will, especially the part relating to personal property.
2 something that serves as a sign or evidence of a specified fact, event, or quality: growing attendance figures are a testament to the event's popularity.
3 (in biblical use) a covenant or dispensation. 
• (Testament) a division of the Bible. See also Old Testament, New Testament. 
• (Testament) a copy of the New Testament. ORIGIN Middle English: from Latin testamentum ‘a will’ (from testari ‘testify’), in Christian Latin also translating Greek diathēkē ‘covenant.’

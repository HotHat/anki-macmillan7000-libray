confine A. /BrE kənˈfʌɪn, AmE kənˈfaɪn/ transitive verb 
① (in room) 禁闭 jìnbì ‹person›; 关起 guānqǐ ‹animal›(in cell, prison) 监禁 jiānjìn (in institution) 关押 guānyā to be confined to bed/the house/a wheelchair 卧病在床/被困在家里/离不开轮椅 to be confined to barracks 被禁闭在营房内 
② (limit) 限制 xiànzhì to confine [something] to [something]; 将某事物限制在某范围内 please confine your comments to the matter in hand 请就眼下的问题发表评论 to be confined to [something]; «problem, activity» 局限于 ‹topic, area›the problem is not confined to old people 这个问题并不限于老年人 
B.  /BrE kənˈfʌɪn, AmE kənˈfaɪn/ reflexive verb to confine oneself to [something]/doing [something]; 将自己局限于某事/做某事 jiāng zìjǐ júxiàn yú mǒu shì/zuò mǒu shì please confine yourself to the facts 请你不要脱离实际情况 
C.  confines /BrE ˈkɒnfʌɪn, AmE ˈkɑnfaɪn/ noun plural 
① (borders) 界限 jièxiàn within/beyond the confines of … 在…的界限内/超越了…的界限 
② figurative (constraints) 限制 xiànzhì within the confines of morality/the law 在道德/法律允许的范围内 
confine 
▶verb
1 they were confined in the house: enclose, incarcerate, imprison, intern, impound, hold captive, trap; shut in/up, keep, lock in/up, coop (up); fence in, hedge in, wall in/up.
2 he confined his remarks to the weather: restrict, limit.

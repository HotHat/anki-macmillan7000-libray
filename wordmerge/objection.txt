objection 
əbˈjekSH(ə)n

▶noun an expression or feeling of disapproval or opposition; a reason for disagreeing: they have raised no objections to the latest plans. 
• the action of challenging or disagreeing with something: his view is open to objection. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French, or from late Latin objectio(n-), from the verb obicere (see object) .

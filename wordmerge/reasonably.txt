reasonably 
ˈrēz(ə)nəblē

▶adverb
1 in a fair and sensible way: he began to talk calmly and reasonably about his future. 
• by fair or sensible standards of judgment; rightly or justifiably: police must reasonably believe that a threat to the security of the embassy is present 
[sentence adverb] : it was assumed, reasonably enough, that the murder had taken place by the pond.
2 to a moderate or acceptable degree: fairly; quite: [as submodifier] : she played the piano reasonably well. 
• inexpensively: ski wear which looks good and is reasonably priced.

adverbORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French adjectif, -ive, from Latin adject- ‘added,’ from the verb adjicere, from ad- ‘toward’ + jacere ‘throw.’ The term was originally used in the phrase noun adjective, translating Latin nomen adjectivum, the latter being a translation of Greek onoma epitheton ‘attributive name.’

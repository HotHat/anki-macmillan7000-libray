chicken 
ˈCHikən

▶noun
1 a domestic fowl kept for its eggs or meat, especially a young one. 
• meat from a chicken: roast chicken.
2 informal a game in which the first person to lose nerve and withdraw from a dangerous situation is the loser. 
• a coward.
3 informal (among homosexuals) an adolescent male. 
▶adjective [predicative] informal cowardly: they were too chicken to follow the murderers into the mountains. 
▶verb [no object] (chicken out) informal withdraw from or fail in something through lack of nerve: the referee chickened out of giving a penalty. 
PHRASES
don't count your chickens before they're hatched see count1. ORIGIN Old English cīcen, cȳcen, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch kieken and German Küchlein, and probably also to cock1 .

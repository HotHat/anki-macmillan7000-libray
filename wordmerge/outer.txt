outer 
ˈoudər

▶adjective [attributive] outside; external: the outer door. 
• further from the center or inside: the outer hall at the museum's main entrance. 
• (especially in place names) more remote: Outer Mongolia. 
• objective or physical; not subjective. ORIGIN late Middle English: from out + -er2, replacing earlier utter1 .

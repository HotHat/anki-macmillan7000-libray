rug 
rəɡ

▶noun a floor covering of thick woven material or animal skin, typically not extending over the entire floor. 
• chiefly British a thick woolen coverlet or wrap, used especially when traveling. 
• informal, chiefly North American a toupee or wig. 
PHRASES
pull the rug (out) from under abruptly withdraw support from (someone): the rug was pulled right out from beneath our feet.ORIGIN mid 16th century (denoting a type of coarse woolen cloth): probably of Scandinavian origin; compare with Norwegian dialect rugga ‘coverlet,’ Swedish rugg ‘ruffled hair’; related to rag1. The sense ‘small carpet’ dates from the early 19th century.

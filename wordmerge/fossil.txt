fossil 
ˈfäsəl

▶noun the remains or impression of a prehistoric organism preserved in petrified form or as a mold or cast in rock. 
• derogatory or humorous an antiquated or stubbornly unchanging person or thing: he can be a cantankerous old fossil at times. 
• a word or phrase that has become obsolete except in set phrases or forms, e.g., hue in hue and cry. ORIGIN mid 16th century (denoting a fossilized fish found, and believed to have lived, underground): from French fossile, from Latin fossilis ‘dug up,’ from fodere ‘dig.’

appreciate 
əˈprēSHēˌāt

▶verb [with object]
1 recognize the full worth of: she feels that he does not appreciate her. 
• be grateful for (something): I'd appreciate any information you could give me.
2 understand (a situation) fully; recognize the full implications of: they failed to appreciate the pressure he was under 
[with clause] : I appreciate that you cannot be held totally responsible.
3 [no object] rise in value or price: they expected the house to appreciate in value. DERIVATIVES appreciator 
-ˌātər 
nounORIGIN mid 16th century: from late Latin appretiat- ‘set at a price, appraised,’ from the verb appretiare, from ad- ‘to’ + pretium ‘price.’

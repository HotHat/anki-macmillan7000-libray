diagnosis 
ˌdīəɡˈnōsəs

▶noun (plural diagnoses 
-ˌsēz
)
1 the identification of the nature of an illness or other problem by examination of the symptoms: early diagnosis and treatment are essential 
a diagnosis of Crohn's disease was made.
2 the distinctive characterization in precise terms of a genus, species, or phenomenon. ORIGIN late 17th century: modern Latin, from Greek, from diagignōskein ‘distinguish, discern,’ from dia ‘apart’ + gignōskein ‘recognize, know.’

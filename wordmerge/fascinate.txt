BrE ˈfasɪneɪt, AmE ˈfæsəˌneɪt 
transitive verb 迷住 mízhù she really fascinates me 她让我神魂颠倒 
fascinate 
ˈfasəˌnāt

▶verb [with object] (usually be fascinated) draw irresistibly the attention and interest of (someone): I've always been fascinated by other cultures 
[with object] : she was fascinated to learn about this strange land. 
• archaic (especially of a snake) deprive (a person or animal) of the ability to resist or escape by the power of a look or gaze: the serpent fascinates its prey. ORIGIN late 16th century (in the sense ‘bewitch, put under a spell’): from Latin fascinat- ‘bewitched,’ from the verb fascinare, from fascinum ‘spell, witchcraft.’

nation 
ˈnāSH(ə)n

▶noun a large aggregate of people united by common descent, history, culture, or language, inhabiting a particular country or territory: leading industrialized nations. 
• a North American Indian people or confederation of peoples. ORIGIN Middle English: via Old French from Latin natio(n-), from nat- ‘born,’ from the verb nasci .

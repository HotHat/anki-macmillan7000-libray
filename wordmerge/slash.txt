slash
1 
slaSH

▶verb [with object] cut (something) with a violent sweeping movement, typically using a knife or sword: a tire was slashed on my car 
they cut and slashed their way to the river 
[no object] : the man slashed at him with a sword. 
• informal reduce (a price, quantity, etc.) greatly: the workforce has been slashed by 2,000. 
• archaic lash, whip, or thrash severely. 
• archaic crack (a whip). 
• archaic criticize (someone or something) severely. 
▶noun
1 a cut made with a wide, sweeping stroke: the man took a mighty slash at his head with a large sword. 
• a wound or gash made by a cut with a wide, sweeping stroke: he staggered over with a crimson slash across his temple. 
• a bright patch or flash of color or light: yellow and gold foliage, with the odd slash of red.
2 an oblique stroke (/) in print or writing, used between alternatives (e.g., and/or), in fractions (e.g., 3/4), in ratios (e.g., miles/day), or between separate elements of a text. 
• [usually as modifier] a genre of fiction, published chiefly in fanzines or online, in which characters who appear together in movies, television, or other popular media are portrayed as having a sexual (especially homosexual) relationship. 3 North American debris resulting from the felling or destruction of trees.

scrutiny 
ˈskro͞otnē

▶noun (plural scrutinies) critical observation or examination: every aspect of local government was placed under scrutiny. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin scrutinium, from scrutari ‘to search’ (originally ‘sort trash,’ from scruta ‘trash’). Early use referred to the taking of individual votes in an election procedure.

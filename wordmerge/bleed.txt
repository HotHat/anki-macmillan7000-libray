bleed 
▶verb
1 his arm was bleeding: lose blood, hemorrhage.
2 the doctor bled him: draw blood from; Medicine exsanguinate; archaic phlebotomize.
3 one color bled into another: flow, run, seep, filter, percolate, leach.
4 sap was bleeding from the trunk: flow, run, ooze, seep, exude, weep.
5 funds are in danger of being bled dry : drain, sap, deplete, milk, exhaust.
6 my heart bleeds for them: grieve for, ache for, sorrow for, mourn for, lament for, feel for, suffer for; sympathize with, pity.

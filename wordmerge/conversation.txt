conversation 
ˌkänvərˈsāSH(ə)n

▶noun the informal exchange of ideas by spoken words: the two men were deep in conversation. 
• an instance of this: she picked up the phone and held a conversation in French. 
PHRASES
make conversation talk for the sake of politeness without having anything to say.ORIGIN Middle English (in the sense ‘living among, familiarity, intimacy’): via Old French from Latin conversatio(n-), from the verb conversari (see converse1) .

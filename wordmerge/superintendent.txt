superintendent 
ˌso͞op(ə)r(ə)nˈtendənt

▶noun a person who manages or superintends an organization or activity: the construction superintendent 
[as modifier] : the superintendent registrar. 
• a high-ranking official, especially the head of a large urban police department. 
• North American the caretaker of a building. ORIGIN mid 16th century: from ecclesiastical Latin superintendent- ‘overseeing,’ from the verb superintendere (see superintend) .

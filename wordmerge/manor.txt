manor 
ˈmanər

▶noun (also manor house) a large country house with lands; the principal house of a landed estate. 
• chiefly historical (especially in England and Wales) a unit of land, originally a feudal lordship, consisting of a lord's demesne and lands rented to tenants. 
• historical (in North America) an estate or district leased to tenants, especially one granted by royal charter in a British colony or by the Dutch governors of what is now New York State. DERIVATIVES manorial

minimize 
BrE ˈmɪnɪmʌɪz, AmE ˈmɪnəˌmaɪz 
transitive verb 
① (reduce) 将…减少到最低限度 jiāng… jiǎnshǎo dào zuì dī xiàndù ‹risk, cost, loss›
② (play down) 降低 jiàngdī ‹seriousness›; 减轻 jiǎnqīng ‹pain›; 贬低 biǎndī ‹contribution, achievement›
minimize 
▶verb
1 the aim is to minimize costs: keep down, keep at/to a minimum, reduce, decrease, cut down, lessen, curtail, diminish, prune; informal slash. ANTONYMS maximize, increase.
2 we should not minimize his contribution: belittle, make light of, play down, underestimate, underrate, downplay, undervalue, underappreciate, understate; informal pooh-pooh; archaic hold cheap. ANTONYMS exaggerate.

isolated 
ˈīsəˌlādəd

▶adjective far away from other places, buildings, or people; remote: isolated farms and villages. 
• having minimal contact or little in common with others: he lived a very isolated existence. 
• single; exceptional: they were isolated incidents. ORIGIN mid 18th century: from French isolé, from Italian isolato, from late Latin insulatus ‘made into an island,’ from Latin insula ‘island.’

undo 
ˌənˈdo͞o

▶verb (undoes 
-ˈdəz
, undoing; past undid; past participle undone) [with object]
1 unfasten, untie, or loosen (something): the knot was difficult to undo.
2 cancel or reverse the effects or results of (a previous action or measure): there wasn't any way Evelyn could undo the damage. 
• cancel (the last one or more commands executed by a computer).
3 formal cause the downfall or ruin of: Iago's hatred of women undoes him. 
▶noun Computing a feature of a computer program that allows a user to cancel or reverse the last one or more commands executed. ORIGIN Old English undōn (see un-2, do1) .

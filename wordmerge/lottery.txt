lottery 
ˈlädərē

▶noun (plural lotteries) a means of raising money by selling numbered tickets and giving prizes to the holders of numbers drawn at random. 
• [in singular] a process or thing whose success or outcome is governed by chance: the lottery of life. ORIGIN mid 16th century: probably from Dutch loterij, from lot ‘lot.’

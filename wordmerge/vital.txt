vital 
ˈvīdl

▶adjective
1 absolutely necessary or important; essential: secrecy is of vital importance 
it is vital that the system is regularly maintained. 
• indispensable to the continuance of life: the vital organs.
2 full of energy; lively: a beautiful, vital girl.
3 archaic fatal: the wound is vital. 
▶noun (vitals) the body's important internal organs, especially the gut or the genitalia. 
• short for vital signs. ORIGIN late Middle English (describing the animating principle of living beings, also sense
2 of the adjective): via Old French from Latin vitalis, from vita ‘life.’ The sense ‘essential’ dates from the early 17th century.

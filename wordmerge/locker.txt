locker 
ˈläkər

▶noun
1 a small lockable closet or compartment, typically as one of a number placed together for public or general use, e.g., in schools, gymnasiums, or train stations. 
• a chest or compartment on a ship or boat for clothes, stores, equipment, or ammunition.
2 a device that locks something. ORIGIN late Middle English: probably related to Flemish loker .

exile 
ˈeɡˌzīl ˈekˌsīl

▶noun the state of being barred from one's native country, typically for political or punitive reasons: he knew now that he would die in exile. 
• a person who lives away from their native country, either from choice or compulsion: the return of political exiles. 
• (the Exile) another term for Babylonian Captivity. 
▶verb [with object] (usually be exiled) expel and bar (someone) from their native country, typically for political or punitive reasons: a corrupt dictator who had been exiled from his country 
he was exiled to Tasmania in 1849 
(as adjective exiled) : supporters of the exiled king. ORIGIN Middle English: the noun partly from Old French exil ‘banishment’ and partly from Old French exile ‘banished person’; the verb from Old French exiler; all based on Latin exilium ‘banishment,’ from exul ‘banished person.’

sour 
BrE saʊə, AmE ˈsaʊ(ə)r 

A.  adjective 
① (bitter, sharp) 酸的 suān de to taste sour 有酸味 
② (off, bad) 馊的 sōu de ‹milk, wine›; 酸臭的 suānchòu de ‹taste, flavour, smell›to go or turn sour 变馊 
③ figurative (bitter) 辛酸的 xīnsuān de (resentful) 怨恨的 yuànhèn de (bad-tempered) 脾气坏的 píqi huài de ‹person›; 坏的 huài de ‹temper›(angry) 阴郁的 yīnyù de ‹mood›to go or turn sour 变得令人不快 relations between them have turned sour 他们的关系恶化了 to have a sour look on one's face 阴沉着脸 to feel sour 闷闷不乐 
B.  noun 酸味鸡尾酒 suānwèi jīwěijiǔ a rum/vodka/whisky sour 酸味朗姆/伏特加/威士忌鸡尾酒 
C.  transitive verb 
① (make sour) 使…变酸 shǐ… biànsuān ‹milk›
② figurative (make unpleasant) 使…恶化 shǐ… èhuà ‹relationship, atmosphere›; 使…变坏 shǐ… biànhuài ‹personality›
D.  intransitive verb 
① (turn bad) «milk» 变酸腐 biàn suānfǔ 
② figurative (become bitter, acrimonious) «relationship, friendship» 恶化 èhuà

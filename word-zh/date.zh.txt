date
1 
BrE deɪt, AmE deɪt 

A.  noun 
① (day of the month) 日期 rìqī what's the date today? 今天几号？ date of birth 出生日期 date of expiry 到期日 at a later date 日后 at a or some future date 将来某一天 to date 到目前为止 
② (year of event) 年份 niánfèn the date of the Battle of Hastings 黑斯廷斯战役的年份 Shakespeare's dates are 1564-1616 莎士比亚的生卒年份是1564－1616年 
③ (romantic meeting) 约会 yuēhuì to go (out) on a date (with [somebody]) （与某人）约会 to have a date with [somebody] 与某人约会 on their first date 他们第一次约会时 
④ BrE (appointment to meet) 约见 yuējiàn a lunch date 午餐会晤 to make a date 约好见面时间 
⑤ especially AmE (boyfriend/girlfriend) 约会对象 yuēhuì duìxiàng 
B.  transitive verb 
① (mark with date) 给…注明日期 gěi… zhùmíng rìqī ‹cheque, document, work of art›in reply to your letter dated May 12th 兹答复你方5月12日来信 
② (identify age of) 确定…的年代 quèdìng… de niándài ‹fossil, artefact›experts have dated the skeleton to or at 300 BC 专家们确定这具骷髅的年代在公元前300年 
③ (reveal age of) «clothes, hairstyle, tastes» 使…显老 shǐ… xiǎnlǎo ‹person›that dates me, doesn't it? 这使我显老了，对吧？ 
④ especially AmE (have romantic association with) 与…约会 yǔ… yuēhuì she is dating my brother 她正在和我哥哥谈恋爱 
C.  intransitive verb 
① (originate) 追溯 zhuīsù to date from or back to; 追溯到 ‹point in time, event›the college dates back to the 14th century 这所学院始建于14世纪 her fear of fire dates from or back to a childhood accident 她怕火，这一点始于她小时候发生的一起事故 
② (become dated) «fashion, movie» 过时 guòshí this style of shoe will never date 这种款式的鞋子永远都不会过时

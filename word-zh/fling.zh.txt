fling 
BrE flɪŋ, AmE flɪŋ 

A.  transitive verb(past tense, past participle flung) 
① (throw) 扔 rēng ‹ball, missile, garment, papers›she flung her book at him, but missed 她把自己的书朝他掷去，但是没打中 she was flung from her horse 她从马上被抛了下来 she flung him a look of contempt 她轻蔑地看了他一眼 to fling [something] in [somebody's] face 用某事攻击某人 
② (propel) 猛推 měng tuī ‹person›he flung his opponent to the ground 他将对手摔倒在地 
③ (move part of body) 猛甩 měng shuǎi ‹hands, head, hair›she flung her arms around his neck/him 她张开双臂搂住他的脖子/抱住他 
④ (send unceremoniously) «person, authority, event» 使突然陷入 shǐ tūrán xiànrù the prisoner was flung into the dungeons 囚犯被投入地牢 to fling [somebody] into confusion 使某人陷入困惑 
⑤ (hurl) 激烈地说出 jīliè de shuōchū ‹remark›they were flinging abuse at each other 他们激烈地互相辱骂 
B.  reflexive verb(past tense, past participle flung); to fling oneself 
① (move forcefully) 扑 pū she flung herself on the bed 她扑倒在床上 he flung himself over the wall 他跃过墙 the child flung herself into her father's arms 孩子扑进父亲的怀抱 
② (begin with commitment) 全身心投入 quánshēnxīn tóurù to fling oneself into [something]/doing [something]; 全身心投入某事/全力以赴做某事 
C.  intransitive verb(past tense, past participle flung) 猛冲 měng chōng he was flinging about like a madman 他像个疯子般横冲直撞 
D.  noun 
① (throw) 扔 rēng 
② (spree) 一时的放纵 yīshí de fàngzòng to have a fling 恣意行乐 youth must have its fling proverb 年轻人总要放纵一下 
③ (brief enthusiasm) 短暂的热情 duǎnzàn de rèqíng a brief fling with Marxism 对马克思主义的短暂热情 
④ colloquial (attempt) 尝试 chángshì to have or take a fling at [something]/doing [something]; 尝试某事/做某事

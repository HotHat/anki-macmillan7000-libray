moral 
BrE ˈmɒr(ə)l, AmE ˈmɔrəl 

A.  adjective 
① (ethical) 道德的 dàodé de on moral grounds 基于道德原因 to take the moral high ground 在道义上占优势 human beings are moral animals 人类是有道德感的动物 
② (virtuous) 品行端正的 pǐnxíng duānzhèng de to lead a moral life 为人正派 
③ (morally instructive) 有教益的 yǒu jiàoyì de ‹tale›
B.  noun (practical lesson) 寓意 yùyì to draw a moral from [something]; 从某事物中得到教益 to point a moral 指明寓意 
C.  morals noun plural (standards and principles of behaviour) 道德 dàodé public morals 公德 to have no morals 不道德 a woman of loose morals 荡妇

shot 
BrE ʃɒt, AmE ʃɑt 

A.  noun 
① (act of shooting) 射击 shèjī (sound of shooting) 枪声 qiāngshēng to take or fire a shot at [somebody]/[something]; 对某人/某物射击 he fired a shot at the burglar 他对着盗贼开了一枪 to have a shot (at [somebody]/[something]) （对某人/某物）开一枪 to fire the first shot 打响第一枪 like a shot colloquial (at once) 马上 (very fast) 飞快地 
② colloquial (attempt at shooting) 射击尝试 shèjī chángshì ; figurative (attempt) 尝试 chángshì he asked me if I would like to have a shot at a pheasant 他问我是否愿意去打野鸡 a shot at [something]/doing [something]; 尝试某事/做某事 to have a shot 尝试一下 
③ (in cricket, tennis, billiards, golf, etc.) 击球 jīqiú (in football, hockey) 踢球 tīqiú that was a fine shot! 那个球打得真漂亮！ a backhand/forehand shot 反手/正手击球 to have or take a shot at goal 射门 good shot! 好球！ two shots behind [somebody] 落后某人两个球 
④ (in athletics) 铅球 qiānqiú to put the shot 推铅球 tuī qiānqiú 
⑤ Uncountable(lead pellets) 铅弹 qiāndàn ; (coarse metal powder) 金属粒屑 jīnshǔ lì xiè 
⑥ (person who shoots) 射击手 shèjīshǒu a good/poor shot 好的/差的射击手 a crack shot 神枪手 
⑦ (photograph) 照片 zhàopiàn a long/close-up shot 远景/特写照片 
⑧ (continuous film sequence) 一段影片 yī duàn yǐngpiàn a slow-motion shot 慢镜头 
⑨ (launch of space vehicle) 发射 fāshè a moon/Mars shot 登月/火星探测航天器发射 
⑩ colloquial (injection) 注射 zhùshè to give [somebody] a shot 给某人注射 the doctor gave her a shot to kill the pain 医生给她打了止痛针 a shot in the arm figurative 激励 the improved trade figures are a much-needed shot in the arm for the economy 贸易数字的改善为经济打了一剂急需的强心针 
B.  adjective 
① (streaked) 闪色的 shǎnsè de ‹satin›a dress of shot silk 用闪色绸做成的连衣裙 brown hair shot with grey 花白的褐色头发 (to be) shot through with [something] 充斥某事物 humour shot through with sadness 饱含哀伤的幽默 
② colloquial (worn out) 累坏的 lèihuài de (used up) 用完的 yòngwán de (wrecked) 毁坏的 huǐhuài de his nerves were completely shot 他的神经脆弱到了极点 
③ BrE colloquial (rid) to be/get shot of [somebody]/[something] 摆脱某人/某事物 bǎituō mǒu rén/mǒu shìwù I just can't get shot of this cold! 我的感冒就是好不了！

spark 
BrE spɑːk, AmE spɑrk 

A.  noun 
① (from fire) 火花 huǒhuā (the) sparks will fly (there will be an argument) 会发生激烈争吵 (there will be a fight) 会打架 to strike sparks off each other 互相启发 
② Electricity 电火花 diànhuǒhuā 
③ figurative (sparkle) 闪现 shǎnxiàn there was a spark of mischief/excitement/interest in her eyes 她的眼睛闪出一丝调皮/兴奋/兴致 
④ figurative (small amount) 一点儿 yīdiǎnr he hasn't a spark of generosity/decency in him 他没有一点起码的宽宏/体面 
B.  intransitive verb «fire» 飞火星 fēi huǒxīng ; «firework, electrical appliance» 冒火花 mào huǒhuā 
C.  transitive verb «event, electrical failure» 引发 yǐnfā the explosion sparked a fire 爆炸引起了火灾 PHRASAL VERB spark off transitive verb 
① [spark off something], [spark something off] (provide stimulus for) 引起 yǐnqǐ ‹conflict, riot, controversy›
② [spark off somebody] (stimulate mentally) 启发 qǐfā

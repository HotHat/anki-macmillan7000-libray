cause 
BrE kɔːz, AmE kɔz 

A.  noun 
① Uncountable and countable(person, thing making sth. happen) 原因 yuányīn to die of or from natural causes 自然死亡 the cause of the accident 事故的起因 the root cause of a problem 问题的根源 
② Uncountable(reason) 理由 lǐyóu there is no cause for alarm 不必惊慌 you have no cause for complaint 你没理由抱怨 to give cause for concern 令人担忧 to have cause to do [something]; 有理由做某事 with/without good cause 理由充分地/无缘无故地 
③ Uncountable and countable(principle, aim) 事业 shìyè to be (all) in or for a good cause 有好的初衷 a lost cause 注定失败的事业 to make common cause with [somebody]/[something] 与某人/某团体联合起来 
④ Uncountable and countableLaw (grounds) 诉讼理由 sùsòng lǐyóu a cause of action 起诉的理由 to show cause 陈述理由 
⑤ CountableLaw (claim, dispute) 诉讼案 sùsòng'àn to plead [somebody's] cause 为某人辩护 
B.  transitive verb 引起 yǐnqǐ to cause [somebody] to do [something]; 使某人做某事 to cause trouble 惹麻烦 to cause [somebody] problems/anxiety 给某人带来问题/使某人感到焦虑 the heavy rains caused widespread flooding 大雨造成了大面积的洪涝

surrender 
BrE səˈrɛndə, AmE səˈrɛndər 

A.  intransitive verb 
① (submit) (to enemy control) «soldier, army, country» 投降 tóuxiáng ; (to authorities) «criminal, fugitive» 自首 zìshǒu ; (to influence) «person, authority» 屈服 qūfú to surrender to [somebody]; 屈服于某人 the government surrendered to the influence of the unions 面对工会的影响力，政府让步了 I surrender figurative 我投降 
② (give in to emotion) 听任 tīngrèn to surrender to one's craving for drugs/the desire to retain power 克制不住犯毒瘾/压抑不住保住权势的渴望 
B.  transitive verb 
① (cede to enemy, give up) 放弃 fàngqì ‹town, liberties, rights›he agreed to surrender all claims to the property 他同意放弃所有的产权要求 to surrender power to the opposition 将权力交给反对党 
② (hand over or in) 交出 jiāochū 
③ Finance (cancel) 撤销 chèxiāo ‹insurance policy›
④ Law 放弃 fàngqì ‹lease, tenancy›
C.  reflexive verbto surrender oneself 
① (to the enemy) 投降 tóuxiáng ; (to the authorities) «criminal, fugitive» 自首 zìshǒu the hijackers eventually surrendered themselves to the police 劫持犯最终向警方投降了 
② (to emotion) 放任自己 fàngrèn zìjǐ to surrender oneself to the joy/despair of [something] 因某事物沉浸在快乐之中/陷于绝望之中 
③ literary (to sexual partner) 听任摆布 tīngrèn bǎibu she could hardly wait to surrender herself to him 她迫不及待地要臣服于他 
D.  nounUncountable and countable
① (submission) (of soldier, army, garrison, town, country) 投降 tóuxiáng (of criminal, fugitive) 自首 zìshǒu (of person, authorities) 屈服 qūfú to force [somebody] into surrender 迫使某人投降 the surrender of the bank robbers to the police 银行劫匪向警察投降 the government's surrender to the unions 政府对工会组织的让步 
② (giving up) (of territory) 割让 gēràng (of liberties, rights, power) 放弃 fàngqì they agreed to the surrender of their land to the Canadian state 他们同意把他们的土地割让给加拿大 
③ (handing over or in) 交出 jiāochū 
④ (giving in to emotion) 听任 tīngrèn surrender to despair 陷入绝望 
⑤ Finance (cancellation of policy) 退保 tuìbǎo 
⑥ Law (giving up of lease, tenancy) 弃租 qì zū

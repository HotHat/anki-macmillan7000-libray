southwest 
ˌsouTHˈwest

▶noun
1 (usually the southwest) the direction toward the point of the horizon midway between south and west, or the point of the horizon itself: clouds uncoiled from the southwest. 
• the compass point corresponding to this.
2 the southwestern part of a country, region, or town: the beach is in the southwest of the island. 
• (usually the Southwest) the southwestern part of the US: the desert turtle population in the Southwest. 
▶adjective [attributive]
1 lying toward, near, or facing the southwest: the southwest tower collapsed in a storm. 
• (of a wind) blowing from the southwest.
2 of or denoting the southwestern part of a specified country, region, or town or its inhabitants: fishing in southwest Alaska's Bristol Bay area. 
▶adverb to or toward the southwest: they drove directly southwest.

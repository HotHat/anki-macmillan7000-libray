demand 
BrE dɪˈmɑːnd, AmE dəˈmænd 

A.  transitive verb 
① (request forcefully) 强烈要求 qiángliè yāoqiú to demand one's money back 坚决要求还钱 to demand [something] from [somebody] 向某人索要某物 I demand to know the truth 我一定要知道真相 I demand to be fed; 我要求吃饭 to demand that [somebody] do [something] + subjunctive 强烈要求某人做某事 we demand that our employees be punctual 我们严格要求我们的雇员守时 to demand of [somebody] that he/she etc. + subjunctive ; 强烈要求某人… we demand of our employees that they be punctual 我们严格要求雇员守时 she demanded of him that he tell her his secret 她极力要他把自己的秘密告诉她 
② (insist on being told) 盘查 pánchá she demanded what I was doing 她质问我在干什么 
③ (require) 需要 xūyào ‹skill, time, patience, workers›the qualities demanded of a manager 做经理应有的素质 
B.  noun 
① Countable(request) 要求 yāoqiú to make a demand for [something]; 要求得到某物 a demand for [somebody] to do [something]; 要某人做某事的呼声 on demand 一经要求 payable on demand 见票即付的 
② Countable(requirement) [令人烦恼的] 要求 yāoqiú I have many demands on my time 我的时间安排得很紧 to make or put demands on [somebody]/[something]; 对某人/某事提出要求 
③ Countable and uncountableEconomics 需求 xūqiú supply and demand 供求 to be in demand 需求大 
④ Countable(bill) 付款请求 fùkuǎn qǐngqiú

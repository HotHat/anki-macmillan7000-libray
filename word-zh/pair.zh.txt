pair 
BrE pɛː, AmE pɛr 

A.  noun 
① (of gloves, socks, shoes) 一双 yī shuāng (of cards, ornaments) 一对 yduì to be one of a pair 是一对中的一个 a matching pair 相匹配的一对 these gloves aren't a pair 这两只手套不是一副 I've only got one pair of hands! 我只有一双手！ she's got a nice pair of legs 她有一双美腿 in pairs 成对地 
② (of trousers, pants) 一条 yī tiáo (of pyjamas) 一套 ytào (of scissors, shears) 一把 ybǎ a pair of glasses 一副眼镜 
③ (two people) 俩人 liǎ rén you're a right pair of fools/layabouts colloquial 你们是一对十足的傻瓜/懒汉 
④ (married couple) 夫妻俩 fūqī liǎ 
⑤ (of animals) 一对动物 yī duì dòngwù a coach and pair 双驾四轮马车 
⑥ (matching item) 配对的另一个 pèiduì de lìng yī gè I'm looking for a pair to this china figure 我正在寻找与这个瓷人配对的另一只 
B.  transitive verb 
① (organize in pairs) 把…配对 bǎ… pèiduì I'll pair the socks and put them in the drawer 我来把这些短袜配成双放在抽屉里 
② (put together as a pair) 与…搭配 yǔ… dāpèi ‹clothes›; 为…配搭档 wèi… pèi dādàng ‹people›; 使…交配 shǐ… jiāopèi ‹animals›to pair the jeans with a T-shirt 用T恤和牛仔裤搭配 to pair each name with a photograph 把每个名字与相应的照片放在一起 to pair [somebody] with [somebody] 让某人和某人结对 I've been paired with Jonathan for the mixed doubles 我被指派与乔纳森组成一对参加混合双打 
C.  intransitive verb «animals, birds» 交配 jiāopèi ; «people» 结婚 jiéhūn I'll pair with Timothy 我将与蒂莫西结成夫妻 PHRASAL VERBS pair off

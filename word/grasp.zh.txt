grasp 
BrE ɡrɑːsp, AmE ɡræsp 

A.  noun 
① (hold, grip) 紧抓 jǐnzhuā the pen fell from her grasp 钢笔从她手中掉落 to take a firm/strong grasp on or of [something] 紧紧地/有力地握住某物 to tighten/loosen one's grasp 握紧手/松开手 he held her in an iron grasp 他死死地抓住她 
② figurative (power, reach) (of person, organization) 掌握 zhǎngwò (of emotion) 控制 kòngzhì in the grasp of [somebody]/[something]; 在某人/某物的控制之中 to escape/slip from or out of [somebody's] grasp 逃脱/摆脱某人的控制 the prize has eluded her grasp 她没能获奖 beyond the grasp of [somebody]/[something] 在某人/某物的掌控之外 to have [somebody]/[something] within one's grasp 掌握某人/某事物 
③ (understanding, imagination) 理解 lǐjiě to have a good/sound grasp of [something] 对某事物有充分/彻底的理解 to have a poor grasp of [something] 对某事物了解甚少 within [somebody's] grasp 在某人的理解范围内 beyond [somebody's] grasp 超出某人的理解范围 
B.  transitive verb 
① (seize) 抓住 zhuāzhù he grasped me by the arm 他揪住了我的胳膊 to grasp the chance or opportunity figurative 抓住机会 
② (understand) 理解 lǐjiě she could never grasp what to do/how to do it 她永远不明白该做什么/怎么做 to grasp the seriousness/enormity/meaning of [something] 理解某事的严重性/艰巨性/意思 
C.  intransitive verb to grasp at [something]; 试图抓住某物 shìtú zhuāzhù mǒu wù to grasp at straws figurative 抓救命稻草 zhuā jiùmìng dàocǎo to grasp at the chance or opportunity figurative 尽力把握机会 jìnlì bǎwò jīhuì

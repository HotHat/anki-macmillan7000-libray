explode 
BrE ɪkˈspləʊd, ɛkˈspləʊd, AmE ɪkˈsploʊd 

A.  intransitive verb 
① (blow up) «bomb, rocket, gas, star» 爆炸 bàozhà 
② (be destroyed) «aircraft, ship, building» 被炸毁 bèi zhàhuǐ 
③ (expand) «gas, chemical» 剧烈膨胀 jùliè péngzhàng 
④ (arise suddenly) «violence» 爆发 bàofā to explode into [something]; «crowd, audience» 突然爆发出… to explode into applause 爆发出鼓掌喝彩声 to explode with or into rage/laughter 勃然大怒/哄然大笑 to be ready to explode «person» 怒火一触即发 
⑤ (increase) «population, birthrate» 激增 jīzēng 
⑥ (appear suddenly) 突然出现 tūrán chūxiàn to explode on to the scene; 突然现身 to explode on to the musical/pop/theatrical scene 突然出现在音乐界/流行音乐界/戏剧界 
B.  transitive verb 
① (detonate) 引爆 yǐnbào ‹bomb›
② (refute) 戳穿 chuōchuān ‹rumour, myth›; «publication» 推翻 tuīfān ‹theory, assumption›

stain 
BrE steɪn, AmE steɪn 

A.  transitive verb 
① (soil) 弄脏 nòngzāng ‹clothes, carpet, fingers›
② (dye) 给…染色 gěi… rǎnsè ‹wood, fabric›to stain [something] with [something]; 用某物给某物染色 to stain [something] teak/mahogany 把某物漆成柚木色/红木色 
③ figurative (damage) 玷污 diànwū ‹reputation, honour›his good name was stained by the accusation 这一指控玷污了他的好名声 
B.  intransitive verb 
① (mark) «wine, ink, blood» 留下污渍 liúxia wūzì 
② (be marked) «clothes, material» 被弄脏 bèi nòngzāng this fabric stains easily 这种布料容易脏 
C.  noun 
① (mark) 污迹 wūjì a blood/coffee stain 血污/咖啡污渍 to remove a stain from [something] 除去某物上的污点 it will leave a stain 这会留下污渍 
② (dye) 染色剂 rǎnsèjì 
③ figurative (moral blemish) 污点 wūdiǎn without a stain on one's character 品行没有任何污点的 a stain on the reputation or good name of one's family 对家族声誉的玷污

mad 
BrE mad, AmE mæd 

A.  adjective 
① especially BrE (insane) 疯的 fēng de to drive [somebody] mad 使某人发疯 to go mad 发疯 to go mad with … 因…而丧失理智 ‹grief, rage›to be [something] gone mad figurative 是疯狂的某物 it's nationalism gone mad 这是疯狂的民族主义 [somebody] must be mad to do [something] 某人一定是疯了，居然做某事 to be as mad as a hatter 疯疯癫癫 
② colloquial (foolish) 愚蠢的 yúchǔn de to be mad to do [something]; 做某事是愚蠢的 you'd have to be mad to go out in weather like this 在这种天气还要出去，你一定是犯傻了 in a mad moment, I asked her to come too 我一时糊涂把她也叫来了 
③ predicative colloquial (angry) 气愤的 qìfèn de was I (ever) mad! 真是气死我了！ to make or drive [somebody] mad; 使某人生气 to be or get mad about [something]; 因某事而生气 to be mad at or with [somebody] for doing [something]; 因某人做某事而对其发火 
④ predicative (enthusiastic) 狂热的 kuángrè de to be mad about or on [something]/[somebody]; 对某事/某人很着迷 …-mad; 对…着迷的 to be car-/football-mad 对汽车/足球着迷 
⑤ (frantic) 狂乱的 kuángluàn de ‹dash, round›to be in a mad rush/panic 忙得团团转/恐慌不堪 to go mad 忙乱地做事 to be mad with …; 因…而狂乱 ‹anxiety, joy›to go mad with [something] colloquial 用太多的 ‹chilli, perfume›like mad colloquial 拼命地 to run like mad 狂奔 
⑥ (rabid) 患狂犬病的 huàn kuángquǎnbìng de ‹dog›
⑦ (enraged) 狂暴的 kuángbào de ‹bull›

chill 
BrE tʃɪl, AmE tʃɪl 

A.  noun 
① Uncountable(coldness) 寒冷 hánlěng there is a chill in the air 空气中透着几分寒意 
② Countable(illness) 风寒 fēnghán to catch a chill 着凉 
③ Countablefigurative (feeling of fear) 害怕 hàipà to send a chill through [somebody] or down [somebody's] spine 令某人毛骨悚然 to cast a chill over [something] 使某事物蒙上一层阴影 
B.  transitive verb 
① (make cold) 使…感到冷 shǐ… gǎndào lěng ‹person›; 使…变冷 shǐ… biànlěng ‹air›; figurative 使…变得扫兴 shǐ… biàn de sǎoxìng ‹atmosphere›to chill [somebody] to the bone or marrow 使某人感到寒气刺骨 
② figurative (cause to fear) 惊吓 jīngxià to chill [somebody's] or the blood 令某人不寒而栗 
③ Culinary (refrigerate) 使…冷却 shǐ… lěngquè ‹drink›(keep cool) 冷藏 lěngcáng ‹food, melon›
C.  intransitive verb 
① Culinary 冷却 lěngquè 
② colloquial (relax) 放松 fàngsōng 
D.  adjective 
① (cold) 寒冷的 hánlěng de ‹wind, weather›
② figurative (causing fear) 令人恐惧的 lìng rén kǒngjù de ‹warning›(grim) 令人寒心的 lìng rén hánxīn de ‹reminder›PHRASAL VERB chill out intransitive verb colloquial 放松一下 fàngsōng yīxià

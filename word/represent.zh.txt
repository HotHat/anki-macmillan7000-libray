represent 
BrE rɛprɪˈzɛnt, AmE ˌrɛprəˈzɛnt 
transitive verb 
① (correspond to) 相当于 xiāngdāng yú for the artist those five pictures represented a year's work 对那位画家而言，那5幅画相当于一年的工作 the new engine represents a major breakthrough in car design 这款新发动机代表了汽车设计的重大突破 
② (act on behalf of) 代表 dàibiǎo Prince Charles represented the Queen at the ceremony 查尔斯王子代表女王参加庆典 he represented Great Britain at the Olympics 他代表英国参加该届奥运会 the disaster victims could not afford a lawyer to represent them 灾难的受害者请不起律师为他们打官司 
③ (form part of) 占据 zhànjù women writers are strongly represented in the list of new fiction published today 在当今新出版小说书单中，女性作家的作品占了很大比例 
④ (depict, portray) 描绘 miáohuì the painting represents a battle at sea 这幅画表现的是一场海战 
⑤ (typify) 体现 tǐxiàn ‹political climate, values, ideals›
⑥ (symbolize) 象征 xiàngzhēng on this map a cross represents a church 在这张地图上，十字代表教堂 open frontiers represent political freedom 开放的边界象征着政治自由 
⑦ (convey in words) 表达 biǎodá words cannot adequately represent our anger 言语不足以表达我们的愤怒 
⑧ (portray on stage, film) 演绎 yǎnyì ‹character, situation›
⑨ (present) 声称 shēngchēng he represented himself as a technical expert 他声称自己是技术专家 his supporters represent him to be a great statesman 他的支持者把他说成是一个伟大的政治家

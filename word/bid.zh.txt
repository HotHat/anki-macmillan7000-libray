bid
1 
BrE bɪd, AmE bɪd 

A.  noun 
① (at auction, for property or company) 出价 chūjià (for contract) 投标 tóubiāo to make a bid (for [something]) 出价竞买（某物） to put in a bid 投标 tóubiāo 
② (attempt) 争取 zhēngqǔ a bid for power 夺权的企图 to make a bid for [something] 企图得到某物 an Olympic bid 奥运会的申办 
③ (in card games) 叫牌 jiàopái to make a bid 叫牌 jiàopái ‘no bid’ “不叫牌” is it my bid? 轮到我叫牌了吗？ 
B.  transitive verb(present participle -dd-; past tense, past participle bid) 
① Busn, Finance 出价 chūjià 
② (in card games) 叫 jiào to bid four spades/six clubs 叫4黑桃/6梅花 
C.  intransitive verb(present participle -dd-; past tense, past participle bid) 
① Busn (make offer) «buyer» 出价竞买 chūjià jìngmǎi ; «contractor» 投标争取 tóubiāo zhēngqǔ to bid against [somebody] 与某人竞价 
② (in card games) 叫牌 jiàopái PHRASAL VERB bid up transitive verb 
① [bid something up, bid up something] (increase) 抬高 táigāo ‹price, rent›

advance 
BrE ədˈvɑːns, AmE ədˈvæns 

A.  intransitive verb 
① also Military (move forward) 前进 qiánjìn 
② (progress) «person, civilization» 进步 jìnbù ; «work» 取得进展 qǔdé jìnzhǎn to advance in one's career 在事业上取得进步 
③ (increase) «price, value, shares» 上涨 shàngzhǎng 
④ formal (be promoted) 得到晋升 dédào jìnshēng 
B.  transitive verb 
① (move forward) 使…向前移动 shǐ… xiàng qián yídòng ‹tape, film, cursor›; 把…拨快 bǎ… bō kuài ‹hands (of clock), date (on watch)›
② (move to earlier date) 提前 tíqián ‹time, date›
③ (improve) 增进 zēngjìn ‹understanding, knowledge›
④ Military 推进 tuījìn 
⑤ (in chess) 向前移动 xiàng qián yídòng ‹piece›
⑥ (promote) 提高 tígāo ‹interests›; 促进 cùjìn ‹cause›
⑦ (put forward) 提出 tíchū ‹idea, theory›
⑧ (lend, pay up front) 预支 yùzhī to advance [somebody] [something] 给某人预支某款额 
⑨ formal (increase) 提高 tígāo ‹price, interest rates›
C.  noun 
① also Military (forward movement) 推进 tuījìn with the advance of old age 随着年岁渐老 
② (improvement) 发展 fāzhǎn a great advance for democracy 民主的一大进步 recent advances in medicine 医学的新进展 
③ (sum of money) 预付款 yùfùkuǎn to ask for an advance on one's salary 要求预支薪金 
④ (increase) 增加 zēngjiā any advance on £100? (at auction etc.) 100英镑还有加价的吗？ 
D.  advances noun plural (overtures) 主动姿态 zhǔdòng zītài (sexual) 求爱 qiú'ài to make advances to [somebody] 勾引某人 
E.  in advance adverb phrase 预先 yùxiān a month in advance 提前一个月 here's £30 in advance 这是30英镑预付款 
F.  in advance of adverb phrase 比…提前 bǐ… tíqián she arrived half an hour in advance of the others 她比其他人早到半个小时 a thinker in advance of his time 一个超越其时代的思想家 
G.  adjectiveattributive 
① (given or done beforehand) 预先的 yùxiān de ‹payment, deposit›an advance warning system 预警系统 
② Military (sent on ahead) 先遣的 xiānqiǎn de

snatch 
▶verb
1 she snatched the sandwich: grab, seize, take hold of, get one's hands on, take, pluck; grasp at, clutch at.
2 informal someone snatched my bag. See steal (sense
1 of the verb).
3 informal she snatched the newborn from the hospital. See abduct.
4 he snatched victory: seize, pluck, wrest, achieve, secure, obtain; scrape.
▶noun
1 brief snatches of sleep: period, spell, time, fit, bout, interval, stretch.
2 a snatch of conversation: fragment, snippet, bit, scrap, part, extract, excerpt, portion.

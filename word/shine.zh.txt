shine 
BrE ʃʌɪn, AmE ʃaɪn 

A.  intransitive verb(past tense, past participle shone) 
① (emit light) «stars, light, lamp» 发光 fāguāng ; «sun, moon» 照耀 zhàoyào to shine through [something] 透过…照射过来 ‹mist, gloom›the light is shining in my eyes 亮光照到了我的眼睛 to shine in/out; 照进/射出 to shine up to [somebody] AmE colloquial «person» 试图讨好某人 
② (glow) «metal, shoes, hair, eyes» 闪光 shǎnguāng 
③ figurative (be radiant) «excitement, goodness, courage» 显露 xiǎnlù ; «eyes» 发亮 fāliàng to shine out of [something]; 从某物中透出 her courage shone forth literary 她的勇气表露无遗 to shine with [something]; 因某事物而放光 his face shone with exertion 他的脸因为用力而发红 
④ (excel) «person» 表现出色 biǎoxiàn chūsè he never shone at school 他的学业成绩从来都不出色 to get or have a chance to shine 得到出人头地的机会 
B.  transitive verb 
① (past tense, past participle shone) (direct) 将…照向 jiāng… zhào xiàng ‹searchlight, headlamps, spotlight›shine your torch over here please 把你的手电往这边照 
② (past tense, past participle shined) (polish) 将…擦亮 jiāng… cāliàng ‹buttons, silver, furniture›I shine my shoes every morning 我每天早晨都擦鞋 
C.  nounUncountable
① (brightness) 光亮 guāngliàng the shine of sunlight/moonlight on water 水面上太阳/月亮的闪光 
② (polish) 光泽 guāngzé to give [something] a shine; 给…上光 ‹floor, shoes›to take the shine off [something] figurative 使某物黯然失色 to take a shine to [somebody] figurative colloquial 喜欢上某人

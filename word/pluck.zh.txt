pluck 
BrE plʌk, AmE plək 

A.  transitive verb 
① (pick) 摘 zhāi ‹flower, fruit›
② (pull or tug sharply) 扯 chě ‹paper, sleeve›to pluck [something] from [somebody's] grasp 抢走某人紧抓的某物 
③ (pull feathers off) 拔…的毛 bá… de máo ‹chicken, goose›
④ (pull out with tweezers) 用镊子拔 yòng nièzi bá to pluck one's eyebrows 拔眉毛 
⑤ Music 弹奏 tánzòu ‹guitar, banjo›
⑥ (remove) 解救 jiějiù to pluck [somebody] to safety 救某人脱险 to be plucked from obscurity 一举成名 
B.  noun 
① Countable(sharp tug) 拽 zhuài he felt a pluck at his sleeve 他感到袖子被拉了一下 
② Uncountable(courage) 勇气 yǒngqì he has plenty of pluck 他很有胆量 to show pluck 表现出勇气 to have the pluck to do [something] 有勇气做某事 PHRASAL VERBS pluck at transitive verb[pluck at something] 拽 zhuài ‹garment›to pluck at [somebody's] sleeve 拉某人的袖子 pluck off transitive verb[pluck off something], [pluck something off] 拔掉 bádiào ‹feather, fluff›pluck out transitive verb[pluck out something], [pluck something out] 挖出 wāchū ‹eye›; 掏出 tāochū ‹ball, handkerchief›; 夺走 duózǒu ‹purse›pluck up: transitive verb to pluck up (the) courage (to do [something]) 鼓起勇气（做某事） gǔqǐ yǒngqì(zuò mǒu shì)

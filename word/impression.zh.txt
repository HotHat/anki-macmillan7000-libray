impression 
BrE ɪmˈprɛʃ(ə)n, AmE ɪmˈprɛʃən 
noun 
① (impact, perception) 印象 yìnxiàng to make a good/bad impression (on [somebody]) （给某人）留下好/坏印象 to make or create a good impression at (the) interview 面试时给人留下好印象 his singing made quite an impression on us 他的演唱给我们留下了深刻印象 what is your impression of the new secretary? 你对新秘书印象如何？ she gives an impression of vulnerability 她给人的印象是弱不禁风 first impressions are often deceptive 第一印象往往靠不住 
② (vague idea or belief) 感觉 gǎnjué she had the impression that she had seen him before 她觉得以前见过他 to be under the impression that … 以为… 
③ (imitation) 滑稽模仿 huájī mófǎng to do impressions/an impression (of [somebody]/[something]); （对某人/某事物）进行滑稽模仿 
④ (representation) 印象画 yìnxiànghuà an artist's impression of the attacker 画家为袭击者画的印象画 
⑤ (difference) 效果 xiàoguǒ the floor was too dirty for the mop to make much (of an) impression 地板太脏了，用拖把拖不干净 
⑥ (of foot, finger, seal, etc.) 压痕 yāhén to leave an impression in the mud 在泥地上留下痕迹 
⑦ Printing (unaltered reprint) 重印本 chóngyìnběn (process) 重印 chóngyìn (total number of copies) 印数 yìnshù

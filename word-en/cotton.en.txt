cotton 
ˈkätn

▶noun
1 a soft white fibrous substance that surrounds the seeds of a tropical and subtropical plant and is used as textile fiber and thread for sewing: a cargo of cotton and wheat 
a white cotton blouse 
an Indian hammock woven in colored cottons. 
• a thread of cotton fiber. 
• North American absorbent cotton.
2 (also cotton plant) the plant that is commercially grown for cotton products. Oil and a protein-rich flour are also obtained from the seeds. [Genus Gossypium, family Malvaceae: many species and forms, including 
G.  barbadense, which is grown in the southern U
S. ] 
▶verb [no object] informal
1 (cotton on) begin to understand: he cottoned on to what I was trying to say.
2 (cotton to) North American have a liking for: his rivals didn't cotton to all the attention he was getting. DERIVATIVES cottony 
ˈkätnē 
adjectiveORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French coton, from Arabic ḳuṭn .

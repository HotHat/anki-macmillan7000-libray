junk
1 
jəNGk

▶noun
1 informal old or discarded articles that are considered useless or of little value. 
• informal worthless writing, talk, or ideas: I can't write this kind of junk. 
• informal a person's belongings, equipment, or baggage: I only have an hour to get all my junk together. 
• Finance junk bonds: he invested in junk.
2 informal heroin.
3 the lump of oily fibrous tissue in a sperm whale's head, containing spermaceti.
4 US vulgar slang a man's genitals. 
▶verb [with object] informal discard or abandon unceremoniously: sort out what could be sold off and junk the rest. ORIGIN late Middle English (denoting an old or inferior rope): of unknown origin. Sense
1 of the noun dates from the mid 19th century.

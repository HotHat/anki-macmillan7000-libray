therapy 
ˈTHerəpē

▶noun (plural therapies) treatment intended to relieve or heal a disorder: a course of antibiotic therapy 
cancer therapies. 
• the treatment of mental or psychological disorders by psychological means: he is currently in therapy 
[as modifier] : therapy sessions. ORIGIN mid 19th century: from modern Latin therapia, from Greek therapeia ‘healing,’ from therapeuein ‘minister to, treat medically.’

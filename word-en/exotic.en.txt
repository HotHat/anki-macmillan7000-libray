exotic 
iɡˈzädik

▶adjective originating in or characteristic of a distant foreign country: exotic birds 
they loved to visit exotic places. 
• attractive or striking because colorful or out of the ordinary: an exotic outfit 
(as noun the exotic) : there was a touch of the exotic in her appearance. 
• of a kind not used for ordinary purposes or not ordinarily encountered: exotic elementary particles as yet unknown to science. 
▶noun an exotic plant or animal: he planted exotics in the sheltered garden. DERIVATIVES exotically 
iɡˈzädək(ə)lē 
adverbORIGIN late 16th century: via Latin from Greek exōtikos ‘foreign,’ from exō ‘outside.’

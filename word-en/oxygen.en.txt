oxygen 
ˈäksəjən

▶noun a colorless, odorless reactive gas, the chemical element of atomic number
8 and the life-supporting component of the air. Oxygen forms about
20 percent of the earth's atmosphere, and is the most abundant element in the earth's crust, mainly in the form of oxides, silicates, and carbonates. (Symbol: O) DERIVATIVES oxygenous 
äkˈsijənəs 
adjectiveORIGIN late 18th century: from French (principe) oxygène ‘acidifying constituent’ (because at first it was held to be the essential component in the formation of acids).

antibody 
ˈan(t)əˌbädē

▶noun (plural antibodies) a blood protein produced in response to and counteracting a specific antigen. Antibodies combine chemically with substances that the body recognizes as alien, such as bacteria, viruses, and foreign substances in the blood. ORIGIN early 20th century: from anti- + body, translating German Antikörper, from anti- ‘against’ + Körper ‘body.’

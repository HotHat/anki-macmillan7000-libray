adviser 
ədˈvīzər 
(also advisor) 
▶noun a person who gives advice, typically someone who is expert in a particular field: the military adviser to the president. 
• chiefly North American (in a school, college, or university) a teacher or staff counselor who helps a student plan a course of study: my adviser might switch me back into Wasserman's class. usage: The spellings adviser and advisor are both correct. Adviser is more common, but advisor is also widely used, especially in North America. Adviser may be seen as less formal, while advisor often suggests an official position.

recede 
rəˈsēd

▶verb [no object] go or move back or further away from a previous position: the flood waters had receded 
his footsteps receded down the corridor. 
• (of a quality, feeling, or possibility) gradually diminish: the prospects of an early end to the war receded. 
• (of a man's hair) cease to grow at the temples and above the forehead: his dark hair was receding a little 
(as adjective receding) : a receding hairline. 
• (of a man) begin to go bald at the temples or above the forehead: Fred was receding a bit. 
• (usually as adjective receding) (of a facial feature) slope backward: a slightly receding chin. 
• (recede from) archaic withdraw from (an undertaking, promise, or agreement). ORIGIN late 15th century (in the sense ‘depart from (a usual state or standard)’): from Latin recedere, from re- ‘back’ + cedere ‘go.’

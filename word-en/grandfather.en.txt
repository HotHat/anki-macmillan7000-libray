grandfather 
ˈɡran(d)ˌfäT͟Hər

▶noun the father of one's father or mother. 
• the person who founded or originated something: Freud is often called the grandfather of psychoanalysis. 
▶verb [with object] North American informal exempt (someone or something) from a new law or regulation: smokers who worked here before the ban have been grandfathered. DERIVATIVES grandfatherly adjective

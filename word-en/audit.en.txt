audit 
ˈôdət

▶noun an official inspection of an individual's or organization's accounts, typically by an independent body. 
• a systematic review or assessment of something: a complete audit of flora and fauna at the site. 
▶verb (audits, auditing, audited) [with object]
1 conduct an official financial examination of (an individual's or organization's accounts): companies must have their accounts audited. 
• conduct a systematic review of: auditing obstetrical and neonatal care.
2 North American attend (a class) informally, not for academic credit. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin auditus ‘hearing,’ from audire ‘hear,’ in medieval Latin auditus (compoti)‘audit (of an account),’ an audit originally being presented orally.

impossible 
imˈpäsəb(ə)l

▶adjective not able to occur, exist, or be done: a seemingly impossible task 
[with infinitive] : it was almost impossible to keep up with him. 
• very difficult to deal with: she was in an impossible situation. 
• informal (of a person) very unreasonable: “Impossible woman!” the doctor complained. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French, or from Latin impossibilis, from in- ‘not’ + possibilis (see possible) .

census 
ˈsensəs

▶noun (plural censuses) an official count or survey of a population, typically recording various details of individuals: population estimates extrapolated from the 1981 census 
[as modifier] : census data. ORIGIN early 17th century (denoting a poll tax): from Latin, applied to the registration of citizens and property in ancient Rome, usually for taxation, from censere ‘assess.’ The current sense dates from the mid 18th century.

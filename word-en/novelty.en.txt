novelty 
ˈnävəltē

▶noun (plural novelties)
1 the quality of being new, original, or unusual: the novelty of being a married woman wore off. 
• a new or unfamiliar thing or experience: in 1914 air travel was still a novelty. 
• [as modifier] denoting something intended to be amusing as a result of its new or unusual quality: a novelty teapot.
2 a small and inexpensive toy or ornament: he bought chocolate novelties to decorate the Christmas tree. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French novelte, from novel ‘new, fresh’ (see novel2) .

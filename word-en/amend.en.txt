amend 
əˈmend

▶verb [with object] make minor changes in (a text) in order to make it fairer, more accurate, or more up-to-date: the rule was amended to apply only to nonmembers. 
• modify formally, as a legal document or legislative bill: did she amend her original will later on? 
pressuring Panama to amend its banking laws. 
• make better; improve: if you can amend or alter people's mindset. 
• archaic put right: a few things had gone wrong, but these had been amended. DERIVATIVES amendable 
əˈmendəb(ə)l 
adjective. amender nounORIGIN Middle English: from Old French amender, based on Latin emendare (see emend) .

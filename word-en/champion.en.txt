champion 
ˈCHampēən

▶noun
1 a person who has defeated or surpassed all rivals in a competition, especially in sports: [as modifier] : a champion hurdler.
2 a person who fights or argues for a cause or on behalf of someone else: a champion of women's rights. 
• historical a knight who fought in single combat on behalf of the monarch. 
▶verb [with object] support the cause of; defend: priests who championed human rights. ORIGIN Middle English (denoting a fighting man): from Old French, from medieval Latin campio(n-) ‘fighter,’ from Latin campus (see camp1) .

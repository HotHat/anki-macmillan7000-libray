industry 
ˈindəstrē

▶noun (plural industries)
1 economic activity concerned with the processing of raw materials and manufacture of goods in factories: the competitiveness of American industry. 
• [with adjective or noun modifier] a particular form or branch of economic or commercial activity: the car industry 
the tourist industry. 
• [with adjective or noun modifier] informal an activity or domain in which a great deal of time or effort is expended: the Shakespeare industry.
2 hard work: the kitchen became a hive of industry. ORIGIN late Middle English ( sense 2): from French industrie or Latin industria ‘diligence.’

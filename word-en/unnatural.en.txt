unnatural 
ˌənˈnaCH(ə)rəl

▶adjective contrary to the ordinary course of nature; abnormal: death by unnatural causes. 
• not existing in nature; artificial: the artificial turf looks an unnatural green. 
• affected or stilted: the formal tone of the programs caused them to sound stilted and unnatural. 
• lacking feelings of kindness and sympathy that are considered to be natural: they condemned her as an unnatural woman. DERIVATIVES unnaturalness noun

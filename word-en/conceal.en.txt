conceal 
kənˈsēl

▶verb [with object] keep from sight; hide: a line of sand dunes concealed the distant sea. 
• keep (something) secret; prevent from being known or noticed: love that they had to conceal from others. DERIVATIVES concealable 
kənˈsēləb(ə)l 
adjectiveORIGIN Middle English: from Old French conceler, from Latin concelare, from con- ‘completely’ + celare ‘hide.’

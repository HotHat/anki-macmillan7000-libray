persuasive 
pərˈswāsiv pərˈswāziv

▶adjective good at persuading someone to do or believe something through reasoning or the use of temptation: an informative and persuasive speech. DERIVATIVES persuasiveness 
pərˈswāsivnis pərˈswāzivnis 
nounORIGIN late 15th century: from French persuasif, -ive or medieval Latin persuasivus, from persuas- ‘convinced by reasoning,’ from the verb persuadere (see persuade) .

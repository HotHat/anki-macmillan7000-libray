suspect 
▶verb
1 I suspected she'd made a mistake: have a suspicion, have a feeling, feel, (be inclined to) think, fancy, reckon, guess, surmise, conjecture, conclude, have a hunch; suppose, presume, deduce, infer, sense, imagine; fear.
2 he had no reason to suspect my honesty: doubt, distrust, mistrust, have misgivings about, be skeptical about, have qualms about, be suspicious of, be wary of, harbor reservations about.
▶noun a murder suspect: suspected person, accused, defendant.
▶adjective a suspect package: suspicious, dubious, doubtful, untrustworthy; odd, queer; informal fishy, funny, shady.QUOTE Major Strasser has been shot. Round up the usual suspects.Claude Rains as Captain Renault in Casablanca (1942)

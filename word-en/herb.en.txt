herb 
(h)ərb

▶noun
1 any plant with leaves, seeds, or flowers used for flavoring, food, medicine, or perfume: bundles of dried herbs 
[as modifier] : a formal herb garden.
2 Botany any seed-bearing plant that does not have a woody stem and dies down to the ground after flowering. ORIGIN Middle English: via Old French from Latin herba ‘grass, green crops, herb.’ Although herb has always been spelled with an h, pronunciation without it was usual in British English until the 19th century and is still standard in the U
S.

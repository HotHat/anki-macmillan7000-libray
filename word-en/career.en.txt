career 
kəˈrir

▶noun an occupation undertaken for a significant period of a person's life and with opportunities for progress. 
• the time spent by a person while committed to a particular profession: the end of a distinguished career in the navy. 
• the progress through history of an institution or organization: the court has had a checkered career. 
• [as modifier] working permanently in or committed to a particular profession: a career diplomat. 
• [as modifier] (of a woman) pursuing a profession outside of the home. 
▶verb [no object] move swiftly and in an uncontrolled way in a specified direction: the car careered across the road and went through a hedge. 
PHRASES
in full career archaic at full speed.ORIGIN mid 16th century (denoting a road or racecourse): from French carrière, from Italian carriera, based on Latin carrus ‘wheeled vehicle.’

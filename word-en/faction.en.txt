faction
1 
ˈfakSH(ə)n

▶noun a small, organized, dissenting group within a larger one, especially in politics: the left-wing faction of the party. 
• a state of conflict within an organization; dissension. ORIGIN late 15th century (denoting the action of doing or making something): via French from Latin factio(n-), from facere ‘do, make.’

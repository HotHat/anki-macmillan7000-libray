neglect 
nəˈɡlekt

▶verb [with object] fail to care for properly: the old churchyard has been sadly neglected. 
• not pay proper attention to; disregard: you neglect our advice at your peril. 
• [with infinitive] fail to do something: he neglected to write to her. 
▶noun the state or fact of being uncared for: animals dying through disease or neglect. 
• the action of not taking proper care of someone or something: she was accused of child neglect. 
• failure to do something: he was reported for neglect of duty. ORIGIN early 16th century: from Latin neglect- ‘disregarded,’ from the verb neglegere, from neg- ‘not’ + legere ‘choose, pick up.’

imagination 
iˌmajəˈnāSH(ə)n

▶noun the faculty or action of forming new ideas, or images or concepts of external objects not present to the senses: she'd never been blessed with a vivid imagination. 
• the ability of the mind to be creative or resourceful: technology gives workers the chance to use their imagination. 
• the part of the mind that imagines things: a girl who existed only in my imagination. ORIGIN Middle English: via Old French from Latin imaginatio(n-), from the verb imaginari ‘picture to oneself,’ from imago, imagin- ‘image.’

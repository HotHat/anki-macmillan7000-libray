conclusive 
kənˈklo͞osiv kənˈklo͞oziv

▶adjective (of evidence or argument) serving to prove a case; decisive or convincing: conclusive evidence 
the findings were by no means conclusive. 
• (of a victory) achieved easily or by a large margin. DERIVATIVES conclusiveness 
kənˈklo͞osivnəs kənˈklo͞ozivnəs 
nounORIGIN late 16th century (in the sense ‘summing up, concluding’): from late Latin conclusivus, from Latin conclus- ‘closed up,’ from the verb concludere (see conclusion) .

sincere 
sinˈsir

▶adjective (sincerer, sincerest) free from pretense or deceit; proceeding from genuine feelings: they offer their sincere thanks to Paul. 
• (of a person) saying what they genuinely feel or believe; not dishonest or hypocritical. DERIVATIVES sincereness nounORIGIN mid 16th century (also in the sense ‘not falsified, unadulterated’): from Latin sincerus ‘clean, pure.’

monarchy 
ˈmänərkē ˈmänˌärkē

▶noun (plural monarchies) a form of government with a monarch at the head. 
• a state that has a monarch. 
• (the monarchy) the monarch and royal family of a country: the monarchy is the focus of loyalty and service. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French monarchie, via late Latin from Greek monarkhia ‘the rule of one.’

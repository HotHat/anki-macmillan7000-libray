auditor 
ˈôdədər

▶noun
1 a person who conducts an audit.
2 a listener: so low was Jim's voice that his auditors had to give it close attention. 
• North American a person who attends a class informally without working for academic credit. DERIVATIVES auditorial 
ˌôdəˈtôrēəl 
adjectiveORIGIN Middle English: from Old French auditeur, from Latin auditor, from audire ‘to hear.’

balcony 
ˈbalkənē

▶noun (plural balconies)
1 a platform enclosed by a wall or balustrade on the outside of a building, with access from an upper-floor window or door.
2 (the balcony) the upstairs seats in a theater, concert hall, or auditorium. DERIVATIVES balconied 
ˈbalk(ə)nēd 
adjectiveORIGIN early 17th century: from Italian balcone, probably ultimately of Germanic origin.

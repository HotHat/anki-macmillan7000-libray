gallon 
ˈɡalən

▶noun
1 a unit of volume for liquid measure equal to four quarts, in particular: 
• US equivalent to 3.79 liters. 
• (also imperial gallon) British equivalent to 4.55 liters (also used for dry measure).
2 (gallons of) informal a large volume: gallons of fake blood. DERIVATIVES gallonage 
-nij 
nounORIGIN Middle English: from Anglo-Norman French galon, from the base of medieval Latin galleta, galletum ‘pail, liquid measure,’ perhaps of Celtic origin.

downward 
ˈdounwərd

▶adverb (also downwards) toward a lower place, point, or level: he was lying face downward. 
• used to indicate that something applies to everyone in a certain hierarchy or set: new rules on sick leave affect employees of all grades, from managers downward. 
▶adjective moving or leading toward a lower place or level: the downward curve of the stairs 
a downward trend in inflation. ORIGIN Middle English: shortening of Old English adūnweard.

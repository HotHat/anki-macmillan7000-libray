resignation 
ˌrezəɡˈnāSH(ə)n

▶noun
1 an act of retiring or giving up a position: he announced his resignation. 
• a document conveying someone's intention of retiring: I'm thinking of handing in my resignation. 
• Chess an act of ending a game by conceding defeat without being checkmated.
2 the acceptance of something undesirable but inevitable: a shrug of resignation. ORIGIN late Middle English: via Old French from medieval Latin resignatio(n-), from resignare ‘unseal, cancel’ (see resign) .

oppression 
əˈpreSHən

▶noun prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control: a region shattered by oppression and killing. 
• the state of being subject to unjust treatment or control. 
• mental pressure or distress: her mood had initially been alarm and a sense of oppression. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French, from Latin oppressio(n-), from the verb opprimere (see oppress) .

accumulate 
əˈkyo͞om(y)əˌlāt

▶verb [with object] gather together or acquire an increasing number or quantity of: investigators have yet to accumulate enough evidence. 
• gradually gather or acquire (a resulting whole): her goal was to accumulate a huge fortune. 
• [no object] gather or build up: the toxin accumulated in their bodies. ORIGIN late 15th century: from Latin accumulat- ‘heaped up,’ from the verb accumulare, from ad- ‘to’ + cumulus ‘a heap.’

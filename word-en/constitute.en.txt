constitute 
ˈkänstəˌt(y)o͞ot

▶verb [with object]
1 be (a part) of a whole: single parents constitute a great proportion of the poor. 
• (of people or things) combine to form (a whole): there were enough members present to constitute a quorum. 
• be or be equivalent to (something): his failure to act constituted a breach of duty.
2 (usually be constituted) give legal or constitutional form to (an institution); establish by law. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin constitut- ‘established, appointed,’ from the verb constituere, from con- ‘together’ + statuere ‘set up.’

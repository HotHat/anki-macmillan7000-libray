incur 
inˈkər

▶verb (incurs, incurring, incurred) [with object] become subject to (something unwelcome or unpleasant) as a result of one's own behavior or actions: I will pay any expenses incurred. DERIVATIVES incurrence 
-əns 
nounORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin incurrere, from in- ‘toward’ + currere ‘run.’

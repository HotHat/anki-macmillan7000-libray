procedure 
prəˈsējər

▶noun an established or official way of doing something: the police are now reviewing procedures 
rules of procedure. 
• a series of actions conducted in a certain order or manner: the standard procedure for informing new employees about conditions of work. 
• a surgical operation: the procedure is carried out under general anesthesia. 
• Computing another term for subroutine. ORIGIN late 16th century: from French procédure, from procéder (see proceed) .

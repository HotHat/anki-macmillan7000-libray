altitude 
ˈaltəˌt(y)o͞od 
(abbreviation alt.) 
▶noun the height of an object or point in relation to sea level or ground level: flight data including airspeed and altitude 
flying at altitudes over 15,000 feet. 
• great height: the mechanism can freeze at altitude. 
• Astronomy the apparent height of a celestial object above the horizon, measured as an angle. 
• Geometry the length of the perpendicular line from a vertex to the opposite side of a figure. DERIVATIVES altitudinal

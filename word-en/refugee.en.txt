refugee 
ˌrefyo͝oˈjē

▶noun a person who has been forced to leave their country in order to escape war, persecution, or natural disaster: tens of thousands of refugees fled their homes 
[as modifier] : a refugee camp. ORIGIN late 17th century: from French réfugié ‘gone in search of refuge,’ past participle of (se) réfugier, from refuge (see refuge) .

lion 
ˈlīən

▶noun
1 a large tawny-colored cat that lives in prides, found in Africa and northwestern India. The male has a flowing shaggy mane and takes little part in hunting, which is done cooperatively by the females. lion[Panthera leo, family Felidae.] 
• (the Lion) the zodiacal sign or constellation Leo. 
• a brave or strong person. 
• an influential or celebrated person: a literary lion. 
• the lion as an emblem (e.g., of English or Scottish royalty) or as a charge in heraldry.
2 (Lion) a member of a Lions Club. 
PHRASES
throw someone to the lions cause someone to be in an extremely dangerous or unpleasant situation. [with reference to the throwing of Christians to the lions in Roman times.]DERIVATIVES lionlike 
-ˌlīk 
adjectiveORIGIN Middle English: from Anglo-Norman French liun, from Latin leo, leon-, from Greek leōn, leont- .

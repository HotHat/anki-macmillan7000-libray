anger 
ˈaNGɡər

▶noun a strong feeling of annoyance, displeasure, or hostility: the colonel's anger at his daughter's disobedience. 
▶verb [with object] fill (someone) with anger; provoke anger in: she was angered by his terse answer 
[with object and infinitive] : I was angered to receive a further letter from them 
[with object and clause] : he was angered that he had not been told. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old Norse angr ‘grief,’ angra ‘vex.’ The original use was in the Old Norse senses; current senses date from late Middle English.

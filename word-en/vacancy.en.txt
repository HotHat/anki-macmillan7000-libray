vacancy 
ˈvākənsē

▶noun (plural vacancies)
1 an unoccupied position or job: a vacancy for a shorthand typist. 
• an available room in a hotel or other establishment providing accommodations.
2 empty space: Cathy stared into vacancy, seeing nothing. 
• emptiness of mind; lack of intelligence or understanding: vacancy, vanity, and inane deception. 
• Crystallography a defect in a crystal lattice, consisting of the absence of an atom or an ion from a position where there should be one.

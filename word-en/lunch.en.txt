lunch 
lən(t)SH

▶noun a meal eaten in the middle of the day, typically one that is lighter or less formal than an evening meal: a vegetarian lunch 
do join us for lunch. 
▶verb [no object] eat lunch: he told his wife he was lunching with a client. 
• [with object] take (someone) out for lunch: public relations people lunch their clients there. 
PHRASES
do lunch informal, chiefly North American meet for lunch. out to lunch informal unaware of or inattentive to present conditions. there's no such thing as a free lunch proverb it isn't possible to get something for nothing.DERIVATIVES luncher nounORIGIN early 19th century: abbreviation of luncheon .

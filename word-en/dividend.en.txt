dividend 
ˈdivəˌdend

▶noun
1 a sum of money paid regularly (typically quarterly) by a company to its shareholders out of its profits (or reserves). 
• a payment divided among a number of people, e.g., members of a cooperative or creditors of an insolvent estate. 
• an individual's share of a dividend. 
• (dividends) a benefit from an action or policy: persistence pays dividends. See also peace dividend.
2 Mathematics a number to be divided by another number. ORIGIN late 15th century (in the general sense ‘portion, share’): from Anglo-Norman French dividende, from Latin dividendum ‘something to be divided,’ from the verb dividere (see divide) .

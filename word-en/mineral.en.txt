mineral 
ˈmin(ə)rəl

▶noun
1 a solid inorganic substance of natural occurrence. 
• a substance obtained by mining. 
• an inorganic substance needed by the human body for good health.
2 (minerals) British (in commercial use) effervescent soft drinks. 
▶adjective of or denoting a mineral: mineral ingredients such as zinc oxide. ORIGIN late Middle English: from medieval Latin minerale, neuter (used as a noun) of mineralis, from minera ‘ore.’

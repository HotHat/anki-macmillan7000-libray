offshore 
ˌôfˈSHôr ˌäfˈSHôr

▶adjective & adverb
1 situated at sea some distance from the shore: [as adjective] : this huge stretch of coastline is dominated by offshore barrier islands 
[as adverb] : we dropped anchor offshore. 
• (of the wind) blowing toward the sea from the land. 
• relating to the business of extracting oil or gas from the seabed: offshore drilling.
2 made, situated, or conducting business abroad, especially in order to take advantage of lower costs or less stringent regulation: [as adjective] : deposits in offshore accounts. 
• relating to or derived from a foreign country: [as adjective] : offshore politics. 
▶verb [with object] relocate (a business or department) to a foreign country to take advantage of lower costs: firms had offshored some activities by early 2004.

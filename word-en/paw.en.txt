paw 
pô

▶noun an animal's foot having claws and pads. 
• chiefly derogatory a person's hand: touch her with your filthy paws and I'll ram my fist into your face. 
▶verb [with object] (of an animal) feel or scrape with a paw or hoof: the horse rose on its strong haunches, its forelegs pawing the air 
[no object] : young dogs may paw at the floor and whine. 
• informal (of a person) touch or handle clumsily or lasciviously: some overweight, ugly Casanova had tried to paw her. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French poue, probably of Germanic origin and related to Dutch poot .

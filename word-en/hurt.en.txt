hurt 
hərt

▶verb (past and past participle hurt) [with object] cause physical pain or injury to: Ow! You're hurting me! 
[no object] : does acupuncture hurt? 
• [no object] (of a part of the body) suffer pain: my back hurts. 
• cause mental pain or distress to (a person or their feelings): she didn't want to hurt his feelings. 
• [no object] (of a person) feel mental pain or distress: he was hurting badly, but he smiled through his tears. 
• be detrimental to: high interest rates are hurting the local economy. 
• [no object] (hurt for) North American informal have a pressing need for: Frank wasn't hurting for money. 
▶adjective physically injured: he complained of a hurt leg and asked his trainer to stop the fight 
dogs and cats with hurt paws. 
• distressed or offended by another person's behavior: “You know I care,” he said, in a hurt voice 
I felt so hurt and alone. 
▶noun physical injury; harm. 
• mental pain or distress: the hurt of being constantly ignored 
wariness that masked a hurt. ORIGIN Middle English (originally in the senses ‘to strike’ and ‘a blow’): from Old French hurter (verb), hurt (noun), perhaps ultimately of Germanic origin.

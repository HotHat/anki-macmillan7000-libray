intact 
inˈtakt

▶adjective [often as complement] not damaged or impaired in any way; complete: the church was almost in ruins, but its tower remained intact. DERIVATIVES intactness 
inˈtak(t)nəs 
nounORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin intactus, from in- ‘not’ + tactus (past participle of tangere ‘touch’).

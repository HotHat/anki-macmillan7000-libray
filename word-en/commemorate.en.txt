commemorate 
kəˈmeməˌrāt

▶verb [with object] recall and show respect for (someone or something): a wreath-laying ceremony to commemorate the war dead 
a stone commemorating a boy who died at sea. 
• celebrate (an event, a person, or a situation) by doing or building something: it was a night commemorated in a song. DERIVATIVES commemorator 
-ˌrātər 
nounORIGIN late 16th century: from Latin commemorat- ‘brought to remembrance,’ from the verb commemorare, from com- ‘altogether’ + memorare ‘relate’ (from memor ‘mindful’).

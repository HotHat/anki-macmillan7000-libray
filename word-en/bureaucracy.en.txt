bureaucracy 
byo͝oˈräkrəsē

▶noun (plural bureaucracies) a system of government in which most of the important decisions are made by state officials rather than by elected representatives. 
• a state or organization governed or managed as a bureaucracy. 
• the officials in a bureaucracy, considered as a group or hierarchy. 
• excessively complicated administrative procedure, seen as characteristic of bureaucracy: the unnecessary bureaucracy in local government. ORIGIN early 19th century: from French bureaucratie, from bureau (see bureau, -cracy) .

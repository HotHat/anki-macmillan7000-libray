organism 
ˈôrɡəˌnizəm

▶noun an individual animal, plant, or single-celled life form. 
• the material structure of an individual life form: the heart's contribution to the maintenance of the human organism. 
• a whole with interdependent parts, likened to a living being: the upper strata of the American social organism. DERIVATIVES organismal 
ˌôrɡəˈnizməl 
adjective. organismic 
ˌôrɡəˈnizmik 
adjectiveORIGIN early 18th century (in the sense ‘organization,’ from organize): current senses derive from French organisme .

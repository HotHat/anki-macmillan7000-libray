supervise 
ˈso͞opərˌvīz

▶verb [with object] observe and direct the execution of (a task, project, or activity): the sergeant left to supervise the loading of the trucks. 
• observe and direct the work of (someone): nurses were supervised by a consulting psychiatrist. 
• keep watch over (someone) in the interest of their or others' security: prisoners were supervised by two officers. ORIGIN late 15th century (in the sense ‘survey, peruse’): from medieval Latin supervis- ‘surveyed, supervised,’ from supervidere, from super- ‘over’ + videre ‘to see.’

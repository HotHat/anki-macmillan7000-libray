notion 
ˈnōSH(ə)n

▶noun
1 a conception of or belief about something: children have different notions about the roles of their parents 
I had no notion of what her words meant.
2 an impulse or desire, especially one of a whimsical kind: she had a notion to call her friend at work.
3 (notions) chiefly North American items used in sewing, such as buttons, pins, and hooks. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin notio(n-) ‘idea,’ from notus ‘known,’ past participle of noscere .

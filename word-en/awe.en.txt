awe 
ô

▶noun a feeling of reverential respect mixed with fear or wonder: they gazed in awe at the small mountain of diamonds 
the sight filled me with awe 
his staff members are in awe of him. 
• archaic capacity to inspire awe: is it any wonder that Christmas Eve has lost its awe? 
▶verb [with object] (usually be awed) inspire with awe: they were both awed by the vastness of the forest. ORIGIN Old English ege ‘terror, dread, awe,’ replaced in Middle English by forms related to Old Norse agi .

stumble 
ˈstəmbəl

▶verb [no object] trip or momentarily lose one's balance; almost fall: her foot caught a shoe and she stumbled. 
• [with adverbial of direction] trip repeatedly as one walks: his legs still weak, he stumbled after them. 
• make a mistake or repeated mistakes in speaking: she stumbled over the words. 
• (stumble across/on/upon) find or encounter by chance: they stumbled across a farmer selling 25 acres. 
▶noun an act of stumbling. 
• a stumbling walk: he parodied my groping stumble across the stage. DERIVATIVES stumbler

fraud 
frôd

▶noun wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain: he was convicted of fraud 
prosecutions for social security frauds. 
• a person or thing intended to deceive others, typically by unjustifiably claiming or being credited with accomplishments or qualities: mediums exposed as tricksters and frauds. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French fraude, from Latin fraus, fraud- ‘deceit, injury.’

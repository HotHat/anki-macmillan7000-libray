manuscript 
ˈmanyəˌskript

▶noun a book, document, or piece of music written by hand rather than typed or printed: an illuminated manuscript. 
• an author's text that has not yet been published: preparing the final manuscript 
her autobiography remained in manuscript. ORIGIN late 16th century: from medieval Latin manuscriptus, from manu ‘by hand’ + scriptus ‘written’ (past participle of scribere).

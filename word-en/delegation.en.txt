delegation 
ˌdeləˈɡāSH(ə)n

▶noun
1 [treated as singular or plural] a body of delegates or representatives; a deputation: a delegation of teachers.
2 the act or process of delegating or being delegated: prioritizing tasks for delegation. ORIGIN early 17th century (denoting the act or process of delegating; also in the sense ‘delegated power’): from Latin delegatio(n-), from delegare ‘send on a commission’ (see delegate) .

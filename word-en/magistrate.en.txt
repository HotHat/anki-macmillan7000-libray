magistrate 
ˈmajəˌstrāt

▶noun a civil officer or lay judge who administers the law, especially one who conducts a court that deals with minor offenses and holds preliminary hearings for more serious ones. DERIVATIVES magistrature 
ˈmajəˌstrāCHər ˈmajəstrəˌCHər 
nounORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin magistratus ‘administrator,’ from magister ‘master.’

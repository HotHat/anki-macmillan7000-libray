prisoner 
ˈpriz(ə)nər

▶noun a person legally held in prison as a punishment for crimes they have committed or while awaiting trial. 
• a person captured and kept confined by an enemy, opponent, or criminal: the heroine was being held prisoner in a cave 
200 rebels were taken prisoner. 
• a person who is or feels confined or trapped by a situation or set of circumstances: he's become a prisoner of the publicity he's generated. 
PHRASES
take no prisoners be ruthlessly aggressive or uncompromising in the pursuit of one's objectives.ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French prisonier, from prison (see prison) .

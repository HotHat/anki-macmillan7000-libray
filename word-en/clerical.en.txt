clerical 
ˈklerək(ə)l

▶adjective
1 (of a job or person) concerned with or relating to work in an office, especially routine documentation and administrative tasks: temps are always needed for clerical work.
2 relating to the clergy: he was still attired in his clerical outfit. DERIVATIVES clericalism 
ˈklerəkəlˌizəm 
noun clerical ( sense 2) . clericalist 
ˈklerəkələst 
noun clerical ( sense 2) . clerically adverbORIGIN late 15th century ( sense 2): from ecclesiastical Latin clericalis, from clericus ‘clergyman’ (see cleric) .

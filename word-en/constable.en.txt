constable 
ˈkänstəb(ə)l

▶noun
1 a peace officer with limited policing authority, typically in a small town. 
• British a police officer.
2 the governor of a royal castle. 
• historical the highest-ranking official in a royal household. ORIGIN Middle English ( sense 2): from Old French conestable, from late Latin comes stabuli ‘count (head officer) of the stable.’

ratio 
ˈrāSHēˌō

▶noun (plural ratios) the quantitative relation between two amounts showing the number of times one value contains or is contained within the other: the ratio of men's jobs to women's is
8 to 1. 
• the relative value of silver and gold in a bimetallic system of currency. ORIGIN mid 17th century: from Latin, literally ‘reckoning,’ from rat- ‘reckoned,’ from the verb reri .

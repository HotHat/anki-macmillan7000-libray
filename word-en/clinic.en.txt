clinic 
ˈklinik

▶noun
1 an establishment or hospital department where outpatients are given medical treatment or advice, especially of a specialist nature: a mental health clinic. 
• an occasion or time when outpatients are given medical treatment: we're now holding regular clinics. 
• a gathering at a hospital bedside for the teaching of medicine or surgery.
2 a conference or short course on a particular subject: a ski clinic. ORIGIN mid 19th century (in the sense ‘teaching of medicine at the bedside’): from French clinique, from Greek klinikē (tekhnē)‘bedside (art),’ from klinē ‘bed.’

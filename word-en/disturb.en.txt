disturb 
dəˈstərb

▶verb [with object] interfere with the normal arrangement or functioning of: being sent to jail had apparently not disturbed his cheerfulness 
the site surface had been disturbed by bulldozer activity. 
• cause to feel anxious: I am disturbed by the document I have just read. 
• interrupt the sleep, relaxation, or privacy of: I'll see my patient now and we are not to be disturbed. DERIVATIVES disturber 
dəˈstərbər 
nounORIGIN Middle English: from Old French destourber, from Latin disturbare, from dis- ‘utterly’ + turbare ‘disturb’ (from turba ‘tumult’).

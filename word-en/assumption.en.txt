assumption 
əˈsəm(p)SH(ə)n

▶noun
1 a thing that is accepted as true or as certain to happen, without proof: they made certain assumptions about the market 
[with clause] : we're working on the assumption that the time of death was after midnight.
2 the action of taking or beginning to take power or responsibility: the assumption of an active role in regional settlements.
3 (Assumption) the reception of the Virgin Mary bodily into heaven. This was formally declared a doctrine of the Roman Catholic Church in 1950. See also Dormition. 
• the feast in honor of this, celebrated on August 15.
4 archaic arrogance or presumption. ORIGIN Middle English ( sense 3): from Old French asompsion or Latin assumptio(n-), from the verb assumere (see assume) .

speculation 
ˌspekyəˈlāSH(ə)n

▶noun
1 the forming of a theory or conjecture without firm evidence: there has been widespread speculation that he plans to quit 
this is pure speculation on my part 
these are only speculations.
2 investment in stocks, property, or other ventures in the hope of gain but with the risk of loss: the company's move into property speculation 
he was a millionaire from speculations on the stock market.

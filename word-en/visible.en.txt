visible 
ˈvizəb(ə)l

▶adjective
1 able to be seen: the church spire is visible from miles away. 
• Physics (of light) within the range of wavelengths to which the eye is sensitive. 
• able to be perceived or noticed easily: a visible improvement. 
• in a position of public prominence: a highly visible member of the royal entourage.
2 relating to imports or exports of tangible commodities: the visible trade gap. DERIVATIVES visibleness nounORIGIN Middle English: from Old French, or from Latin visibilis, from videre ‘to see.’

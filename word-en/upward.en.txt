upward 
ˈəpwərd

▶adverb (also upwards) toward a higher place, point, or level: she peered upward at the sky. 
▶adjective moving, pointing, or leading to a higher place, point, or level: an upward trend in sales. 
PHRASES
upwards (or upward) of more than: upwards of 3,500 copies 
Gooden can throw the ball at upward of 95 miles per hour.ORIGIN Old English upweard(es) (see up, -ward) .

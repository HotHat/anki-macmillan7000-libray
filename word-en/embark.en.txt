embark 
əmˈbärk

▶verb [no object] go on board a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle: he embarked for India in 1817. 
• [with object] put or take on board a ship or aircraft: its passengers were ready to be embarked. 
• (embark on/upon) begin (a course of action, especially one that is important or demanding): he embarked on a new career. DERIVATIVES embarkation 
ˌemˌbärˈkāSHən 
noun. embarkment nounORIGIN mid 16th century: from French embarquer, from em- ‘in’ + barque ‘bark, ship.’

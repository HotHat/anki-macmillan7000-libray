anywhere 
ˈenēˌ(h)wer

▶adverb [usually with negative or in questions] in or to any place: he couldn't be found anywhere. 
• [without negative] used for emphasis: I could go anywhere in the world. 
• used to indicate a range: this iron garden seat dates anywhere from 1890 to 1920 
she could have been anywhere between twenty-five and forty. 
▶pronoun any place: he doesn't have anywhere to live. 
PHRASES
anywhere near [with negative or in questions] (used for emphasis) at all near: I wouldn't dream of letting a surgeon anywhere near my eyes. 
• remotely close to in extent, level, or scope: imitations rarely look anywhere near as good as the real thing.

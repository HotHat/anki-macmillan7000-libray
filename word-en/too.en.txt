too 
to͞o

▶adverb
1 [as submodifier] to a higher degree than is desirable, permissible, or possible; excessively: he was driving too fast 
he wore suits that seemed a size too small for him. 
• informal very: you're too kind.
2 in addition; also: is he coming too? 
• moreover (used when adding a further point): she is a grown woman, and a strong one too. 
PHRASES
all too —— used to emphasize that something is the case to an extreme or unwelcome extent: failures are all too common. none too —— far from; not very: her sight's none too good. only too see only. too bad see bad. too far see far. too much see much. ORIGIN Old English, stressed form of to, spelled too from the 16th century.

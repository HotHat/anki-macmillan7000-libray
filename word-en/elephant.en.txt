elephant 
ˈeləfənt

▶noun (plural same or elephants)
1 a heavy plant-eating mammal with a prehensile trunk, long curved ivory tusks, and large ears, native to Africa and southern Asia. It is the largest living land animal. elephants [Family Elephantidae, order Proboscidea: two species. See African elephant, Indian elephant .]
2 chiefly British a size of paper, now standardized at 28 × 23 inches (approximately 711 × 584 mm). 
PHRASES
the elephant in the room a major problem or controversial issue that is obviously present but avoided as a subject for discussion because it is more comfortable to do so.DERIVATIVES elephantoid 
ˌeləˈfantoid, ˈeləfənˌtoid 
adjectiveORIGIN Middle English: from Old French elefant, via Latin from Greek elephas, elephant- ‘ivory, elephant.’

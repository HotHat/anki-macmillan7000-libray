coroner 
ˈkôrənər ˈkärənər

▶noun an official who investigates violent, sudden, or suspicious deaths. 
• historical in England, an official responsible for safeguarding the private property of the Crown. DERIVATIVES coronership 
-ˌSHip 
nounORIGIN Middle English: from Anglo-Norman French coruner, from corune ‘a crown’ (see crown); reflecting the Latin title custos placitorum coronae ‘guardian of the pleas of the crown.’

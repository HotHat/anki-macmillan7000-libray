hunger 
ˈhəNGɡər

▶noun a feeling of discomfort or weakness caused by lack of food, coupled with the desire to eat: she was faint with hunger. 
• a severe lack of food: they died from cold and hunger. 
• a strong desire or craving: her hunger for knowledge. 
▶verb [no object]
1 (hunger after/for) have a strong desire or craving for: all actors hunger for such a role.
2 archaic feel or suffer hunger through lack of food. ORIGIN Old English hungor (noun), hyngran (verb), of Germanic origin; related to Dutch honger and German Hunger .

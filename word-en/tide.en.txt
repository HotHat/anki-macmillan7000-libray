tide 
▶noun
1 ships come up the river with the tide: tidewater, ebb and flow, tidal flow.
2 the tide of history: course, movement, direction, trend, current, drift, run, turn, tendency, tenor.
PHRASES
tide someone over these canned goods should tide us over until the storm is over and the power is restored: sustain, keep someone going, keep someone afloat, keep someone's head above water, see someone through; keep the wolf from the door; help out, assist, aid. QUOTE Like we say in the sewer, "time and tide wait for no man."Art Carney as Ed Norton on the TV series The Honeymooners (1955–56)

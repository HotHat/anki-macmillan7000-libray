constraint 
kənˈstrānt

▶noun a limitation or restriction: the availability of water is the main constraint on food production 
time constraints make it impossible to do everything. 
• stiffness of manner and inhibition in relations between people: they would be able to talk without constraint. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘coercion’): from Old French constreinte, feminine past participle of constraindre (see constrain) .

conviction 
kənˈvikSH(ə)n

▶noun
1 a formal declaration that someone is guilty of a criminal offense, made by the verdict of a jury or the decision of a judge in a court of law: she had a previous conviction for a similar offense.
2 a firmly held belief or opinion: his conviction that the death was no accident 
she takes pride in stating her political convictions. 
• the quality of showing that one is firmly convinced of what one believes or says: his voice lacked conviction. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin convictio(n-), from the verb convincere (see convince) .

procession 
prəˈseSHən

▶noun
1 a number of people or vehicles moving forward in an orderly fashion, especially as part of a ceremony or festival: a funeral procession. 
• the action of moving forward as part of a ceremony: the fully robed civic dignitaries walk in procession. 
• a relentless succession of people or things: his path was paved by a procession of industry executives.
2 Theology the emanation of the Holy Spirit. ORIGIN late Old English, via Old French from Latin processio(n-), from procedere ‘move forward’ (see proceed) .

attractive 
əˈtraktiv

▶adjective (of a thing) pleasing or appealing to the senses: an attractive home 
foliage can be as attractive as flowers. 
• (of a person) appealing to look at; sexually alluring: an attractive, charismatic man. 
• (of a thing) having beneficial qualities or features that induce someone to accept what is being offered: the site is close to the high-rent district, which should make it attractive to developers. 
• relating to attraction between physical objects. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘absorbent’): from French attractif, -ive, from late Latin attractivus, from the verb attrahere (see attract) .

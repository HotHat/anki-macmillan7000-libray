editor 
ˈedədər

▶noun
1 a person who is in charge of and determines the final content of a text, particularly a newspaper or magazine: the editor of The New York Times 
a sports editor. 
• a person who works for a publishing company, commissioning or preparing material for publication.
2 a computer program enabling the user to enter or alter text. ORIGIN mid 17th century: from Latin, ‘producer (of games), publisher,’ from edit- ‘produced, put forth,’ from the verb edere .

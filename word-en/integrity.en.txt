integrity 
inˈteɡrədē

▶noun
1 the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles; moral uprightness: he is known to be a man of integrity.
2 the state of being whole and undivided: upholding territorial integrity and national sovereignty. 
• the condition of being unified, unimpaired, or sound in construction: the structural integrity of the novel. 
• internal consistency or lack of corruption in electronic data: [as modifier] : integrity checking. ORIGIN late Middle English ( sense 2): from French intégrité or Latin integritas, from integer ‘intact’ (see integer). Compare with entirety, integral, and integrate .

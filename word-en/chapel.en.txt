chapel 
ˈCHapəl

▶noun
1 a small building for Christian worship, typically one attached to an institution or private house: a service in the chapel 
attendance at chapel was compulsory. 
• a part of a large church or cathedral with its own altar and dedication. 
• a room or building in which funeral services are held. 
• British a place of worship for certain Protestant denominations.
2 British Printing the members or branch of a labor union at a particular place of work. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French chapele, from medieval Latin cappella, diminutive of cappa ‘cap or cape’ (the first chapel being a sanctuary in which St. Martin's cloak was preserved).

attendance 
əˈtendəns

▶noun the action or state of going regularly to or being present at a place or event: my attendance at church was very irregular. 
• the number of people present at a particular event, function, or meeting: reports placed the attendance at 500,000. 
PHRASES
in attendance present at a function or a place. 
• accompanying a member of royalty or the aristocracy in the capacity of an assistant or servant.ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French, from atendre ‘give one's attention to’ (see attend) .

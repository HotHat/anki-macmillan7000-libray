among 
əˈməNG 
(chiefly British also amongst 
əˈməNGst
) 
▶preposition
1 surrounded by; in the company of: wild strawberries hidden among the roots of the trees 
you're among friends.
2 being a member or members of (a larger set): he was among the first 29 students enrolled 
snakes are among the animals most feared by humans.
3 occurring in or practiced by (some members of a community): a drop in tooth decay among children 
this pronunciation is not popular among the general public 
rooting out abuses among the clergy. 
• involving most or all members of a group reciprocally: members of the government bickered among themselves.
4 indicating a division, choice, or differentiation involving three or more participants: the king called the three princesses to divide his kingdom among them 
choosing a privatization scheme from among five models. ORIGIN Old English ongemang (from on ‘in’ + gemang ‘assemblage, mingling’). The -st of amongst represents -s (adverbial genitive) + -t probably by association with superlatives (as in against). usage: See usage at between .

isolation 
ˌīsəˈlāSH(ə)n

▶noun the process or fact of isolating or being isolated: the isolation of older people. 
• an instance of isolating something, especially a compound or microorganism. 
• [as modifier] denoting a hospital or ward for patients with contagious or infectious diseases. 
PHRASES
in isolation without relation to other people or things; separately: environmental problems must not be seen in isolation from social ones.ORIGIN mid 19th century: from isolate, partly on the pattern of French isolation .

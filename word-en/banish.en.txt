banish 
ˈbaniSH

▶verb [with object] send (someone) away from a country or place as an official punishment: they were banished to Siberia for political crimes. 
• forbid, abolish, or get rid of (something unwanted): it's perfectly feasible to banish the smoke without banning smoking 
all thoughts of romance were banished from her head. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French baniss-, lengthened stem of banir; ultimately of Germanic origin and related to ban1 .

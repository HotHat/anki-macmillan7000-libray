vowel 
ˈvou(ə)l

▶noun a speech sound that is produced by comparatively open configuration of the vocal tract, with vibration of the vocal cords but without audible friction and is a unit of the sound system of a language that forms the nucleus of a syllable. Contrasted with consonant. 
• a letter representing a vowel sound, such as a, e, i, o, u. DERIVATIVES voweled 
ˈvou(ə)ld 
(British vowelled) adjective [usually in combination] . vowelless adjectiveORIGIN Middle English: from Old French vouel, from Latin vocalis (littera)‘vocal (letter).’

accusation 
ˌakyəˈzāSH(ə)n

▶noun a charge or claim that someone has done something illegal or wrong: accusations of bribery. 
• the action or process of accusing someone: there was accusation in Brian's voice. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French, from Latin accusatio(n-), from accusare ‘call to account’ (see accuse) .

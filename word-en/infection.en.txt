infection 
inˈfekSH(ə)n

▶noun the process of infecting or the state of being infected: strict hygiene will limit the risk of infection. 
• an infectious disease: a chest infection. 
• Computing the presence of a virus in, or its introduction into, a computer system. ORIGIN late Middle English: from late Latin infectio(n-), from Latin inficere ‘dip in, taint’ (see infect) .

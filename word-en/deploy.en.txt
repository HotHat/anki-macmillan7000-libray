deploy 
dəˈploi

▶verb [with object] move (troops or equipment) into position for military action: forces were deployed at strategic locations. 
• [no object] (of troops) move into position for military action: the air force began to deploy forward. 
• bring into effective action; utilize: they are not always able to deploy this skill. ORIGIN late 18th century: from French déployer, from Latin displicare and late Latin deplicare ‘unfold or explain,’ from dis-, de- ‘un-’ + plicare ‘to fold.’ Compare with display .

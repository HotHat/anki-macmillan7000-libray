redundant 
rəˈdəndənt

▶adjective not or no longer needed or useful; superfluous: this redundant brewery has been converted into a library 
many of the old skills had become redundant. 
• (of words or data) able to be omitted without loss of meaning or function: our peculiar affection for redundant phrases 
most of the inflectional endings are redundant. 
• Engineering (of a component) not strictly necessary to functioning but included in case of failure in another component. 
• chiefly British (of a person) no longer employed because there is no more work available: eight permanent staff were made redundant. DERIVATIVES redundantly adverbORIGIN late 16th century (in the sense ‘abundant’): from Latin redundant- ‘surging up,’ from the verb redundare (see redound) .

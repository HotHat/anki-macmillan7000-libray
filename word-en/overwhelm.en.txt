overwhelm 
ˌōvərˈ(h)welm

▶verb [with object] bury or drown beneath a huge mass: the water flowed through to overwhelm the whole dam and the village beneath. 
• defeat completely: his teams overwhelmed their opponents. 
• give too much of a thing to (someone); inundate: they were overwhelmed by farewell messages. 
• (usually be overwhelmed) have a strong emotional effect on: I was overwhelmed with guilt. 
• be too strong for; overpower: the wine doesn't overwhelm the flavor of the trout.

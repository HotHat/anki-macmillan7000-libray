ethnic 
ˈeTHnik

▶adjective relating to a population subgroup (within a larger or dominant national or cultural group) with a common national or cultural tradition: leaders of ethnic communities. 
• relating to national and cultural origins: two playwrights of different ethnic origins. 
• denoting origin by birth or descent rather than by present nationality: ethnic Albanians in Kosovo. 
• characteristic of or belonging to a non-Western cultural tradition: ethnic dishes 
folk and ethnic music. 
• archaic neither Christian nor Jewish; pagan or heathen. 
▶noun dated or offensive a member of an ethnic minority. ORIGIN late Middle English (denoting a person not of the Christian or Jewish faith): via ecclesiastical Latin from Greek ethnikos ‘heathen,’ from ethnos ‘nation.’ Current senses date from the 19th century.

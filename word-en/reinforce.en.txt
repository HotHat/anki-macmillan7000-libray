reinforce 
ˌrēinˈfôrs

▶verb [with object] strengthen or support (an object or substance), especially with additional material: the helmet has been reinforced with a double layer of cork. 
• strengthen (an existing feeling, idea, or habit): various actions of the leaders so reinforced fears and suspicions that war became unavoidable. 
• strengthen (a military force) with additional personnel or equipment: paratroopers were sent to reinforce the troops already in the area. DERIVATIVES reinforcer nounORIGIN late Middle English: from French renforcer, influenced by inforce, an obsolete spelling of enforce; the sense of providing military support is probably from Italian rinforzare .

crouch 
krouCH

▶verb [no object] adopt a position where the knees are bent and the upper body is brought forward and down, sometimes to avoid detection or to defend oneself: we crouched down in the trench 
she was crouching over some flower bed 
(be crouched) : Leo was crouched before the fire. 
▶noun [in singular] a crouching stance or posture. ORIGIN late Middle English: perhaps from Old French crochir ‘be bent,’ from croche (see crotch) .

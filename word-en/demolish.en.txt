demolish 
dəˈmäliSH

▶verb [with object] pull or knock down (a building). 
• comprehensively refute (an argument or its proponent): I looked forward keenly to demolishing my opponent. 
• informal overwhelmingly defeat (a player or team): they demolished the Denver Broncos, 55-10. 
• humorous eat up (food) quickly: we demolished the potato pancakes. DERIVATIVES demolisher nounORIGIN mid 16th century: from French démoliss-, lengthened stem of démolir, from Latin demoliri, from de- (expressing reversal) + moliri ‘construct’ (from moles ‘mass’).

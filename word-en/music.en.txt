music 
ˈmyo͞ozik

▶noun
1 vocal or instrumental sounds (or both) combined in such a way as to produce beauty of form, harmony, and expression of emotion: couples were dancing to the music 
baroque music. 
• the art or science of composing or performing music: he devoted his life to music. 
• a sound perceived as pleasingly harmonious: the background music of softly lapping water.
2 the written or printed signs representing vocal or instrumental sound: Tony learned to read music. 
• the score or scores of a musical composition or compositions: the music was open on a stand. 
PHRASES
face the music see face. music of the spheres see sphere. music to one's ears something that is pleasant or gratifying to hear or discover: the commission's report was music to the ears of the administration.ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French musique, via Latin from Greek mousikē (tekhnē)‘(art) of the Muses,’ from mousa ‘muse.’

entity 
ˈen(t)ədē

▶noun (plural entities) a thing with distinct and independent existence: church and empire were fused in a single entity. 
• existence; being: entity and nonentity. DERIVATIVES entitative 
-ˌtātiv 
adjective (chiefly Philosophy)ORIGIN late 15th century (denoting a thing's existence): from French entité or medieval Latin entitas, from late Latin ens, ent- ‘being’ (from esse ‘be’).

relevant 
ˈreləvənt

▶adjective closely connected or appropriate to what is being done or considered: what small companies need is relevant advice 
the candidate's experience is relevant to the job. 
• appropriate to the current time, period, or circumstances; of contemporary interest: critics may find themselves unable to stay relevant in a changing world 
her films are relevant for feminists today. DERIVATIVES relevancy 
-vənsē 
noun. relevantly adverbORIGIN early 16th century (as a Scots legal term meaning ‘legally pertinent’): from medieval Latin relevant- ‘raising up,’ from Latin relevare .

economics 
ˌekəˈnämiks ˌēkəˈnämiks

▶pl. noun [often treated as singular]
1 the branch of knowledge concerned with the production, consumption, and transfer of wealth.
2 the condition of a region or group as regards material prosperity: he is responsible for the island's modest economics. ORIGIN late 16th century (denoting the science of household management): from economic + the plural suffix -s, originally on the pattern of Greek ta oikonomika (plural), the name of a treatise by Aristotle. Current senses date from the late 18th century.

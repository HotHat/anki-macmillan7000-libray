boom
1 
bo͞om

▶noun a loud, deep, resonant sound: the deep boom of the bass drum. 
• the characteristic resonant call of the bittern. 
▶verb [no object] make a loud, deep, resonant sound: thunder boomed in the sky 
her voice boomed out. 
• [with direct speech] say in a loud, deep, resonant voice: the imperative “Silence!” boomed out by Ray himself. 
• (of a bittern) utter its characteristic resonant call. DERIVATIVES boomy adjectiveORIGIN late Middle English (as a verb): ultimately imitative; perhaps from Dutch bommen ‘to hum, buzz.’

cellar 
ˈselər

▶noun a room below ground level in a house, typically one used for storing wine or coal. 
• a stock of wine. 
▶verb [with object] store (wine) in a cellar. ORIGIN Middle English (in the general sense ‘storeroom’): from Old French celier, from late Latin cellarium ‘storehouse,’ from cella ‘storeroom or chamber.’

parrot 
ˈperət

▶noun a bird, often vividly colored, with a short down-curved hooked bill, grasping feet, and a raucous voice, found especially in the tropics and feeding on fruits and seeds. Many are popular as cage birds, and some are able to mimic the human voice. parrot[Order Psittaciformes: numerous species, sometimes all placed in the family Psittacidae. The order also contains the cockatoos, lories, lovebirds, macaws, conures, and budgerigars.] 
▶verb (parrots, parroting, parroted) [with object] repeat mechanically: encouraging students to parrot back information. ORIGIN early 16th century: probably from dialect French perrot, diminutive of the male given name Pierre ‘Peter.’ Compare with parakeet .

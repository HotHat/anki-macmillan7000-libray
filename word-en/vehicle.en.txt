vehicle 
ˈvēək(ə)l ˈvēˌhik(ə)l

▶noun
1 a thing used for transporting people or goods, especially on land, such as a car, truck, or cart.
2 a thing used to express, embody, or fulfill something: I use paint as a vehicle for my ideas. 
• a substance that facilitates the use of a drug, pigment, or other material mixed with it. 
• the figurative language used in a metaphor, as distinct from the metaphor's subject. Often contrasted with tenor2 ( sense 1). 
• a movie, television program, song, etc., that is intended to display the leading performer to the best advantage. ORIGIN early 17th century: from French véhicule or Latin vehiculum, from vehere ‘carry.’

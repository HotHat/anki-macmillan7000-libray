accuse 
əˈkyo͞oz

▶verb [with object] charge (someone) with an offense or crime: he was accused of murdering his wife's lover. 
• claim that (someone) has done something wrong: he was accused of favoritism. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French acuser, from Latin accusare ‘call to account,’ from ad- ‘toward’ + causa ‘reason, motive, lawsuit.’

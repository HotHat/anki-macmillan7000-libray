forth 
fôrTH

▶adverb chiefly archaic out from a starting point and forward or into view: the plants will bush out, putting forth fresh shoots. 
• onward in time: from that day forth he gave me endless friendship. 
PHRASES
and so forth see so1. ORIGIN Old English, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch voort and German fort, from an Indo-European root shared by fore- .

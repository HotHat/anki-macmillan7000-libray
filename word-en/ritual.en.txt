ritual 
ˈriCH(o͞o)əl

▶noun a religious or solemn ceremony consisting of a series of actions performed according to a prescribed order: the ancient rituals of Christian worship 
the role of ritual in religion. 
• the prescribed order of performing a ceremony, especially one characteristic of a particular religion or church. 
• a series of actions or type of behavior regularly and invariably followed by someone: her visits to Joy became a ritual. 
▶adjective [attributive] relating to or done as a religious or solemn rite: ritual burial. 
• (of an action) arising from convention or habit: the players gathered for the ritual pregame huddle. ORIGIN late 16th century (as an adjective): from Latin ritualis, from ritus (see rite) .

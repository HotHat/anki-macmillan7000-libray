reptile 
ˈreptəl ˈrepˌtīl

▶noun
1 a vertebrate animal of a class that includes snakes, lizards, crocodiles, turtles, and tortoises. They are distinguished by having a dry scaly skin and typically laying soft-shelled eggs on land. [Class Reptilia: orders Chelonia (turtles and tortoises), Squamata (snakes and lizards), Rhynchocephalia (the tuatara), and Crocodylia (crocodilians). Modern reptiles are cold-blooded, though among extinct groups, including dinosaurs, pterosaurs, and ichthyosaurs, some may have been warm-blooded.]
2 informal a person regarded with loathing and contempt. 
▶adjective [attributive] belonging to a reptile or to the class of reptiles: reptile eggs. ORIGIN late Middle English: from late Latin, neuter of reptilis, from Latin rept- ‘crawled,’ from the verb repere .

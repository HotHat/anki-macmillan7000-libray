dedicate 
ˈdedəˌkāt

▶verb [with object] (often dedicate something to) devote (time, effort, or oneself) to a particular task or purpose: Joan has dedicated her life to animals. 
• devote (something) to a particular subject or purpose: you should dedicate a telephone line to each modem you plan to install. 
• cite (a book or other artistic work) as being issued or performed in someone's honor: the novel is dedicated to the memory of my mother. 
• formally open or unveil (a building or memorial): the ex-president came to dedicate a $2.6 million recreation center. 
• ceremonially assign (a church or other building) to a deity or saint: the parish church is dedicated to St. Paul. DERIVATIVES dedicatee

opponent 
əˈpōnənt

▶noun someone who competes against or fights another in a contest, game, or argument; a rival or adversary: he beat his opponent by a landslide margin. 
• a person who disagrees with or resists a proposal or practice: an opponent of the economic reforms. ORIGIN late 16th century (denoting a person opening an academic debate by proposing objections to a philosophical or religious thesis): from Latin opponent- ‘setting against,’ from the verb opponere, from ob- ‘against’ + ponere ‘place.’

insight 
ˈinˌsīt

▶noun the capacity to gain an accurate and deep intuitive understanding of a person or thing: this paper is alive with sympathetic insight into Shakespeare. 
• a deep understanding of a person or thing: the signals would give marine biologists new insights into the behavior of whales. 
• Psychiatry new understanding by a mentally ill person of the causes of their disorder. ORIGIN Middle English (in the sense ‘inner sight, mental vision, wisdom’): probably of Scandinavian and Low German origin and related to Swedish insikt,Danish indsigt,Dutch inzicht, and German Einsicht .

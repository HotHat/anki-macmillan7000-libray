medicine 
ˈmedəsən

▶noun
1 the science or practice of the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of disease (in technical use often taken to exclude surgery).
2 a compound or preparation used for the treatment or prevention of disease, especially a drug or drugs taken by mouth: give her some medicine 
your doctor will be able to prescribe medicines. 
• healing substances or drugs collectively: an aid convoy loaded with food and medicine.
3 (among North American Indians and some other peoples) a spell, charm, or fetish believed to have healing, protective, or other power: Fleur was murdering him by use of bad medicine. 
PHRASES
give someone a dose (or taste) of their own medicine give someone the same bad treatment that they have given to others: tired of his humiliation of me, I decided to give him a taste of his own medicine. take one's medicine submit to something disagreeable such as punishment.ORIGIN Middle English: via Old French from Latin medicina, from medicus ‘physician.’

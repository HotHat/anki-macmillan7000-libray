persuasion 
pərˈswāZHən

▶noun
1 the action or fact of persuading someone or of being persuaded to do or believe something: Monica needed plenty of persuasion before she actually left.
2 a belief or set of beliefs, especially religious or political ones: writers of all political persuasions. 
• a group or sect holding a particular religious belief: the village had two chapels for those of the Methodist persuasion. 
• humorous any group or type of person or thing linked by a specified characteristic, quality, or attribute: an ancient gas oven of the enamel persuasion. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin persuasio(n-), from the verb persuadere (see persuade) .

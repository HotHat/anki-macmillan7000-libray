customary 
ˈkəstəˌmerē

▶adjective according to the customs or usual practices associated with a particular society, place, or set of circumstances: it is customary to mark an occasion like this with a toast. 
• according to a person's habitual practice: I put the kettle on for our customary cup of tea. 
• Law established by or based on custom rather than common law or statute. 
▶noun (plural customaries) historical another term for custumal. DERIVATIVES customariness nounORIGIN late Middle English (as a noun): from medieval Latin custumarius, from custuma, from Anglo-Norman French custume (see custom) .

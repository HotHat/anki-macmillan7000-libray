comma 
ˈkämə

▶noun
1 a punctuation mark (,) indicating a pause between parts of a sentence. It is also used to separate items in a list and to mark the place of thousands in a large numeral.comma 3
2 Music a minute interval or difference of pitch.
3 (also comma butterfly) a butterfly that has wings with irregular, ragged edges and typically a white or silver comma-shaped mark on the underside of each hind wing. [Genus Polygonia, subfamily Nymphalinae, family Nymphalidae: numerous species, in particular the common eastern comma (
P.  comma) of eastern North America.] ORIGIN late 16th century (originally as a term in rhetoric denoting a group of words shorter than a colon; see colon1): via Latin from Greek komma ‘piece cut off, short clause,’ from koptein ‘cut.’

dominate 
ˈdäməˌnāt

▶verb [with object] have a commanding influence on; exercise control over: the company dominates the market for operating system software. 
• be the most important or conspicuous person or thing in: the race was dominated by the 1992 champion. 
• (of something tall or high) have a commanding position over; overlook: a picturesque city dominated by the cathedral tower. DERIVATIVES dominator 
ˈdäməˌnādər 
nounORIGIN early 17th century: from Latin dominat- ‘ruled, governed,’ from the verb dominari, from dominus ‘lord, master.’

tariff 
ˈterəf

▶noun a tax or duty to be paid on a particular class of imports or exports. 
• a list of taxes on imports or exports. 
• chiefly British a list of the fixed charges made by a business, especially for use of gas, electricity, or a mobile phone. 
▶verb [with object] fix the price of (something) according to a tariff: these services are tariffed by volume. ORIGIN late 16th century (also denoting an arithmetical table): via French from Italian tariffa, based on Arabic ῾arrafa ‘notify.’

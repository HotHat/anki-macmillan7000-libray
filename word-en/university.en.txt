university 
ˌyo͞onəˈvərsədē

▶noun (plural universities) an educational institution designed for instruction, examination, or both, of students in many branches of advanced learning, conferring degrees in various faculties, and often embodying colleges and similar institutions: [in names] : Oxford University 
the University of California 
[as modifier] : the university buildings 
a university professor. 
PHRASES
at university chiefly British studying at a university. the university of life the experience of life regarded as a means of instruction.ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French universite, from Latin universitas ‘the whole,’ in late Latin ‘society, guild,’ from universus (see universe) .

massive 
ˈmasiv

▶adjective
1 large and heavy or solid: a massive rampart of stone.
2 exceptionally large: massive crowds are expected. 
• very intense or severe: a massive heart attack. 
• informal particularly successful or influential: the title song became a massive hit.
3 Geology (of rocks or beds) having no discernible form or structure. 
• (of a mineral) not visibly crystalline. DERIVATIVES massiveness nounORIGIN late Middle English: from French massif, -ive, from Old Fre massis, based on Latin massa (see mass) .

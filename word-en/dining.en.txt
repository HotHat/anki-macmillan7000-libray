dine 
dīn

▶verb [no object] eat dinner: we dined at a restaurant 
(as noun dining) : a dining area. 
• (dine out) eat dinner in a restaurant or the home of friends. 
• (dine on) eat (something) for dinner. 
• (dine out on) regularly entertain friends with (a humorous story or interesting piece of information): it should have been one of those stories one dines out on afterward. 
• [with object] take (someone) to dinner: I'll dine you soon. 
PHRASES
wine and dine see wine1. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French disner, probably from desjëuner ‘to break fast,’ from des- (expressing reversal) + jëun ‘fasting’ (from Latin jejunus).

legacy 
ˈleɡəsē

▶noun (plural legacies) an amount of money or property left to someone in a will. 
• a thing handed down by a predecessor: the legacy of centuries of neglect. 
▶adjective Computing denoting software or hardware that has been superseded but is difficult to replace because of its wide use. ORIGIN late Middle English (also denoting the function or office of a deputy, especially a papal legate): from Old French legacie, from medieval Latin legatia ‘legateship,’ from legatus ‘person delegated’ (see legate) .

video 
ˈvidēō

▶noun (plural videos)
1 the recording, reproducing, or broadcasting of moving visual images.
2 a recording of moving visual images made digitally or on videotape: they sat down to watch a video 
[as modifier] : a site on which people can post their own video clips. 
• a short movie made by a pop or rock group to accompany a song. 
• a videocassette: a blank video 
the film will soon be released on video. 
• British a videocassette recorder. 
▶verb (videos, videoing, videoed) record on videotape: he declined an invitation to be videoed. ORIGIN 1930s: from Latin videre ‘to see,’ on the pattern of audio.

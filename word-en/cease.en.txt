cease 
sēs

▶verb [no object] bring or come to an end: the hostilities had ceased and normal life was resumed 
[with infinitive] : on his retirement the job will cease to exist 
[with object] : they were asked to cease all military activity. 
PHRASES
never cease to (in hyperbolic use) do something very frequently: her exploits never cease to amaze me. without cease without stopping.ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French cesser, from Latin cessare ‘stop,’ from cedere ‘to yield.’

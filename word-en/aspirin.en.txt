aspirin 
ˈasp(ə)rən

▶noun a synthetic compound used medicinally to relieve mild or chronic pain and to reduce fever and inflammation. [Alternative name: acetylsalicylic acid; chemical formula: C 6H 4(OCOCH 3)COO
H. ] 
• (plural same or aspirins) a tablet containing aspirin. ORIGIN late 19th century: from German, from acetylierte Spirsäure ‘acetylated salicylic acid’ (the element Spir- being from the plant genus name Spiraea).

pond 
pänd

▶noun a small body of still water formed naturally or by hollowing or embanking. 
• (the pond) informal the Atlantic ocean: he's relatively unknown on this side of the pond. 
▶verb [with object] hold back or dam up (flowing water or another liquid) to form a small lake. 
• [no object] (of flowing water or other liquids) form a small lake: (as noun ponding) : where a path goes down into a dip, you'll have to ensure that ponding doesn't occur. ORIGIN Middle English: alteration of pound3, commonly used in dialect in the same sense.

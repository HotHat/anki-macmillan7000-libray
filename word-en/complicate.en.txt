complicate 
ˈkämpləˌkāt

▶verb [with object] make (something) more difficult or confusing by causing it to be more complex: middlemen can complicate the process 
(as adjective complicating) : a complicating factor. 
• Medicine introduce complications in (an existing condition): smoking may complicate pregnancy. ORIGIN early 17th century (in the sense ‘combine, entangle, intertwine’): from Latin complicat- ‘folded together,’ from the verb complicare, from com- ‘together’ + plicare ‘to fold.’

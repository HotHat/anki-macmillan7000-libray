pavement 
ˈpāvmənt

▶noun any paved area or surface. 
• North American the hard surface of a road or street. 
• British a sidewalk. 
• Geology a more or less horizontal expanse of bare rock. 
PHRASES
pound the pavement see pound2. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French, from Latin pavimentum ‘trodden-down floor,’ from pavire ‘beat, tread down.’

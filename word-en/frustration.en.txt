frustration 
frəˈstrāSH(ə)n

▶noun the feeling of being upset or annoyed, especially because of inability to change or achieve something: I sometimes feel like screaming with frustration. 
• an event or circumstance that causes one to have a feeling of frustration: the inherent frustrations of assembly line work. 
• the prevention of the progress, success, or fulfillment of something: the frustration of their wishes. ORIGIN mid 16th century: from Latin frustratio(n-), from frustrare ‘disappoint’ (see frustrate) .

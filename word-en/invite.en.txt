invite 
▶verb 
inˈvīt 
[with object] make a polite, formal, or friendly request to (someone) to go somewhere or to do something: we were invited to a dinner at the embassy 
[with object and infinitive] : she invited Patrick to sit down. 
• make a formal or polite request for (something, especially an application for a job or opinions on a particular topic) from someone. 
• (of an action or situation) tend to elicit (a particular reaction or response) or to tempt (someone) to do something: his use of the word did little but invite criticism. 
▶noun 
ˈinvīt 
informal an invitation. DERIVATIVES invitee 
inˌvīˈtē 
noun. inviter 
inˈvīdər 
nounORIGIN mid 16th century: from Old French inviter, or from Latin invitare .

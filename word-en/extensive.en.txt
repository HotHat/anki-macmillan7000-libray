extensive 
ikˈstensiv

▶adjective
1 covering or affecting a large area: an extensive garden. 
• large in amount or scale: an extensive collection of silver.
2 (of agriculture) obtaining a relatively small crop from a large area with a minimum of attention and expense: extensive farming techniques. Often contrasted with intensive ( sense
1 of the adjective). DERIVATIVES extensiveness 
ikˈstensivnəs 
nounORIGIN late Middle English: from French extensif, -ive or late Latin extensivus, from extens- ‘stretched out,’ from the verb extendere (see extend) .

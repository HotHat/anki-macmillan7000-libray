invitation 
ˌinvəˈtāSH(ə)n

▶noun a written or verbal request inviting someone to go somewhere or to do something: a wedding invitation. 
• the action of inviting someone to go somewhere or to do something: a club with membership by invitation only 
an herb garden where guests can go only at the invitation of the chef. 
• [in singular] a situation or action that tempts someone to do something or makes a particular outcome likely: tactics like those of the colonel would have been an invitation to disaster. ORIGIN late Middle English: from French, or from Latin invitatio(n-), from invitare (see invite) .

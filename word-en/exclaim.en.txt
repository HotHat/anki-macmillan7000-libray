exclaim 
ikˈsklām

▶verb [no object] cry out suddenly, especially in surprise, anger, or pain: she looked in the mirror, exclaiming in dismay at her appearance 
[with direct speech] : “Well, I never,” she exclaimed. ORIGIN late 16th century: from French exclamer or Latin exclamare, from ex- ‘out’ + clamare ‘to shout.’

topic 
ˈtäpik

▶noun a matter dealt with in a text, discourse, or conversation; a subject: her favorite topic of conversation is her partner. 
• Linguistics that part of a sentence about which something is said, typically the first major constituent. ORIGIN late 15th century (originally denoting a set or book of general rules or ideas): from Latin topica, from Greek ta topika, literally ‘matters concerning commonplaces’ (the title of a treatise by Aristotle), from topos ‘a place.’

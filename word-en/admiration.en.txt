admiration 
ˌadməˈrāSH(ə)n

▶noun respect and warm approval: their admiration for each other was genuine. 
• (the admiration of) something regarded as impressive or worthy of respect: her house was the admiration of everyone. 
• pleasurable contemplation: they were lost in admiration of the scenery. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘marveling, wonder’): from Latin admiratio(n-), from the verb admirari (see admire) .

formulate 
ˈfôrmyəˌlāt

▶verb [with object] create or devise methodically (a strategy or a proposal): economists and statisticians were needed to help formulate economic policy. 
• express (an idea) in a concise or systematic way: the argument is sufficiently clear that it can be formulated mathematically. ORIGIN mid 19th century: from formula + -ate3, on the pattern of French formuler, from medieval Latin formulare .

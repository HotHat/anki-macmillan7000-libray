brow
1 
brou

▶noun
1 a person's forehead: he wiped his brow. 
• (usually brows) an eyebrow: his brows lifted in surprise.
2 the summit of a hill or pass: the cottages were built on the brow of a hill. ORIGIN Old English brū ‘eyelash, eyebrow,’ of Germanic origin. The sense ‘forehead’ dates from Middle English; compare with brae .

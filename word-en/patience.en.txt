patience 
ˈpāSHəns

▶noun
1 the capacity to accept or tolerate delay, trouble, or suffering without getting angry or upset: you can find bargains if you have the patience to sift through the dross.
2 chiefly British term for solitaire ( sense 1). 
PHRASES
lose patience (or lose one's patience) become unable to keep one's temper: even Lawrence finally lost patience with him.ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French, from Latin patientia, from patient- ‘suffering,’ from the verb pati .

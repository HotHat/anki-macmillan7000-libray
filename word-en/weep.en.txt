weep 
wēp

▶verb (past and past participle wept 
wept
) [no object]
1 shed tears: a grieving mother wept over the body of her daughter 
[with object] : he wept bitter tears at her cruelty. 
• utter or express with tears: [with direct speech] : “No!” she wept. 
• [with object] archaic mourn for; shed tears over: a young widow weeping her lost lord.
2 exude liquid: she rubbed one of the sores, making it weep. 
▶noun [in singular] a fit or spell of shedding tears. ORIGIN Old English wēpan (verb), of Germanic origin, probably imitative.

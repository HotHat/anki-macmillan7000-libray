universe 
ˈyo͞onəˌvərs

▶noun (the universe) all existing matter and space considered as a whole; the cosmos. The universe is believed to be at least
10 billion light years in diameter and contains a vast number of galaxies; it has been expanding since its creation in the Big Bang about
13 billion years ago. 
• a particular sphere of activity, interest, or experience: the front parlor was the hub of her universe. 
• (Logic also universe of discourse) another term for universal set. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French univers or Latin universum, neuter of universus ‘combined into one, whole,’ from uni- ‘one’ + versus ‘turned’ (past participle of vertere).

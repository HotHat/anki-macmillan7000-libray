absurd 
əbˈsərd əbˈzərd

▶adjective wildly unreasonable, illogical, or inappropriate: the allegations are patently absurd 
so you think I'm a spy? How absurd! 
she was being absurd—and imagining things 
(as noun the absurd) : he had a keen eye for the incongruous and the absurd. 
• arousing amusement or derision; ridiculous: gym shorts and knee socks looked absurd on such a tall girl. ORIGIN mid 16th century: from Latin absurdus ‘out of tune,’ hence ‘irrational’; related to surdus ‘deaf, dull.’

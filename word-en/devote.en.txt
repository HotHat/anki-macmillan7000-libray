devote 
dəˈvōt

▶verb [with object]
1 (devote something to) give all or a large part of one's time or resources to (a person, activity, or cause): I wanted to devote more time to my family 
she devoted herself to fundraising.
2 archaic invoke or pronounce a curse upon. ORIGIN late 16th century (in the sense ‘dedicate formally, consecrate’): from Latin devot- ‘consecrated,’ from the verb devovere, from de- ‘formally’ + vovere ‘to vow.’

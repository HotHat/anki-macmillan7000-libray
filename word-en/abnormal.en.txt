abnormal 
abˈnôrməl

▶adjective deviating from what is normal or usual, typically in a way that is undesirable or worrying: the illness is recognizable from the patient's abnormal behavior. ORIGIN mid 19th century: alteration (by association with Latin abnormis ‘monstrosity’) of 16th-century anormal, from French, variant of anomal, via Latin from Greek anōmalos (see anomalous) .

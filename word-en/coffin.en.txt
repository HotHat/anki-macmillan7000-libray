coffin 
ˈkôfən ˈkäfən

▶noun a long, narrow box, typically of wood, in which a corpse is buried or cremated. 
• informal an old and unsafe aircraft or vessel. 
▶verb (coffins, coffining, coffined) [with object] put (a dead body) in a coffin. ORIGIN Middle English (in the general sense ‘box, chest, casket’): from Old French cofin ‘little basket or case,’ from Latin cophinus (see coffer) .

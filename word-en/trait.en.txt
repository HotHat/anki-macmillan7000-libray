trait 
trāt

▶noun a distinguishing quality or characteristic, typically one belonging to a person: he was a letter-of-the-law man, a common trait among coaches. 
• a genetically determined characteristic. ORIGIN mid 16th century: from French, from Latin tractus ‘drawing, pulling’ (see tract1). An early sense was ‘stroke of the pen or pencil in a picture,’ giving rise to the sense ‘a particular feature of mind or character’ (mid 18th century).

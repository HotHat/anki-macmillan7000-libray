doctrine 
ˈdäktrən

▶noun a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a church, political party, or other group: the doctrine of predestination. 
• US a stated principle of government policy, mainly in foreign or military affairs: the Monroe Doctrine. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French, from Latin doctrina ‘teaching, learning,’ from doctor ‘teacher,’ from docere ‘teach.’

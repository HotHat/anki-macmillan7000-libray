diameter 
dīˈamədər 
(abbreviation diam.) 
▶noun
1 a straight line passing from side to side through the center of a body or figure, especially a circle or sphere. 
• the length of the diameter of a figure, especially a circle or sphere. 
• a transverse measurement of something; width or thickness.
2 a unit of linear measurement of magnifying power. DERIVATIVES diametral 
dīˈamətrəl 
adjectiveORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French diametre, via Latin from Greek diametros (grammē)‘(line) measuring across,’ from dia ‘across’ + metron ‘measure.’

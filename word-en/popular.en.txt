popular 
ˈpäpyələr

▶adjective
1 liked, admired, or enjoyed by many people or by a particular person or group: she was one of the most popular girls in the school 
these cheeses are very popular in Europe.
2 [attributive] (of cultural activities or products) intended for or suited to the taste, understanding, or means of the general public rather than specialists or intellectuals: the popular press. 
• (of a belief or attitude) held by the majority of the general public: many adult cats, contrary to popular opinion, dislike milk.
3 [attributive] (of political activity) of or carried on by the people as a whole rather than restricted to politicians or political parties: a popular revolt against colonial rule. DERIVATIVES popularism 
-ˌrizəm 
nounORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘prevalent among the general public’): from Latin popularis, from populus ‘people.’ Sense
1 dates from the early 17th century.

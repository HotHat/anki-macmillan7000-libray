advertise 
ˈadvərˌtīz

▶verb [with object] describe or draw attention to (a product, service, or event) in a public medium in order to promote sales or attendance: a billboard advertising beer 
many rugs are advertised as machine washable 
[no object] : we had a chance to advertise on television. 
• seek to fill (a vacancy) by putting a notice in a newspaper or other medium: for every job we advertise we get a hundred applicants 
[no object] : he advertised for dancers in the trade papers. 
• make (a quality or fact) known: Meryl coughed briefly to advertise her presence. 
• archaic notify (someone) of something: some prisoners advertised the French of this terrible danger. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French advertiss-, lengthened stem of advertir, from Latin advertere ‘turn toward’ (see advert2) .

create 
krēˈāt

▶verb [with object] bring (something) into existence: he created a thirty-acre lake 
over 170 jobs were created. 
• cause (something) to happen as a result of one's actions: divorce only created problems for children. 
• (of an actor) originate (a role) by playing a character for the first time. 
• [with object and complement] invest (someone) with a new rank or title: he was created a baronet. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘form out of nothing,’ used of a divine or supernatural being): from Latin creat- ‘produced,’ from the verb creare .

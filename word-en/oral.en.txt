oral 
ˈôrəl

▶adjective
1 by word of mouth; spoken rather than written: they had reached an oral agreement. 
• relating to the transmission of information or literature by word of mouth rather than in writing: oral literature 
a society with an oral tradition. 
• (of a society) not having reached the stage of literacy.
2 relating to the mouth: oral hygiene. 
• done or taken by the mouth: oral contraceptives. 
• Phonetics (of a speech sound) pronounced by the voice resonating in the mouth, as the vowels in English. Compare with nasal ( sense
2 of the adjective). 
• Psychoanalysis (in Freudian theory) relating to or denoting a stage of infantile psychosexual development in which the mouth is the main source of pleasure and the center of experience. 
▶noun (often orals) a spoken examination or test: he was preparing for his orals 
a French oral. ORIGIN early 17th century: from late Latin oralis, from Latin os, or- ‘mouth.’ usage: See usage at verbal. See also usage at aural .

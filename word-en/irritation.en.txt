irritation 
ˌirəˈtāSH(ə)n

▶noun the state of feeling annoyed, impatient, or angry. 
• a cause of this: the minor irritations of life. 
• the production of inflammation or other discomfort in a bodily part or organ. 
• Biology the stimulation of an organism, cell, or organ to produce an active response. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin irritatio(n-), from the verb irritare (see irritate) .

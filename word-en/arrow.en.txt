arrow 
ˈerō

▶noun a shaft sharpened at the front and with feathers or vanes at the back, shot from a bow as a weapon or for sport: his ability to launch an arrow accurately. 
• a mark or sign resembling an arrow, used to show direction or position; a pointer: we drove in the main gate and followed a series of arrows 
[as modifier] : you can use the up and down arrow keys. 
PHRASES
arrow of time (or time's arrow) the direction of travel from past to future in time considered as a physical dimension.straight as an arrow perfectly straight, with no deviation.DERIVATIVES arrowy adjectiveORIGIN Old English arewe, arwe, from Old Norse.

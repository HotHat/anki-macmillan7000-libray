guerrilla 
ɡəˈrilə 
(also guerilla) 
▶noun a member of a small independent group taking part in irregular fighting, typically against larger regular forces: this small town fell to the guerrillas 
[as modifier] : guerrilla warfare. 
• [as modifier] referring to actions or activities performed in an impromptu way, often without authorization: guerrilla theater. ORIGIN early 19th century (introduced during the Peninsular War (1808–14)): from Spanish, diminutive of guerra ‘war.’

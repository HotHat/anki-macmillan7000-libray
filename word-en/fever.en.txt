fever 
ˈfēvər

▶noun an abnormally high body temperature, usually accompanied by shivering, headache, and in severe instances, delirium: I would take aspirin to help me with the pain and reduce the fever 
African equine fever. 
• a state of nervous excitement or agitation: I was mystified, and in a fever of expectation. 
• [with modifier] the excitement felt by a group of people about a particular public event: election fever reaches its climax tomorrow. 
▶verb [with object] archaic bring about a high body temperature or a state of nervous excitement in (someone): a heart which sin has fevered. ORIGIN Old English fēfor, from Latin febris; reinforced in Middle English by Old French fievre, also from febris .

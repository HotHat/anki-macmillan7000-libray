cousin 
ˈkəz(ə)n

▶noun (also first cousin) a child of one's uncle or aunt. 
• a person belonging to the same extended family. 
• a thing related or analogous to another: the new motorbikes are not proving as popular as their four-wheeled cousins. 
• (usually cousins) a person of a kindred culture, race, or nation: the Russians and their Slavic cousins. 
• historical a title formerly used by a sovereign in addressing another sovereign or a noble of their own country.

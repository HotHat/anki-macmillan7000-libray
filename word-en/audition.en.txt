audition 
ôˈdiSH(ə)n

▶noun an interview for a particular role or job as a singer, actor, dancer, or musician, consisting of a practical demonstration of the candidate's suitability and skill. 
▶verb [no object] perform an audition: he was auditioning for the lead role in the play. 
• [with object] assess the suitability of (someone) for a role by means of an audition: she was auditioning people for her new series. ORIGIN late 16th century (in the sense ‘power of hearing or listening’): from Latin auditio(n)-, from audire ‘hear.’ The current sense of the noun dates from the late 19th century.

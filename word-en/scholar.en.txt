scholar 
ˈskälər

▶noun a specialist in a particular branch of study, especially the humanities; a distinguished academic: a Hebrew scholar. 
• chiefly archaic a person who is highly educated or has an aptitude for study: Mr. Bell declares himself no scholar. 
• a student holding a scholarship. 
• archaic a student. ORIGIN Old English scol(i)ere ‘schoolchild, student,’ from late Latin scholaris, from Latin schola (see school1) .

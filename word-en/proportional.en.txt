proportional 
prəˈpôrSH(ə)n(ə)l

▶adjective corresponding in size or amount to something else: the punishment should be proportional to the crime. 
• Mathematics (of a variable quantity) having a constant ratio to another quantity. ORIGIN late Middle English: from late Latin proportionalis, from proportio(n-) (see proportion) . usage: Except in certain long-established phrases, such as proportional representation, the adjectives proportional and proportionate may be used interchangeably.

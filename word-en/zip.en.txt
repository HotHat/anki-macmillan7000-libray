zip 
zip

▶verb (zips, zipping, zipped)
1 [with object] fasten with a zipper: I zipped up my sweater. 
• (zip someone up) fasten the zipper of a garment that someone is wearing: he zipped himself up. 
• Computing compress (a file) so that it takes less space in storage.
2 [no object] informal move at high speed: swallows zipped back and forth across the lake. 
• [with object] cause to move or be delivered or dealt with rapidly: he zipped a pass out to his receiver. 
▶noun
1 (also zip fastener) chiefly British a zipper. 
• [as modifier] denoting something fastened by a zipper: a zip pocket.
2 informal energy; vigor: he's full of zip.
3 short for zip code. 
▶pronoun (also zippo) North American informal nothing at all: you got zip to do with me and my kind, buddy. ORIGIN mid 19th century: imitative.

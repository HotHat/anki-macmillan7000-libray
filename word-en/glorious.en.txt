glorious 
ˈɡlôrēəs

▶adjective
1 having, worthy of, or bringing fame or admiration: the most glorious victory of all time.
2 having a striking beauty or splendor that evokes feelings of delighted admiration: a glorious autumn day. DERIVATIVES gloriousness nounORIGIN Middle English: from Old French glorieus, from Latin gloriosus, from gloria ‘glory.’

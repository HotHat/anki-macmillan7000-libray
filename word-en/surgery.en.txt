surgery 
ˈsərj(ə)rē

▶noun (plural surgeries)
1 the treatment of injuries or disorders of the body by incision or manipulation, especially with instruments: cardiac surgery 
he had surgery on his ankle.
2 British a place where a doctor, dentist, or other medical practitioner treats or advises patients. 
• [in singular] an occasion on which medical treatment or consultation occurs: Doctor Bailey had finished his evening surgery. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French surgerie, contraction of serurgerie, from serurgien (see surgeon) .

cereal 
ˈsirēəl

▶noun a grain used for food, such as wheat, oats, or corn. 
• (usually cereals) a grass producing a cereal grain, grown as an agricultural crop: [as modifier] : low yields for cereal crops. 
• a breakfast food made from roasted grain, typically eaten with milk: a bowl of cereal 
[as modifier] : a cereal box. ORIGIN early 19th century (as an adjective): from Latin cerealis, from Ceres, the name of the Roman goddess of agriculture.

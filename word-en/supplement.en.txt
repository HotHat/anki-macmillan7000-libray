supplement 
▶noun 
ˈsəpləmənt 

1 something that completes or enhances something else when added to it: the handout is a supplement to the official manual. 
• a substance taken to remedy the deficiencies in a person's diet: multivitamin supplements. 
• a separate section, especially a color magazine, added to a newspaper or periodical.
2 Geometry the amount by which an angle is less than 180°. 
▶verb 
ˈsəpləˌment ˈsəpləmənt 
[with object] add an extra element or amount to: she took the job to supplement her husband's income. DERIVATIVES supplementally 
ˌsəpləˈmentl-ē 
adverbORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin supplementum, from supplere ‘fill up, complete’ (see supply1) .

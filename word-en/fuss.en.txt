fuss 
fəs

▶noun [in singular] a display of unnecessary or excessive excitement, activity, or interest: I don't know what all the fuss is about. 
• a protest or dispute of a specified degree or kind: he didn't put up too much of a fuss. 
• elaborate or complex procedures; trouble or difficulty: they settled in with very little fuss. 
▶verb [no object] show unnecessary or excessive concern about something: she's always fussing about her food. 
• move around or busy oneself restlessly: beside him Kelly was fussing with sheets of paper. 
• [with object] British disturb or bother (someone): when she cries in her sleep, try not to fuss her.

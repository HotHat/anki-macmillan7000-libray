radiator 
ˈrādēˌādər

▶noun
1 a thing that radiates or emits light, heat, or sound. 
• a device for heating a room consisting of a metal tank connected by pipes through which hot water is pumped by a central heating system. 
• a portable heater resembling a radiator.
2 an engine-cooling device in a motor vehicle or aircraft consisting of a bank of thin tubes in which circulating fluid is cooled by the surrounding air.

cruelty 
ˈkro͞o(ə)ltē

▶noun (plural cruelties) callous indifference to or pleasure in causing pain and suffering: he has treated her with extreme cruelty. 
• behavior that causes pain or suffering to a person or animal: we can't stand cruelty to animals 
the cruelties of forced assimilation and genocide. 
• Law behavior that causes physical or mental harm to another, especially a spouse, whether intentionally or not. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French crualte, based on Latin crudelitas, from crudelis (see cruel) .

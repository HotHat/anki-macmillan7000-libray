prohibit 
prəˈhibit prōˈhibit

▶verb (prohibits, prohibiting, prohibited) [with object] formally forbid (something) by law, rule, or other authority: laws prohibiting cruelty to animals. 
• (prohibit someone/something from doing something) formally forbid a person or group from doing something: he is prohibited from being a director. 
• (of a fact or situation) prevent (something); make impossible: the budget agreement had prohibited any tax cuts. DERIVATIVES prohibiter noun. prohibitor 
-ər 
noun. prohibitory 
prəˈhibiˌtôrē prōˈhēbiˌtôrē 
adjectiveORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin prohibit- ‘kept in check,’ from the verb prohibere, from pro- ‘in front’ + habere ‘to hold.’

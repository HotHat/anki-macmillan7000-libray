complementary 
ˌkämpləˈment(ə)rē

▶adjective
1 combining in such a way as to enhance or emphasize the qualities of each other or another: three guitarists playing interlocking, complementary parts 
Internet technology is actually complementary to traditional technologies. 
• Biochemistry (of gene sequences, nucleotides, etc.) related by the rules of base pairing.
2 [attributive] relating to complementary medicine: complementary therapies such as aromatherapy. DERIVATIVES complementarily 
ˌkämpləˈmentrəlē, -menˈterəlē 
adverb. complementariness noun

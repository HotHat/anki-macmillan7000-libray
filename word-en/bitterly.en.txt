bitterly 
ˈbidərlē

▶adverb
1 in an angry, hurt, or resentful way: passengers complained bitterly about grand promises made by the company 
the two governments quarreled bitterly over the terms of the loan.
2 [as submodifier] to an extreme, forceful, or particularly harsh degree: he was bitterly opposed by the majority shareholder 
its humor is cutting and bitterly sarcastic.
3 (relating to wind or weather) to an intensely cold degree: a bitterly cold day.

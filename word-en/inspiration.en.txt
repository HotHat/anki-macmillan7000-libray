inspiration 
ˌinspəˈrāSH(ə)n

▶noun
1 the process of being mentally stimulated to do or feel something, especially to do something creative: Helen had one of her flashes of inspiration 
the history of fashion has provided designers with invaluable inspiration. 
• the quality of being inspired, especially when evident in something: a rare moment of inspiration in an otherwise dull display. 
• a person or thing that inspires: he is an inspiration to everyone. 
• a sudden brilliant, creative, or timely idea: then I had an inspiration. 
• the divine influence believed to have led to the writing of the Bible.
2 the drawing in of breath; inhalation. 
• an act of breathing in; an inhalation. ORIGIN Middle English (in the sense ‘divine guidance’): via Old French from late Latin inspiratio(n-), from the verb inspirare (see inspire) .

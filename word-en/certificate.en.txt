certificate 
▶noun 
sərˈtifəkət 
an official document attesting a certain fact, in particular: 
• a document recording a person's birth, marriage, or death. 
• a document attesting a level of achievement in a course of study or training: graduate certificate in information technology. 
• a document attesting ownership of a certain item: a stock certificate. 
▶verb 
ˌsərˈtifəkāt 
[with object] British provide with or attest in an official document. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘certification, attestation’): from French certificat or medieval Latin certificatum, from certificare (see certify) .

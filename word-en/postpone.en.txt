postpone 
pōˈspōn pōstˈpōn

▶verb [with object] cause or arrange for (something) to take place at a time later than that first scheduled: the visit had to be postponed for some time 
[with present participle] : the judge postponed sentencing a former government spokesman for fraud. DERIVATIVES postponable 
ˌpōs(t)ˈpōnəbəl pəˈspōnəbəl 
adjective. postponer nounORIGIN late 15th century: from Latin postponere, from post ‘after’ + ponere ‘to place.’

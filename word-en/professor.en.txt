professor 
prəˈfesər

▶noun
1 (also full professor) a teacher of the highest rank in a college or university. 
• North American an associate professor or an assistant professor. 
• North American informal any instructor, especially in a specialized field.
2 a person who affirms a faith in or allegiance to something: the professors of true religion. DERIVATIVES professorate 
-rət 
noun. professoriate 
ˌpräfəˈsôrēət 
nounORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin professor, from profess- ‘declared publicly,’ from the verb profiteri (see profess) .

assemble 
əˈsembəl

▶verb
1 [no object] (of people) gather together in one place for a common purpose: a crowd had assembled outside the gates. 
• [with object] bring (people or things) together for a common purpose: he assembled the surviving members of the group for a tour. 
• (usually as noun assembling) Entomology (of male moths) gather for mating in response to a pheromone released by a female.
2 [with object] fit together the separate component parts of (a machine or other object): a factory that assembled parts for trucks. 
• Computing translate (a program) from assembly language into machine code. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French asembler, based on Latin ad- ‘to’ + simul ‘together.’

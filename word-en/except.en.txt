except 
ikˈsept

▶preposition not including; other than: naked except for my socks 
they work every day except Sunday. 
▶conjunction used before a statement that forms an exception to one just made: I didn't tell him anything, except that I needed the money 
our berets were the same except mine had a leather band inside. 
• archaic unless: she never offered advice, except it were asked of her. 
▶verb [with object] formal specify as not included in a category or group; exclude: he excepted from his criticism a handful of distinguished writers. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin except- ‘taken out,’ from the verb excipere, from ex- ‘out of’ + capere ‘take.’ usage: See usage at accept .

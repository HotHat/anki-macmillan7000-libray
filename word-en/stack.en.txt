stack 
▶noun
1 a stack of boxes: heap, pile, mound, mountain, pyramid, tower.
2 a stack of hay: haystack, rick, hayrick, mow, shock, haycock; dated cock.
3 informal a stack of money. See lot (pronoun).
4 chimney, smokestack, funnel, exhaust pipe.
▶verb
1 Leo was stacking plates: heap (up), pile (up), make a heap/pile/stack of; assemble, put together, collect, hoard, store, stockpile.
2 they stacked the shelves: load, fill (up), lade, pack, charge, stuff, cram; stock. ANTONYMS empty.

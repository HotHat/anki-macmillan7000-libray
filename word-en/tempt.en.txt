tempt 
tem(p)t

▶verb [with object] entice or attempt to entice (someone) to do or acquire something that they find attractive but know to be wrong or not beneficial: don't allow impatience to tempt you into overexposure and sunburn 
there'll always be someone tempted by the rich pickings of poaching 
[with object and infinitive] : jobs that involve entertaining may tempt you to drink more than you intend. 
• (be tempted to do something) have an urge or inclination to do something: I was tempted to look at my watch, but didn't dare. 
• attract; allure: he was tempted out of retirement to save the team. 
• archaic risk provoking (a deity or abstract force), usually with undesirable consequences. 
PHRASES
tempt fate (or providence) do something that is risky or dangerous.DERIVATIVES temptability

potato 
pəˈtādō

▶noun (plural potatoes)
1 a starchy plant tuber that is one of the most important food crops, cooked and eaten as a vegetable: roasted potatoes 
the meal comes with rice or potato 
[as modifier] : leek and potato soup. 
• see sweet potato.
2 the plant of the nightshade family that produces the potato tubers on underground runners. [Solanum tuberosum, family Solanaceae. It was first cultivated in the Andes about 1,800 years ago and was introduced to Europe in c. 1570.] ORIGIN mid 16th century: from Spanish patata, variant of Taino batata ‘sweet potato.’ The English word originally denoted the sweet potato and gained its current sense in the late 16th century.

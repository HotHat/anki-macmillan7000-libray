elect 
əˈlekt

▶verb [with object] choose (someone) to hold public office or some other position by voting: the members who were elected to the committee 
[with object and infinitive] : they elected him leader. 
• opt for or choose to do something: freshman year you could elect Industrial Arts 
[no object] : more people elected to work at home. 
• Christian Theology (of God) choose (someone) in preference to others for salvation. 
▶adjective (usually as plural noun the elect) (of a person) chosen or singled out: one of the century's elect. 
• [postpositive in combination] elected to or chosen for a position but not yet in office: the president-elect. 
• Christian Theology chosen by God for salvation. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin elect- ‘picked out,’ from the verb eligere, from e- (variant of ex-)‘out’ + legere ‘to pick.’

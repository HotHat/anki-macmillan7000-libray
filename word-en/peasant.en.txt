peasant 
ˈpez(ə)nt

▶noun a poor farmer of low social status who owns or rents a small piece of land for cultivation (chiefly in historical use or with reference to subsistence farming in poorer countries). 
• informal an ignorant, rude, or unsophisticated person; a person of low social status. DERIVATIVES peasanty adjectiveORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French paisent ‘country dweller,’ from pais ‘country,’ based on Latin pagus ‘country district.’

metal 
ˈmedl

▶noun
1 a solid material that is typically hard, shiny, malleable, fusible, and ductile, with good electrical and thermal conductivity (e.g., iron, gold, silver, copper, and aluminum, and alloys such as brass and steel): vessels made of ceramics or metal 
being a metal, aluminum readily conducts heat. 
• Heraldry gold and silver (as tinctures in blazoning).
2 British (also road metal) broken stone for use in making roads.
3 molten glass before it is blown or cast.
4 heavy metal or similar rock music. 
▶verb (metals, metaling, metaled; chiefly British metals, metalling, metalled) [with object] (usually as adjective metaled)
1 make out of or coat with metal: metaled key rings.
2 British make or mend (a road) with road metal: follow the metalled road for about 200 yards. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French metal or Latin metallum, from Greek metallon ‘mine, quarry, or metal.’

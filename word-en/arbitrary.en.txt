arbitrary 
ˈärbəˌtrerē

▶adjective based on random choice or personal whim, rather than any reason or system: his mealtimes were entirely arbitrary. 
• (of power or a ruling body) unrestrained and autocratic in the use of authority: arbitrary rule by King and bishops has been made impossible. 
• Mathematics (of a constant or other quantity) of unspecified value. ORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘dependent on one's will or pleasure, discretionary’): from Latin arbitrarius, from arbiter ‘judge, supreme ruler,’ perhaps influenced by French arbitraire .

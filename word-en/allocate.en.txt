allocate 
ˈaləˌkāt

▶verb [with object] distribute (resources or duties) for a particular purpose: the authorities allocated 50,000 places to refugees 
[with two objects] : he has been allocated a generous slice of the annual budget. DERIVATIVES allocable 
ˈaləkəb(ə)l 
adjective. allocator 
-ˌkātər 
nounORIGIN mid 17th century: from medieval Latin allocat- ‘allotted,’ from the verb allocare, from ad- ‘to’ + locare (see locate) .

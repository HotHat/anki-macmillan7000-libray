squadron 
ˈskwädrən

▶noun an operational unit in an air force consisting of two or more flights of aircraft and the personnel required to fly them. 
• a principal division of an armored or cavalry regiment, consisting of two or more troops. 
• a group of warships detached on a particular duty or under the command of a flag officer. 
• informal a large group of people or things: he immediately commissioned a squadron of architects. ORIGIN mid 16th century (originally denoting a group of soldiers in square formation): from Italian squadrone, from squadra ‘square.’

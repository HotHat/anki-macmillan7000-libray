cushion 
ˈko͝oSHən

▶noun a pillow or pad stuffed with a mass of soft material, used as a comfortable support for sitting or leaning on. 
• something providing support or protection against impact: the pad forms a cushion between carpet and floor 
figurative : a poll showed the candidate with a 14-point cushion. 
• the elastic lining of the sides of a billiard table, from which the ball rebounds. 
• the layer of air supporting a hovercraft or similar vehicle. 
▶verb [with object] soften the effect of an impact on: the bag cushions equipment from inevitable knocks. 
• mitigate the adverse effects of: he called for federal assistance to cushion the blow for farmers. DERIVATIVES cushiony 
ˈko͝oSH(ə)nē 
adjectiveORIGIN Middle English: from Old French cuissin, based on a Latin word meaning ‘cushion for the hip,’ from coxa ‘hip, thigh.’

regime 
rəˈZHēm rāˈZHēm 
(also régime) 
▶noun
1 a government, especially an authoritarian one.
2 a system or planned way of doing things, especially one imposed from above: detention centers with a very tough physical regime. 
• a coordinated program for the promotion or restoration of health; a regimen: a low-calorie, low-fat regime. 
• the conditions under which a scientific or industrial process occurs. ORIGIN late 15th century (in the sense ‘regimen’): French régime, from Latin regimen ‘rule’ (see regimen). Sense
1 dates from the late 18th century (with original reference to the Ancien Régime).

diagnose 
ˌdīəɡˈnōs

▶verb [with object] identify the nature of (an illness or other problem) by examination of the symptoms: doctors diagnosed a rare and fatal liver disease. 
• (usually be diagnosed) identify the nature of the medical condition of (someone): she was finally diagnosed as having epilepsy 
20,000 men are diagnosed with skin cancer every year. DERIVATIVES diagnosable adjectiveORIGIN mid 19th century: back-formation from diagnosis .

intelligent 
inˈteləjənt

▶adjective having or showing intelligence, especially of a high level: Annabelle is intelligent and hardworking 
an intelligent guess. 
• (of a device, machine, or building) able to vary its state or action in response to varying situations, varying requirements, and past experience. 
• (especially of a computer terminal) incorporating a microprocessor and having its own processing capability. Often contrasted with dumb. ORIGIN early 16th century: from Latin intelligent- ‘understanding,’ from the verb intelligere, variant of intellegere ‘understand,’ from inter ‘between’ + legere ‘choose.’

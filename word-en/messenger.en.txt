messenger 
ˈmes(ə)njər

▶noun
1 a person who carries a message or is employed to carry messages. 
• Biochemistry a substance that conveys information or a stimulus within the body.
2 Nautical (also messenger line) an endless rope, cable, or chain used with a capstan to haul an anchor cable or to drive a powered winch. 
• a light line used to haul or support a larger cable. 
▶verb [with object] chiefly US send (a document or package) by messenger: could you have it messengered over to me? 
PHRASES
shoot (or kill) the messenger treat the bearer of bad news as if they were to blame for it.ORIGIN Middle English: from Old Northern French messanger, variant of Old French messager, from Latin missus (see message) .

climax 
ˈklīˌmaks

▶noun the most intense, exciting, or important point of something; a culmination or apex: the climax of her speech 
a thrilling climax to the game. 
• an orgasm. 
• Ecology the final stage in a succession in a given environment, at which a plant community reaches a state of equilibrium: [as modifier] : a mixed hardwood climax forest. 
• Rhetoric a sequence of propositions or ideas in order of increasing importance, force, or effectiveness of expression. 
▶verb [no object] culminate in an exciting or impressive event; reach a climax: the day climaxed with a gala concert. 
• [with object] bring (something) to a climax: the sentencing climaxed a seven-month trial. 
• have an orgasm. ORIGIN mid 16th century (in rhetoric): from late Latin, from Greek klimax ‘ladder, climax.’ The sense ‘culmination’ arose in the late 18th century.

ambiguity 
ˌambəˈɡyo͞owədē

▶noun (plural ambiguities) the quality of being open to more than one interpretation; inexactness: we can detect no ambiguity in this section of the Act 
ambiguities in such questions are potentially very dangerous. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French ambiguite or Latin ambiguitas, from ambiguus ‘doubtful’ (see ambiguous) .

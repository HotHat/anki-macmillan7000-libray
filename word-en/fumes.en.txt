fume 
fyo͞om

▶noun (usually fumes) gas, smoke, or vapor that smells strongly or is dangerous to inhale: clouds of exhaust fumes spewed by cars. 
• a pungent odor of a particular thing or substance: he breathed fumes of wine into her face. 
• literary a watery vapor, steam, or mist rising from the earth or sea: a smouldering fume of dry frost. 
▶verb [no object]
1 emit gas, smoke, or vapor: fragments of lava hit the ground, fuming and sizzling. 
• [with object] (usually as adjective fumed) expose (especially wood) to ammonia fumes in order to produce dark tints: the fumed oak sideboard.
2 feel, show, or express great anger: he is fuming over the interference in his work. DERIVATIVES fumingly

hereditary 
həˈredəˌterē

▶adjective (of a title, office, or right) conferred by or based on inheritance: members of the ancient Polish aristocracy who had hereditary right to elect the king. 
• [attributive] (of a person) holding a position by inheritance: I am the hereditary chief of the Piscataway people. 
• (of a characteristic or disease) determined by genetic factors and therefore able to be passed on from parents to their offspring or descendants: cystic fibrosis is our most common fatal hereditary disease. 
• relating to inheritance: a form of hereditary succession and dynastic rule became standard practice. 
• Mathematics (of a set) defined such that every element that has a given relation to a member of the set is also a member of the set. DERIVATIVES hereditarily 
həˌredəˈterəlē 
adverb. hereditariness 
həˌredəˈterinəs 
nounORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin hereditarius, from hereditas (see heredity) .

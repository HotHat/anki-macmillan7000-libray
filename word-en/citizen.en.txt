citizen 
ˈsidizən ˈsidisən 
(abbreviation cit.) 
▶noun a legally recognized subject or national of a state or commonwealth, either native or naturalized: a Polish citizen 
the rights of every citizen. 
• an inhabitant of a particular town or city: the citizens of Los Angeles. 
PHRASES
citizen of the world a person who is at home in any country.ORIGIN Middle English: from Anglo-Norman French citezein, alteration (probably influenced by deinzein ‘denizen’) of Old French citeain, based on Latin civitas ‘city’ (see city) .

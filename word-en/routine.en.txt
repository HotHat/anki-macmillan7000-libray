routine 
ro͞oˈtēn

▶noun a sequence of actions regularly followed; a fixed program: I settled down into a routine of work and sleep 
as a matter of routine a report will be sent to the director. 
• a set sequence in a performance such as a dance or comedy act: he was trying to persuade her to have a tap routine in the play. 
• Computing a sequence of instructions for performing a task that forms a program or a distinct part of one. 
▶adjective performed as part of a regular procedure rather than for a special reason: the principal insisted that this was just a routine annual drill. 
▶verb [with object] rare organize according to a routine: all had been routined with smoothness. ORIGIN late 17th century (denoting a regular course or procedure): from French, from route ‘road’ (see route) .

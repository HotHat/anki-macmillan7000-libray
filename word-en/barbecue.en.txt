barbecue 
ˈbärbəˌkyo͞o 
(also barbeque) 
▶noun a meal or gathering at which meat, fish, or other food is cooked out of doors on a rack over an open fire or on a portable grill. 
• a portable grill used for the preparation of food at a barbecue, or a brick fireplace containing a grill. 
• North American food cooked on a barbecue. 
▶verb (barbecues, barbecuing, barbecued) [with object] cook (meat, fish, or other food) on a barbecue: fish barbecued with herbs 
(as adjective barbecued) : barbecued chicken. ORIGIN mid 17th century: from Spanish barbacoa, perhaps from Arawak barbacoa ‘wooden frame on posts.’ The original sense was ‘wooden framework for sleeping on, or for storing meat or fish to be dried.’

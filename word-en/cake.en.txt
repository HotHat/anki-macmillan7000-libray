cake 
kāk

▶noun an item of soft, sweet food made from a mixture of flour, shortening, eggs, sugar, and other ingredients, baked and often decorated: a carrot cake 
[as modifier] : cake pans 
a mouthful of cake. 
• an item of savory food formed into a flat, round shape, and typically baked or fried: crab cakes 
buckwheat cakes. 
• a flattish, compact mass of something, especially soap: a cake of soap. 
▶verb [with object] (of a thick or sticky substance that hardens when dry) cover and become encrusted on (the surface of an object): a pair of boots caked with mud. 
• [no object] (of a thick or sticky substance) dry or harden into a solid mass: the blood under his nose was beginning to cake.

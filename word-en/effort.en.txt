effort 
ˈefərt

▶noun a vigorous or determined attempt: hammer birdhouses to country fenceposts in an effort to bring back the eastern bluebird. 
• the result of an attempt: he was a keen gardener, winning many prizes for his efforts. 
• strenuous physical or mental exertion: the doctor spared no effort in helping my father. 
• technical a force exerted by a machine or in a process. 
• [with modifier] the activities of a group of people with a common purpose: the war effort. DERIVATIVES effortful adjective. effortfully adverbORIGIN late 15th century: from French, from Old French esforcier, based on Latin ex- ‘out’ + fortis ‘strong.’

layer 
ˈlāər

▶noun
1 a sheet, quantity, or thickness of material, typically one of several, covering a surface or body: bears depend on a layer of blubber to keep them warm in the water 
figurative : a larger missile would provide a layer of defense at higher altitudes. 
• a level of seniority in the hierarchy of an organization: a managerial layer.
2 [in combination] a person or thing that lays something: the worms are prolific egg-layers.
3 a shoot fastened down to take root while attached to the parent plant. 
▶verb [with object] (often as adjective layered)
1 arrange in a layer or layers: the current trend for layered clothes. 
• cut (hair) in overlapping layers: her layered, shoulder-length hair.
2 propagate (a plant) as a layer: a layered shoot. ORIGIN Middle English (denoting a mason): from lay1 + -er1. The sense ‘stratum of material covering a surface’ (early 17th century) may represent a respelling of an obsolete agricultural use of lair1 denoting quality of soil.

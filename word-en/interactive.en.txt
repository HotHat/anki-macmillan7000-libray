interactive 
ˌin(t)ərˈaktiv

▶adjective (of two people or things) influencing or having an effect on each other: fully sighted children in interactive play with others with defective vision. 
• allowing a two-way flow of information between a computer and a computer-user; responding to a user's input: a fully interactive map of the area. DERIVATIVES interactively 
ˌin(t)ərˈaktəvlē 
adverbORIGIN mid 19th century: from interact, on the pattern of active.

pragmatic 
praɡˈmadik

▶adjective dealing with things sensibly and realistically in a way that is based on practical rather than theoretical considerations: a pragmatic approach to politics. 
• relating to philosophical or political pragmatism. 
• Linguistics relating to pragmatics. ORIGIN late 16th century (in the senses ‘busy, interfering, conceited’): via Latin from Greek pragmatikos ‘relating to fact,’ from pragma ‘deed’ (from the stem of prattein ‘do’). The current sense dates from the mid 19th century.

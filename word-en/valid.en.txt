valid 
ˈvaləd

▶adjective (of an argument or point) having a sound basis in logic or fact; reasonable or cogent: a valid criticism. 
• legally binding due to having been executed in compliance with the law: a valid contract. 
• legally or officially acceptable: the visas are valid for thirty days 
a valid password. ORIGIN late 16th century: from French valide or Latin validus ‘strong,’ from valere ‘be strong.’

fame 
fām

▶noun the condition of being known or talked about by many people, especially on account of notable achievements: winning the Olympic title has brought her fame and fortune. 
PHRASES
fifteen minutes of fame see fifteen. of —— fame having a particular famous association; famous for having or being ——: the Cariboo country of gold rush fame.ORIGIN Middle English (also in the sense ‘reputation,’ which survives in the phrase house of ill fame): via Old French from Latin fama .

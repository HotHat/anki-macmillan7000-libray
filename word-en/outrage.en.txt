outrage 
ˈoutˌrāj

▶noun an extremely strong reaction of anger, shock, or indignation: her voice trembled with outrage. 
• an action or event causing anger, shock, or indignation: the decision was an outrage. 
▶verb [with object] (usually be outraged) arouse fierce anger, shock, or indignation in (someone): he was outraged at this attempt to take his victory away from him. 
• violate or infringe flagrantly (a principle, law, etc.): their behavior outraged all civilized standards. ORIGIN Middle English (in the senses ‘lack of moderation’ and ‘violent behavior’): from Old French ou(l)trage, based on Latin ultra ‘beyond.’ Sense development has been affected by the belief that the word is a compound of out and rage .

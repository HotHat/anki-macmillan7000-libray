arch
1 
ärCH

▶noun a curved symmetrical structure spanning an opening and typically supporting the weight of a bridge, roof, or wall above it. 
• an arched structure forming a passageway or a ceremonial monument: a triumphal arch. 
• a shape resembling an arch or a thing with such a shape: the delicate arch of his eyebrows. 
• the inner side of the foot. 
▶verb
1 [no object] have the curved shape of an arch: a beautiful bridge that arched over a canal. 
• form or cause to form the curved shape of an arch: her eyebrows arched in surprise 
[with object] : she arched her back.
2 [with object] archaic or literary span (something) by or as if by an arch: the vine arched his evening seat. ORIGIN Middle English: from Old French arche, based on Latin arcus ‘bow’ (see arc) .

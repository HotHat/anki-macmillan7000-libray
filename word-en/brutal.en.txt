brutal 
ˈbro͞odl

▶adjective savagely violent: a brutal murder. 
• punishingly hard or uncomfortable: the brutal winter wind. 
• direct and lacking any attempt to disguise unpleasantness: the brutal honesty of his observations. ORIGIN late 15th century (in the sense ‘relating to the lower animals’): from Old French, or from medieval Latin brutalis, from brutus ‘dull, stupid’ (see brute) .

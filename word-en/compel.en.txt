compel 
kəmˈpel

▶verb (compels, compelling, compelled) [with object] force or oblige (someone) to do something: [with object and infinitive] : a sense of duty compelled Harry to answer her questions. 
• bring about (something) by the use of force or pressure: they may compel a witness's attendance at court by issue of a summons. 
• literary drive forcibly: by heav'n's high will compell'd from shore to shore. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin compellere, from com- ‘together’ + pellere ‘drive.’

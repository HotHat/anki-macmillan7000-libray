tragedy 
ˈtrajədē

▶noun (plural tragedies)
1 an event causing great suffering, destruction, and distress, such as a serious accident, crime, or natural catastrophe: a tragedy that killed 95 people 
his life had been plagued by tragedy.
2 a play dealing with tragic events and having an unhappy ending, especially one concerning the downfall of the main character. 
• the dramatic genre represented by tragedy: Greek tragedy. Compare with comedy. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French tragedie, via Latin from Greek tragōidia, apparently from tragos ‘goat’ (the reason remains unexplained) + ōidē ‘song, ode.’ Compare with tragic .

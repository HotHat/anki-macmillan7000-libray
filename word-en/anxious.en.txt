anxious 
ˈaNG(k)SHəs

▶adjective
1 experiencing worry, unease, or nervousness, typically about an imminent event or something with an uncertain outcome: she was extremely anxious about her exams. 
• [attributive] (of a period of time or situation) causing or characterized by worry or nervousness: there were some anxious moments.
2 [usu with infinitive] wanting something very much, typically with a feeling of unease: the company was anxious to avoid any trouble 
[with clause] : my parents were anxious that I get an education. DERIVATIVES anxiousness 
ˈaNG(k)SHəsnəs 
nounORIGIN early 17th century: from Latin anxius (from angere ‘to choke’) + -ous . usage: Anxious and eager both mean ‘looking forward to something,’ but they have different connotations. Eager suggests enthusiasm about something, a positive outlook: I'm eager to get started on my vacation. Anxious implies worry about something: I'm anxious to get started before it rains.

ham

▶noun
1 meat from the upper part of a pig's leg salted and dried or smoked: thin slices of ham 
a honey-baked ham.
2 (hams) the backs of the thighs or the thighs and buttocks: he squatted down on his hams. ORIGIN Old English ham, hom (originally denoting the back of the knee), from a Germanic base meaning ‘be crooked.’ In the late 15th century the term came to denote the back of the thigh, hence the thigh or hock of an animal.

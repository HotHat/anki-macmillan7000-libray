complexion 
kəmˈplekSHən

▶noun
1 the natural color, texture, and appearance of a person's skin, especially of the face: an attractive girl with a pale complexion.
2 the general aspect or character of something: Congress's new complexion became boldly apparent last summer 
wind, rain, and road construction have gradually changed Baja's complexion. DERIVATIVES complexioned 
kəmˈplekSHənd 
adjective [often in combination] : they were both fair-complexionedORIGIN Middle English: via Old French from Latin complexio(n-) ‘combination’ (in late Latin ‘physical constitution’), from complectere ‘embrace, comprise.’ The term originally denoted physical constitution or temperament determined by the combination of the four bodily humors, hence sense
1 (late 16th century) as a visible sign of this.

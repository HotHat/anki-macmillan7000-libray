spontaneous 
spänˈtānēəs

▶adjective performed or occurring as a result of a sudden inner impulse or inclination and without premeditation or external stimulus: the audience broke into spontaneous applause 
a spontaneous display of affection. 
• (of a person) having an open, natural, and uninhibited manner. 
• (of a process or event) occurring without apparent external cause: spontaneous miscarriages. 
• archaic (of a plant) growing naturally and without being tended or cultivated. 
• Biology (of movement or activity in an organism) instinctive or involuntary: the spontaneous mechanical activity of circular smooth muscle. ORIGIN mid 17th century: from late Latin spontaneus (from (sua) sponte ‘of (one's) own accord’) + -ous .

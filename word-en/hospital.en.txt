hospital 
ˈhäˌspidl

▶noun
1 an institution providing medical and surgical treatment and nursing care for sick or injured people.
2 historical a hospice, especially one run by the Knights Hospitaller.
3 [usually in names] British Law a charitable institution for the education of the young. ORIGIN Middle English ( sense 2): via Old French from medieval Latin hospitale, neuter of Latin hospitalis ‘hospitable,’ from hospes, hospit- (see host1) .

repay 
rəˈpā

▶verb (past and past participle repaid 
rēˈpād
) [with object] pay back (a loan, debt, or sum of money): the loans were to be repaid over a 20-year period. 
• pay back money borrowed from (someone): most of his fortune had been spent repaying creditors. 
• do or give something as recompense for (a favor or kindness received): the manager has given me another chance and I'm desperate to repay that faith. DERIVATIVES repayable

sympathetic 
ˌsimpəˈTHedik

▶adjective
1 feeling, showing, or expressing sympathy: he was sympathetic toward staff with family problems 
he spoke in a sympathetic tone. 
• [predicative] showing approval of or favor toward an idea or action: he was sympathetic to evolutionary ideas.
2 pleasant or agreeable, in particular: 
• (of a person) attracting the liking of others: Audrey develops as a sympathetic character. 
• (of a structure) designed in a sensitive or fitting way: buildings that were sympathetic to their surroundings.
3 relating to or denoting the part of the autonomic nervous system consisting of nerves arising from ganglia near the middle part of the spinal cord, supplying the internal organs, blood vessels, and glands, and balancing the action of the parasympathetic nerves.
4 relating to, producing, or denoting an effect that arises in response to a similar action elsewhere. ORIGIN mid 17th century (in the sense ‘relating to an affinity or paranormal influence,’ as in sympathetic magic): from sympathy, on the pattern of pathetic.

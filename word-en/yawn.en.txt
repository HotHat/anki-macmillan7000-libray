yawn 
yôn

▶verb [no object] involuntarily open one's mouth wide and inhale deeply due to tiredness or boredom: he began yawning and looking at his watch. 
• (usually as adjective yawning) be wide open: a yawning chasm. 
▶noun a reflex act of opening one's mouth wide and inhaling deeply due to tiredness or boredom. 
• informal a thing that is considered boring or tedious: the awards show was a four-hour yawn. DERIVATIVES yawningly 
ˈyôniNGlē 
adverbORIGIN Old English geonian, of Germanic origin, from an Indo-European root shared by Latin hiare and Greek khainein. Current noun senses date from the early 18th century.

verdict 
ˈvərdikt

▶noun a decision on a disputed issue in a civil or criminal case or an inquest: the jury returned a verdict of ‘not guilty.’ 
• an opinion or judgment: I'm anxious to know your verdict on me. ORIGIN Middle English: from Anglo-Norman French verdit, from Old French veir ‘true’ (from Latin verus) + dit (from Latin dictum ‘saying’).

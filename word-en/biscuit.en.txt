biscuit 
ˈbiskit

▶noun
1 North American a small, typically round cake of bread leavened with baking powder, baking soda, or sometimes yeast. 
• British a cookie or cracker.
2 another term for bisque3: [as modifier] : biscuit ware.
3 a light brown color.
4 a small flat piece of wood used to join two mortised planks together. 
▶adjective light brown in color. 
PHRASES
take the biscuit British informal be the most remarkable or foolish of its kind.DERIVATIVES biscuity adjectiveORIGIN Middle English: from Old French bescuit, based on Latin bis ‘twice’ + coctus, past participle of coquere ‘to cook’ (so named because originally biscuits were cooked in a twofold process: first baked and then dried out in a slow oven so that they would keep).

predator 
ˈpredədər

▶noun
1 an animal that naturally preys on others: wolves are major predators of rodents.
2 a person or group that ruthlessly exploits others: a website frequented by sexual predators. 
• a company that tries to take over another. ORIGIN 1920s: from Latin praedator ‘plunderer,’ from praedat- ‘seized as plunder,’ from the verb praedari (see predation) .

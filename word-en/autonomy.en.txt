autonomy 
ôˈtänəmē

▶noun (plural autonomies) (of a country or region) the right or condition of self-government, especially in a particular sphere: Tatarstan demanded greater autonomy within the Russian Federation. 
• a self-governing country or region. 
• freedom from external control or influence; independence: economic autonomy is still a long way off for many women. 
• (in Kantian moral philosophy) the capacity of an agent to act in accordance with objective morality rather than under the influence of desires. DERIVATIVES autonomist 
-mist 
noun & adjectiveORIGIN early 17th century: from Greek autonomia, from autonomos ‘having its own laws,’ from autos ‘self’ + nomos ‘law.’

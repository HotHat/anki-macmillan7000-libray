awful 
ˈôfəl

▶adjective
1 very bad or unpleasant: the place smelled awful 
I look awful in a swimsuit 
an awful speech. 
• extremely shocking; horrific: awful, bloody images. 
• [attributive] used to emphasize the extent of something, especially something unpleasant or negative: I've made an awful fool of myself. 
• (of a person) very unwell, troubled, or unhappy: I felt awful for being so angry with him 
you look awful—you should go and lie down.
2 archaic inspiring reverential wonder or fear. 
▶adverb [as submodifier] informal, chiefly North American awfully; very: we're an awful long way from the main road. 
PHRASES
an awful lot a very large amount; a great deal: we've had an awful lot of letters 
you've still got an awful lot to learn.ORIGIN Old English (see awe, -ful) .

subsequent 
ˈsəbsəkwənt

▶adjective coming after something in time; following: the theory was developed subsequent to the earthquake of 1906. 
• Geology (of a stream or valley) having a direction or character determined by the resistance to erosion of the underlying rock, and typically following the strike of the strata. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French, or from Latin subsequent- ‘following after’ (from the verb subsequi).

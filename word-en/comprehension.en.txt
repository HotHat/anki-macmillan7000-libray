comprehension 
ˌkämprəˈhen(t)SH(ə)n

▶noun
1 the action or capability of understanding something: some won't have the least comprehension of what I'm trying to do 
the comprehension of spoken language.
2 archaic inclusion. ORIGIN late Middle English: from French compréhension or Latin comprehensio(n-), from the verb comprehendere ‘seize, comprise’ (see comprehend) .

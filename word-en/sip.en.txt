sip

▶verb (sips, sipping, sipped) [with object] drink (something) by taking small mouthfuls: I sat sipping coffee 
[no object] : she sipped at her tea. 
▶noun a small mouthful of liquid: she took a sip of the red wine. DERIVATIVES sipper nounORIGIN late Middle English: perhaps a modification of sup1, as symbolic of a less vigorous action.

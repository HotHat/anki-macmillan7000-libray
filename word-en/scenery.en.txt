scenery 
ˈsēn(ə)rē

▶noun the natural features of a landscape considered in terms of their appearance, especially when picturesque: spectacular views of mountain scenery. 
• the painted background used to represent natural features or other surroundings on a theater stage or movie set. 
PHRASES
change of scenery a move to different surroundings: we spent the weekend in Seattle just for a change of scenery. chew (up) the scenery informal, chiefly US (of an actor) overact: he chews up the courtroom scenery as the unscrupulous attorney.ORIGIN mid 18th century (earlier as scenary): from Italian scenario (see scenario). The change in the ending was due to association with -ery .

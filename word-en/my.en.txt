my 
mī

▶poss. det.
1 belonging to or associated with the speaker: my name is John 
my friend. 
• informal used with a name to refer to a member of the speaker's family: my Francine won top honors in the science fair. 
• used with forms of address in affectionate, sympathetic, humorous, or patronizing contexts: my dear boy 
my poor baby.
2 used in various expressions of surprise: my goodness! 
oh my! ORIGIN Middle English mi (originally before words beginning with any consonant except h-), reduced from min, from Old English mīn (see mine1) .

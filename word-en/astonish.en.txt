astonish 
əˈstäniSH

▶verb [with object] surprise or impress (someone) greatly: you never fail to astonish me 
[with object and clause] : it astonished her that Mrs. Browning could seem so anxious. ORIGIN early 16th century (as astonished, in the sense ‘stunned, bewildered, dismayed’): from obsolete astone ‘stun, stupefy,’ from Old French estoner, based on Latin ex- ‘out’ + tonare ‘to thunder.’

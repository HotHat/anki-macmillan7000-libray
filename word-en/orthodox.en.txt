orthodox 
ˈôrTHəˌdäks

▶adjective
1 (of a person or their views, especially religious or political ones, or other beliefs or practices) conforming to what is generally or traditionally accepted as right or true; established and approved: the orthodox economics of today 
orthodox medical treatment 
orthodox Hindus. 
• (of a person) not independent-minded; conventional and unoriginal: a relatively orthodox artist.
2 (of a thing) of the ordinary or usual type; normal: they avoided orthodox jazz venues.
3 (usually Orthodox) (of the Jews or Judaism) strictly keeping to traditional doctrine and ritual.
4 (usually Orthodox) relating to the Orthodox Church. DERIVATIVES orthodoxly adverbORIGIN late Middle English: from Greek orthodoxos (probably via ecclesiastical Latin), from orthos ‘straight or right’ + doxa ‘opinion.’

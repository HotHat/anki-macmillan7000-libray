greet
1 
ɡrēt

▶verb [with object] give a polite word or sign of welcome or recognition to (someone) on meeting. 
• receive or acknowledge (something) in a specified way: everyone present greeted this idea warmly. 
• (of a sight or sound) become apparent to or be noticed by (someone) on arrival somewhere: flowers and cheers greeted the shipyard workers. ORIGIN Old English grētan ‘approach, attack, or salute’; related to Dutch groeten and German grüssen ‘greet.’

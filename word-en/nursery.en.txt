nursery 
ˈnərs(ə)rē

▶noun (plural nurseries) a room in a house for the special use of young children. 
• (also day nursery) a place where young children are cared for during the working day; a nursery school. 
• a place where young plants and trees are grown for sale or for planting elsewhere. 
• a place or natural habitat that breeds or supports animals: this estuary provides a vast nursery for fish. 
• an institution or environment in which certain types of people or qualities are fostered or bred: that nursery of traitors. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French nourice ‘nurse’ (see nurse1) + -ery .

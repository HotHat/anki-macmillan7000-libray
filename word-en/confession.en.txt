confession 
kənˈfeSHən

▶noun
1 a formal statement admitting that one is guilty of a crime: he signed a confession to the murders. 
• an admission or acknowledgment that one has done something that one is ashamed or embarrassed about: by his own confession, he had strayed perilously close to alcoholism. 
• a formal admission of one's sins with repentance and desire of absolution, especially privately to a priest as a religious duty: she still had not been to confession. See also sacrament of reconciliation. 
• (confessions) often humorous intimate revelations about a person's private life or occupation, especially as presented in a sensationalized form in a book, newspaper, or movie: confessions of a driving instructor.
2 (also confession of faith) a statement setting out essential religious doctrine. 
• (also Confession) the religious body or church sharing a confession of faith. 
• a statement of one's principles: his words are a political confession of faith. DERIVATIVES confessionary 
kənˈfeSHəˌnerē 
adjectiveORIGIN late Middle English: via Old French from Latin confessio(n-), from confiteri ‘acknowledge’ (see confess) .

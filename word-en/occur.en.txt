occur 
əˈkər

▶verb (occurs, occurring, occurred) [no object] happen; take place: the accident occurred at about 3:30 p.m. 
• exist or be found to be present in a place or under a particular set of conditions: radon occurs naturally in rocks such as granite. 
• (occur to) (of a thought or idea) come into the mind of (someone): [with clause] : it occurred to him that he hadn't eaten. ORIGIN late 15th century: from Latin occurrere ‘go to meet, present itself,’ from ob- ‘against’ + currere ‘to run.’

survive 
sərˈvīv

▶verb [no object] continue to live or exist, especially in spite of danger or hardship: against all odds the child survived. 
• [with object] continue to live or exist in spite of (an accident or ordeal): he has survived several assassination attempts. 
• [with object] remain alive after the death of (a particular person): he was survived by his wife and six children 
(as adjective surviving) : there were no surviving relatives. 
• [no object] manage to keep going in difficult circumstances: she had to work day and night and survive on two hours sleep. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French sourvivre, from Latin supervivere, from super- ‘in addition’ + vivere ‘live.’

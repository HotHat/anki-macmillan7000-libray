kindly 
ˈkīn(d)lē

▶adverb in a kind manner: “Never mind,” she said kindly. 
• please (used in a polite request or demand, often ironically): will you kindly sign the enclosed copy of this letter. 
▶adjective (kindlier, kindliest)
1 kind; warmhearted; gentle: he was a quiet, kindly man.
2 archaic native-born. 
PHRASES
look kindly on regard (someone or something) sympathetically. not take kindly to not welcome or be pleased by (someone or something). take something kindly like or be pleased by something. thank someone kindly thank someone very much.ORIGIN Old English gecyndelīce ‘naturally, characteristically’ (see kind2, -ly2).

perform 
pərˈfôrm

▶verb [with object]
1 carry out, accomplish, or fulfill (an action, task, or function): I have my duties to perform. 
• [no object] work, function, or do something to a specified standard: the car performs well at low speeds. 
• [no object] informal have successful or satisfactory sexual intercourse with someone.
2 present (a form of entertainment) to an audience: the cast of
14 perform the play superbly. 
• [no object] entertain an audience, typically by acting, singing, or dancing on stage: the band will be performing live in Hyde Park. DERIVATIVES performability 
-ˌfôrməˈbilitē 
noun. performable 
pərˈfôrməb(ə)l 
adjectiveORIGIN Middle English: from Anglo-Norman French parfourmer, alteration (by association with forme ‘form’) of Old French parfournir, from par ‘through, to completion’ + fournir ‘furnish, provide.’

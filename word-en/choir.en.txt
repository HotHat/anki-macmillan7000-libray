choir 
ˈkwī(ə)r

▶noun an organized group of singers, typically one that takes part in church services or performs regularly in public: the Baptist choir. 
• one of two or more subdivisions of a choral group performing together: his famous Spem in alium for eight five-part choirs. 
• the part of a cathedral or large church between the altar and the nave, used by the choir and clergy. 
• a group of instruments of one family playing together: a clarinet choir. ORIGIN Middle English quer, quere, from Old French quer, from Latin chorus (see chorus). The spelling change in the 17th century was due to association with Latin chorus and modern French choeur .

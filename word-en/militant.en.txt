militant 
ˈmiləd(ə)nt

▶adjective combative and aggressive in support of a political or social cause, and typically favoring extreme, violent, or confrontational methods: a militant nationalist. 
▶noun a militant person. DERIVATIVES militance 
-təns 
noun. militantly 
ˈmiləd(ə)ntlē ˈmilətntlē 
adverbORIGIN late Middle English (in the sense ‘engaged in warfare’): from Old French, or from Latin militant- ‘serving as a soldier,’ from the verb militare (see militate). The current sense dates from the early 20th century.

permanent 
ˈpərmənənt

▶adjective lasting or intended to last or remain unchanged indefinitely: a permanent ban on the dumping of radioactive waste at sea 
damage was not thought to be permanent 
some temporary workers did not want a permanent job. 
• lasting or continuing without interruption: he's in a permanent state of rage. 
▶noun North American a perm for the hair. DERIVATIVES permanentize 
-ˌtīz 
verb ( rare)ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin permanent- ‘remaining to the end’ (perhaps via Old French), from per- ‘through’ + manere ‘remain.’

excellent 
ˈeks(ə)lənt

▶adjective extremely good; outstanding: a 3-bedroom house in excellent condition 
their results are excellent. 
▶exclam. used to indicate approval or pleasure: “What a lovely idea! Excellent!” ORIGIN late Middle English (in the general sense ‘excelling, outstanding,’ referring to either a good or bad quality): from Old French, from Latin excellent ‘being preeminent,’ from excellere (see excel). The current appreciatory sense dates from the early 17th century.

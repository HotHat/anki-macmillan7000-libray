sting 
stiNG

▶noun
1 a small sharp-pointed organ at the end of the abdomen of bees, wasps, ants, and scorpions, capable of inflicting a painful or dangerous wound by injecting poison. 
• any of a number of minute hairs or other organs of plants, jellyfishes, etc., that inject a poisonous or irritating fluid when touched. 
• a wound from the sting of an insect or plant: a wasp or bee sting. 
• a sharp tingling or burning pain or sensation: I felt the sting of the cold, bitter air. 
• [in singular] a hurtful quality or effect: she smiled to take the sting out of her words.
2 informal a carefully planned operation, typically one involving deception: five blackmailers were jailed last week after they were snared in a police sting. 
▶verb (past and past participle stung 
stəNG
)
1 [with object] wound or pierce with a sting: he was stung by a jellyfish 
[no object] : a nettle stings if you brush it lightly.
2 feel or cause to feel a sharp tingling or burning pain or sensation: [no object] : her eyes stung 
[with object] : the brandy stung his throat 
(as adjective stinging) : a stinging pain. 
• [with object] (typically of something said) hurt or upset (someone): stung by her mockery, Frank hung his head. 
• (sting someone into) provoke someone to do (something) by causing annoyance or offense: he was stung into action by an article in the paper.
3 [with object] informal swindle or exorbitantly overcharge (someone): an elaborate fraud that stung a bank for thousands.

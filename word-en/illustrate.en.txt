illustrate 
ˈiləˌstrāt

▶verb [with object] provide (a book, newspaper, etc.) with pictures: the guide is illustrated with full-color photographs. 
• explain or make (something) clear by using examples, charts, pictures, etc.: the results are illustrated in Figure 7. 
• serve as an example of: a collection of pieces that illustrate Bach's techniques. ORIGIN early 16th century (in the sense ‘illuminate, shed light on’): from Latin illustrat- ‘lit up,’ from the verb illustrare, from in- ‘upon’ + lustrare ‘illuminate.’

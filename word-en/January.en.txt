January 
ˈjanyəˌwerē

▶noun (plural Januaries) the first month of the year, in the northern hemisphere usually considered the second month of winter: Sophie was two in January 
[as modifier] : the January sales. ORIGIN Old English, from Latin Januarius (mensis) ‘(month) of Janus’ (see Janus), the Roman god who presided over doors and beginnings.

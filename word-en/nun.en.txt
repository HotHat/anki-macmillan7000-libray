nun 
nən

▶noun a member of a religious community of women, especially a cloistered one, living under vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience. 
• any of a number of birds whose plumage resembles a nun's habit, especially an Asian mannikin. 
• a pigeon of a breed with a crest on its neck. DERIVATIVES nunlike 
-ˌlīk 
adjective. nunnish adjectiveORIGIN Old English nonne, from ecclesiastical Latin nonna, feminine of nonnus ‘monk,’ reinforced by Old French nonne .

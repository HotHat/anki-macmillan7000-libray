ashamed 
əˈSHāmd

▶adjective [predicative] embarrassed or guilty because of one's actions, characteristics, or associations: you should be ashamed of yourself 
his clothes and manners made me ashamed of him 
[with clause] : she felt ashamed that she had hit him. 
• (ashamed to do something) reluctant to do something through fear of embarrassment or humiliation: I'm ashamed to say I followed him home 
I am not ashamed to be seen with them. DERIVATIVES ashamedly 
əˈSHāmədlē 
adverbORIGIN Old English āscamod, past participle of āscamian ‘feel shame,’ from ā- (as an intensifier) + the verb shame .

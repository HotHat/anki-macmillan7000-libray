caravan 
ˈkerəˌvan

▶noun
1 British a vehicle equipped for living in, typically towed by a car and used for vacations: [as modifier] : a caravan holiday. 
• a covered horse-drawn wagon: a gypsy caravan. 
• North American a covered truck; a van.
2 historical a group of people, especially traders or pilgrims, traveling together across a desert in Asia or North Africa. 
• any large group of people, typically with vehicles or animals traveling together, in single file: a caravan of cars and trucks. ORIGIN late 15th century ( sense 2): from French caravane, from Persian kārwān. The sense ‘covered horse-drawn wagon’ dates from the early 19th century.

junction 
ˈjəNG(k)SH(ə)n

▶noun
1 a point where two or more things are joined: the junction of the two rivers. 
• a place where two or more roads or railroad lines meet.
2 Electronics a region of transition in a semiconductor between a part where conduction is mainly by electrons and a part where it is mainly by holes.
3 the action or fact of joining or being joined. DERIVATIVES junctional 
-SHənl 
adjectiveORIGIN early 18th century ( sense 3): from Latin junctio(n-), from jungere ‘to join.’

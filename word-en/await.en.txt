await 
əˈwāt

▶verb [with object] (of a person) wait for (an event): we await the proposals with impatience 
prisoners awaiting trial 
[as adjective, with submodifier] (awaited) : an eagerly awaited debut. 
• (of an event or circumstance) be in store for (someone): many dangers await them. ORIGIN Middle English: from Anglo-Norman French awaitier, from a- (from Latin ad ‘to, at’) + waitier ‘to wait.’

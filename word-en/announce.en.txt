announce 
əˈnouns

▶verb [reporting verb] make a public and typically formal declaration about a fact, occurrence, or intention: [with clause] : the president's office announced that the state of siege would be lifted 
[with object] : he announced his retirement from football 
[with direct speech] : “I have a confession to make,” she announced. 
• [with object] give information about (transportation) in a station or airport via a public address system: they were announcing her train. 
• [with object] make known the arrival or imminence of (a guest or a meal) at a formal social occasion: dinner was announced. ORIGIN late 15th century: from French annoncer, from Latin annuntiare, from ad- ‘to’ + nuntiare ‘declare, announce’ (from nuntius ‘messenger’).

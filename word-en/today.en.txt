today 
təˈdā

▶adverb on or in the course of this present day: she's thirty today 
he will appear in court today. 
• at the present period of time; nowadays: millions of people today cannot afford adequate housing. 
▶noun this present day: today is a day of rest 
today's game against the Blue Jays. 
• the present period of time: the powerful computers of today 
today's society. ORIGIN Old English tō dæg ‘on (this) day’ Compare with tomorrow and tonight .

college 
ˈkälij

▶noun
1 an educational institution or establishment, in particular: 
• one providing higher education or specialized professional or vocational training: my brother wanted to go to college 
I'm at college studying graphic design. 
• US (within a university) a school offering a general liberal arts curriculum leading only to a bachelor's degree. 
• (in Britain) any of a number of independent institutions within certain universities, each having its own teaching staff, students, and buildings. 
• British a private secondary school: [in names] : Eton College. 
• the teaching staff and students of a college considered collectively: the college was shocked by his death.
2 an organized group of professional people with particular aims, duties, and privileges: [in names] : the electoral college. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French, from Latin collegium ‘partnership, association,’ from collega ‘partner in office,’ from col- ‘together with’ + legare ‘depute.’

collide 
kəˈlīd

▶verb [no object] hit with force when moving: she collided with someone 
two suburban trains collided. 
• come into conflict or opposition: in his work, politics and metaphysics collide. ORIGIN early 17th century (in the sense ‘cause to collide’): from Latin collidere, from col- ‘together’ + laedere ‘to strike or damage.’

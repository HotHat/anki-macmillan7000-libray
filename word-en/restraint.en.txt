restraint 
rəˈstrānt

▶noun
1 (often restraints) a measure or condition that keeps someone or something under control or within limits: decisions are made within the financial restraints of the budget. 
• the action of keeping someone or something under control. 
• deprivation or restriction of personal liberty or freedom of movement: he remained aggressive and required physical restraint. 
• a device that limits or prevents freedom of movement: car safety restraints.
2 unemotional, dispassionate, or moderate behavior; self-control: he urged the protesters to exercise restraint. 
• understatement, especially of artistic expression: with strings and piano, all restraint vanished. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French restreinte, feminine past participle of restreindre ‘hold back’ (see restrain) .

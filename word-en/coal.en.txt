coal 
kōl

▶noun a combustible black or dark brown rock consisting mainly of carbonized plant matter, found mainly in underground deposits and widely used as fuel: [as modifier] : a coal fire. 
• a red-hot piece of coal or other material in a fire: the glowing coals. 
▶verb [with object] provide with a supply of coal: (as noun coaling) : the coaling and watering of the engine. 
PHRASES
coals to Newcastle something brought or sent to a place where it is already plentiful. rake (or haul) someone over the coals reprimand someone severely.DERIVATIVES coaly 
ˈkōlē 
adjectiveORIGIN Old English col (in the senses ‘glowing ember’ and ‘charred remnant’), of Germanic origin; related to Dutch kool and German Kohle. The sense ‘combustible mineral used as fuel’ dates from Middle English.

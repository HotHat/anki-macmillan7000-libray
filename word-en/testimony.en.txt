testimony 
ˈtestəˌmōnē

▶noun (plural testimonies) a formal written or spoken statement, especially one given in a court of law. 
• evidence or proof provided by the existence or appearance of something: his blackened finger was testimony to the fact that he had played in pain. 
• a public recounting of a religious conversion or experience. 
• archaic a solemn protest or declaration. ORIGIN Middle English: from Latin testimonium, from testis ‘a witness.’

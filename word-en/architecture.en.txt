architecture 
ˈärkəˌtek(t)SHər

▶noun
1 the art or practice of designing and constructing buildings. 
• the style in which a building is designed or constructed, especially with regard to a specific period, place, or culture: Victorian architecture.
2 the complex or carefully designed structure of something: the chemical architecture of the human brain. 
• the conceptual structure and logical organization of a computer or computer-based system: a client/server architecture. ORIGIN mid 16th century: from Latin architectura, from architectus (see architect) .
